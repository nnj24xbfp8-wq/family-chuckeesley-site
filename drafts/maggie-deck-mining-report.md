**Authored by:** Margaret "Maggie" McNaughton Eesley (Chuck's aunt, b. November 14, 1951; Will & Peggy Eesley's daughter). Every slide signed `© Maggie M. Eesley. All rights reserved.`

**Catalogue style:** Museum-grade. Every photograph and letter carries Title / Type / Medium / Measurement / Condition / Creator / Date Created / Place Created / ID Number / Provenance / Description / Inscription / Location-Rights. This is exactly the artifact schema this site already has.

---

## Headline finding

The deck is overwhelmingly weighted to **Chuck's grandmother Peggy McMaster Eesley's side** — her parents, her grandparents, her brother. That side of the family is the **least covered on the site right now**. Mining the deck unlocks a significant new block of paternal-side content covering the McMaster and Anderson lines back to the 1830s, plus the Chenoweth-Timmons line behind Lillie Dale that the site flags as "documented in Bean's register but not yet seeded."

Ten missing person pages and approximately 20 missing artifact entries. Three families enter the archive properly: McMaster, Anderson, Timmons.

## Missing people (10)

Each entry below carries everything the deck tells us. Birth/death dates are from slide titles; biographical detail is from the slide bodies.

### Peggy McMaster's parents

1. **Alice Anderson McMaster** (1871–1961) — Peggy's mother, Chuck's great-grandmother
   - Daughter of Rev. Abraham Ramsey Anderson and Nancy Jane Shaw Anderson
   - Was a schoolteacher; ran the **Clifton School** in Pennsylvania (deck has a 1928 class photo with her students)
   - Multiple photographs in deck — young woman cabinet card (Eyer, McDonald PA, 1880s–1890s); on porch with son Don ~1910–12; with her Clifton School students 1928; in her schoolroom

2. **Robert Thompson McMaster** (1866–1919) — Peggy's father, Chuck's great-grandfather
   - One of twin sons of Margaret Thompson McMaster and Gilbert Clement McMaster
   - **Twin's name: Erasmus Darwin** (died young of a weakened heart). Robert had the same weakened heart and lived with it until age 55.
   - Two photographs in deck

### Peggy McMaster's grandparents (McMaster line)

3. **Margaret Thompson McMaster** (1835–1910) — Robert T.'s mother
   - 1866 cabinet card by **B. L. H. Dabbs, 46/48/50 St. Clair Street, Pittsburg, Pennsylvania** — sepia tone, holding twin sons Erasmus and Robert

4. **Gilbert Clement McMaster** (1833–1906) — Robert T.'s father
   - Mid-1850s to early-1860s cabinet card by **Richards, Pittsburg, Pennsylvania**
   - "Walrus mustache," serious expression

### Peggy McMaster's grandparents (Anderson line)

5. **Reverend Abraham Ramsey Anderson** (1832–1919) — Alice McMaster's father; Peggy's maternal grandfather
   - **Presbyterian minister for over 50 years**
   - Young-man cabinet card around 1860 — "pronounced sideburns, modeled after General Burnsides of the Civil War"
   - Author of a May 1894 letter to daughter Margaret Anderson (slide 29/30)

6. **Nancy Jane Shaw Anderson** (1834–1910) — Alice's mother
   - Two photographs: with husband Abraham 1860 (their marriage year) by **J. S. Young, Washington, Pennsylvania**; alone in her sixties (1888) by **Morris, Pittsburg, Pennsylvania**
   - The couple "anticipating a long marriage blessed with six children"
   - Maggie's note: "known for their sense of humor, kindness, and love of children"

### Peggy McMaster's brother

7. **Donald Anderson "Don" McMaster** (1903–1967) — Peggy's brother, Chuck's great-uncle
   - **Dedicated photographer** (the deck flags this twice — slides 12 and 13)
   - Multiple photographs: on porch with mother Alice ~1910–12; portrait; "student" frame; "photographer" frame

### Chenoweth–Timmons line (flagged in Lillie Dale's body as "not yet seeded")

8. **Mary Ohio Timmons Chenoweth** (1845–1919) — Lillie Dale's mother
   - Middle name **honors the state in which she was born (Ohio means "beautiful river")**
   - Source: scanned by John Walker from cousin Roberta Burnes Walker's archive (this Roberta = the Roberta Burnes on the site; "Walker" was her married name; her brother John Walker is the scanner)
   - One of her daughters was named after the **Sciota River** and "went on to become one of the first women medical doctors in the United States in the late 1800s" — a Chenoweth ancestor worth surfacing separately

9. **Joseph Hill Chenoweth** (1832–1910) — Lillie Dale's father
   - Formal portrait ~1865–70, scanned from Walker family CD

10. **John K. Timmons** (1806–1899) — Mary Ohio's father; Lillie Dale's grandfather
    - Formal portrait late 1800s — "looks quite advanced in age"
    - Maggie's editorial: "It's amazing that someone in the family has kept these old photos."

## Side findings worth surfacing

1. **`jean-goldie-eesley` vs `jeanie-goldie` duplication** — the site has both. One is Charles Leonard's mother (Albert Robert's wife, b. 1835); one is Lillie & Charles Leonard's daughter who died at 12 in 1924. These are different people — the daughter was named after the grandmother. Worth checking each file's frontmatter to make sure the relationships and dates are correctly distinguished.

2. **The Sciota Chenoweth medical pioneer** — Mary Ohio Timmons Chenoweth had a daughter named after the Sciota River who became "one of the first women medical doctors in the United States in the late 1800s." This is a Chenoweth aunt of Lillie Dale's, in a line that produced an early woman MD. She deserves a person page and a research pass to identify her by name and verify her medical career — a notable Eesley-side female ancestor whose story isn't on the site.

3. **Roberta Burnes is Roberta Burnes Walker** — the cousin the deck names as a scan-provider. Her brother **John Walker** scanned several of the early Chenoweth–Timmons photos and emailed them to Maggie. This is the same Roberta the family memory notes as holding the 1940s letter collection — worth recording her married name and her brother as a primary-source contributor.

4. **The deck's provenance chain is precise** — Maggie's `Provenance:` field on McMaster/Anderson items consistently reads "Peggy McMaster Eesley" (i.e. inherited mother-to-daughter). On Chenoweth–Timmons items it reads "Roberta Burnes Walker, cousin" (i.e. scanned by Roberta's brother John Walker). When seeding these artifacts, preserve those exact provenance chains.

5. **The twin tragedy** — Robert T. McMaster's twin brother Erasmus Darwin McMaster (b. 1866) died young of a weakened heart, the same condition Robert lived with until age 55. This is a piece of family medical history worth recording on Robert T.'s person page when it's created, and on a new Erasmus Darwin McMaster entry as the deck's only mention of him.

6. **Will Eesley's Christmas Card 1942** already exists as an artifact, but the deck has a **closeup** that isn't on the site. Worth adding as a second image to the existing artifact entry.

## Recommended sequencing

If wiring this in: **start at the leaves and work in.** Adding ancestors first means subsequent person pages have someone to reference as parents. Order:

1. Great-great-grandparents — John K. Timmons.
2. Great-grandparents — Joseph Hill + Mary Ohio Timmons Chenoweth; Gilbert Clement + Margaret Thompson McMaster; Abraham Ramsey + Nancy Jane Shaw Anderson.
3. Grandparents — Robert Thompson McMaster + Alice Anderson McMaster.
4. Great-uncle — Donald Anderson McMaster (Peggy's brother).
5. The Sciota Chenoweth medical pioneer (research-led — name unknown yet).
6. Then artifact entries for each, slotting in Maggie's catalogue metadata wholesale, with images extracted from the pptx.
7. Then Rev. Anderson's 1894 letter as a `documents/letter` entry (the second letter primary source after the Vietnam set).
