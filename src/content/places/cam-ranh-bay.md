**Cam Ranh Bay** is the deep natural harbor on the south-central coast of Vietnam, in present-day Khánh Hòa Province, about 180 miles northeast of Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon). During the Vietnam War it served as the **principal U.S. logistics base in the country** — the main port of entry for personnel and matériel, with a major airbase, a deep-water harbor, supply depots, and the medical infrastructure to support the entire American presence in Vietnam.

## The 6th Convalescent Center

Among Cam Ranh Bay's medical facilities was the **6th Convalescent Center**, opened in 1966 as a 1,500-bed beachfront recovery facility. Its purpose was specific and practical: to take stabilised wounded and sick soldiers off the high-acuity evacuation hospitals so those beds could be freed up for new admissions. A soldier hospitalized with malaria, hepatitis, a non-critical combat wound, or any of the chronic environmental conditions of the field could be transferred to the 6th Convalescent Center for the final weeks of recovery — bed rest, fluids, follow-up medication, and recreation.

The center sat on the beach side of the base. Recreational facilities included beach access, a movie program (a nightly screening in a base theater), and recreation rooms. Malaria patients were a common population — chloroquine-and-primaquine treatment for *Plasmodium vivax* typically required two to four weeks of bed rest and completion of the antimalarial course, exactly the kind of stay the center was designed for.

## Charlie's September 1970 stay

Charlie Eesley spent late September 1970 at the 6th Convalescent Center recovering from a *Plasmodium vivax* malaria episode he had contracted in the I Corps mountains with the 101st Airborne. His [21 September 1970 letter from the admitting hospital](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-09-21-malaria-hospital/) names the diagnosis as *"the curable or 'nice' receiving kind."* His [28 September 1970 letter from Cam Ranh Bay](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-09-28-cam-ranh-bay-convalescence/) names the location:

> *"I'm at Cam Ranh Bay at a convalescence center to fully recover from my bout with Malaria. I feel pretty good now but the longer I spend here the less time I'll have to spend in the field. There is a beach and a movie every night."*

The beach and the movie are the 6th Convalescent Center's signature amenities, matching the facility profile. Charlie was consciously stretching the recovery period to delay his return to the field — a soldier's strategy the convalescent-care model was, in practice, partly designed around.

## Cam Ranh Bay in the archive

Cam Ranh Bay is the **second of Charlie's two surviving Vietnam coastal-rear locations** in the surviving letter corpus, after the [Saigon](/places/saigon/) stand-down referenced in the [29 May 1970 letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-05-29/). Both were the safe-rear-coast counterpart to the I Corps mountain operations that defined the rest of his tour.

The base closed at the end of the U.S. presence in Vietnam. Today Cam Ranh Bay is a commercial port and air base used by the Vietnamese government, with a small civilian commercial airport adjacent. The 6th Convalescent Center site is gone.

## Pilgrimage potential

[Chuck Eesley](/family/charles-eric-eesley/)'s 2011 pilgrimage to [Saigon](/places/saigon/) — visiting the city his father had named explicitly in the corpus as a stand-down location — is the archive's existing pattern for a second-generation Vietnam trip. A similar pilgrimage to Cam Ranh Bay would be a natural extension: the geography of his father's recovery from malaria, the beach the 28 September letter names, the present-day port that grew up where the convalescent center used to be.
