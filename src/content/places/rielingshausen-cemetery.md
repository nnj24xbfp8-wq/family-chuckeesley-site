A steep hillside cemetery behind the village church, where the Wildermuths who did not emigrate are buried. The graves are hand-carved, the older ones eroding past legibility. Set into the churchyard wall is a First World War memorial; twenty-three of the names on it are Wildermuths.

This is the highest-value rephotography site of the trip: the same stones, the same wall, photographed twenty-five years apart, are themselves the document.
