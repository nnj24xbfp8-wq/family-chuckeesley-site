**651 Sixth Street, Marietta** is **the first of Will and Peggy Eesley's three Marietta houses** — the old two-story clapboard house Will bought around 1950 (when Charlie was three), spent **eight years remodeling around the family**, and lived in through his children's growing-up years. **Charlie Eesley grew up here**; his 1965 12th-grade autobiography names the address explicitly. The family moved later in the 1960s to **[Highland Ridge Road](/places/highland-ridge-road-marietta/)**, the next house Will designed and built fresh.

## What Charlie's autobiography says

From [*My Mother's Favorite Son*](/docs/charles-eesley-12th-grade-autobiography-1965/), c. 1964–65, Charlie age 17:

> *"When we moved to Marietta, we only moved into the first floor of our house. Upstairs lived a nice elderly couple who were very kind to us... All too soon, however, they moved. We then took over the whole house. Dad, being the architect that he is, immediately began to make plans to renovate the old house. Dad did the remodeling himself and for the next eight years there was always some part of the house being torn apart and rebuilt to suit his fancy. Margaret's bedroom was the old bathroom and Jeanne and Anne's bedroom was the old upstairs kitchen. The remodeling process has just finished this past year with the completion of the playroom. Now Dad is talking about moving."*

**Eight years of Will's remodeling.** **Maggie's bedroom in the former bathroom.** **Jeanne and Anne's bedroom in the former upstairs kitchen.** The day he finished the last room — the playroom, c. 1964 — Will started talking about moving. He did move, to Highland Ridge, where he designed and built the next house from the ground up.

## The household at 651 Sixth Street

Through the years Charlie's autobiography describes the family was Will, Peggy, and four children:

- **Jeanne** (b. early 1940s) — eldest
- **Anne** (b. 1946)
- **Charlie** (b. 17 February 1947)
- **Maggie** (b. 1951) — youngest

Plus, for the **last six years** (per Charlie's autobiography), **Alice Anderson McMaster** — Peggy's mother and Charlie's grandmother — lived with the family at 651 Sixth Street. She died there or in Marietta around 1961.

## The three Marietta houses

Across the Will-and-Peggy Eesley arc:

1. **651 Sixth Street** (this page) — c. 1950–late 1960s.
2. **[Highland Ridge Road](/places/highland-ridge-road-marietta/)** — late 1960s onward, the house Will designed and built. Charlie inherited it and raised Chuck there.
3. **[424 N 7th Street](/places/424-n-seventh-street-marietta/)** — Peggy's widow's house after Will's 1986 death. The "Grandma Peggy's house on 7th Street" of Chuck's childhood memory, named in the eulogy.

The three together span sixty years of family life, all within blocks of each other in Marietta. The **maternal-side equivalent** is **[413 Harmar Street](/places/413-harmar-street-marietta/)** on the Wildermuth side — Robert Earl's childhood dream house, also Marietta.
