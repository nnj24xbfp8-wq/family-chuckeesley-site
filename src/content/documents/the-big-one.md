This document contains two interleaved threads: the **mission log** (dates, targets, weather, anti-aircraft, losses) and a **crew roster** of the ten men on the B-24 with Robert Earl. The mission log covers November 1944 through May 1945, operating from forward airfields in the western Pacific — Biak, Leyte, and Mindoro. The crew portraits and short bios that follow the missions include navigator Robert Earl himself, the MIT-educated pilot Van Valen, the radio operator "Fuzzy" Kuszmaul, the Texan tail gunner and crew photographer Jesse Murdock, and the other men of the ten-man crew.

The summer of 1945 carries the document's most consequential entry: **Robert Earl was the navigator on the B-24 flying MGM Studios cameras over Hiroshima and Nagasaki to film the bombed cities from the air.** The footage the postwar world would form its understanding of nuclear weapons from needed a navigator to get there. He was it.

## Before they shipped — San Francisco, c. 1944

A single sepia-toned photograph from the combat log places Robert Earl and three of his fellow Army Air Corps officers **at a bar table in San Francisco shortly before deployment** &mdash; the staging stop on the way to the Pacific. The four men are in **Class B officer uniforms with pilot wings**; the table is loaded with **Alpen Brau beer bottles**; the lighting and grain mark it as an interior bar shot, c. 1944. The photograph reads almost as a *"last night before the war"* frame &mdash; the kind of officer's-club composition the crew would have wanted to take precisely because they all knew what they were about to fly into.

![Robert Earl Wildermuth (left) and three fellow officers in a San Francisco bar shortly before Pacific deployment, c. 1944. Class B uniforms, pilot wings, Alpen Brau bottles on the table.](../../assets/family/originals/00000001_00000007_00000008.jpeg)

The print is the only surviving photograph in this archive of Robert Earl *in uniform but not yet in combat* — the calibration moment between the Stanford undergraduate years that hadn't yet happened and the Pacific missions about to begin.

- [The 90th Bomb Group "Jolly Rogers"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/90th_Operations_Group) on Wikipedia — B-24s, the Pacific airfields, the unit lineage.
- [The United States Strategic Bombing Survey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Strategic_Bombing_Survey) — the official US program the post-bombing aerial photography fed into.
- [*The Bomb*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bomb_(book)) by Gerard J. DeGroot — the policy, technical, and military history of the weapons themselves.
