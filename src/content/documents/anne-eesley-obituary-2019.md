The published obituary of Anne Eesley — Will and Peggy Eesley's second daughter, Chuck Eesley's paternal aunt — who died at Country Care in Farwell, Michigan on Saturday, 27 July 2019, age 73. The obituary surfaces the substantial academic career and family-friend network the prior thinner obituary listings did not show.

## Full text

> *Anne Eesley passed away at Country Care in Farwell, Michigan on Saturday, July 27, 2019 at the age of 73. She is preceeded in death by her father, Wilbur Eesley; mother, Margaret Eesley; and brother, Charles Eesley. She is survived by her sister, Jeanne (Joseph) Kamiab and sister, Margaret Eesley. Anne is lovingly remembered by her dear friends Aaron and Dina Wetters, Lydia (Ian) Kirkpatrick, Micah Wetters, and Hannah Wetters whom she cherished like family.*
>
> *Anne was born in Columbus, Ohio on March 1, 1946. She graduated from Marietta High School in 1964 and went on to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree from Marietta College in 1968. She then earned a Master of Arts degree in Spanish from University of Wisconsin in 1976 and a Doctor of Philosphy degree from State University of New York in 1983. Over the years she worked as a professor at Hillsdale College, The Citadel, and Grand Valley State University. She also taught in various other places, including a migrant program and the Muskegon Correctional Facility. Anne had a passion for teaching and the way education could help people reach their full potential.*
>
> *Anne enjoyed cooking and was known for her hospitality, hosting many meals in her home and putting out her best dishes for each guest who sat at her table. Anne was skilled at embroidery and designed her own patterns to create personal gifts.*

## What the obituary reveals

**A scholar of Spanish.** The obituary names the full academic credential chain: Marietta High 1964, Marietta College BA 1968, University of Wisconsin MA in Spanish 1976, State University of New York PhD 1983. The MA-in-Spanish-at-Wisconsin date 1976 sits exactly after the [c. 1972–1975 Madrid years with Peggy](/archive/anne-and-peggy-eesley-europe-c-1973/) — making the Madrid period almost certainly a pre-graduate Spanish-immersion residence that fed the Wisconsin MA. The SUNY PhD (1983) is likely also in Spanish or Spanish literature, given the continuity of her teaching career.

**The Charleston and Muskegon geography explained.** Anne's prior obituary-source addresses — Charleston, South Carolina at Will's 1986 death and Muskegon, Michigan at Charlie's 2015 death — now line up with her academic career: she was teaching at **The Citadel** in Charleston in 1986 and at **Grand Valley State University** in the Muskegon area by 2015. The career-moves geography sets the family-obituary geography; what had read as restless moves was actually academic-job mobility.

**Service teaching alongside university teaching.** The obituary names two non-university teaching settings she worked in: a **migrant program** and **Muskegon Correctional Facility**. Both are populations the standard tenure-track career does not run through. Anne's commitment to teaching across audiences — Citadel cadets, Grand Valley State undergraduates, migrant-worker families, incarcerated men in a Michigan prison — is the obituary's central characterological note: *"Anne had a passion for teaching and the way education could help people reach their full potential."*

**The Wetters family and the Kirkpatricks.** The obituary names two family-friend households that Anne *"cherished like family"*: the Wetters (Aaron and Dina, with their children Micah and Hannah) and Lydia (Ian) Kirkpatrick. For an unmarried Eesley daughter ending her life in Farwell, Michigan with three siblings either gone (Charlie) or living far away (Jeanne in Cleveland, Maggie in Pennsylvania), the chosen-family Michigan friendships were the household at the end.

**Cooking, hospitality, embroidery.** The closing paragraph names three things Anne did well at home: she cooked, she hosted, and she embroidered her own original patterns. The cooking-and-hospitality line is the small-town version of the same warmth her great-aunt [Betty Wolfe's obituary](/docs/betty-jean-wolfe-obituary-2015/) frames on the other side of the family: *"She was an excellent cook and baker who loved to have people come for a meal. Everything was delicious."* Two unmarried-or-widowed women on opposite sides of Chuck's family running their late-life households on hospitality.
