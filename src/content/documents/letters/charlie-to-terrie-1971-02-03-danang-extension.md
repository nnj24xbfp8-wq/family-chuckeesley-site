## What the letter is

3 February 1971. Charlie writes Terrie a short note from Da Nang — one day before the [4 February Laos fore-warning letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/), and within the same Da Nang-rear-area stay that would eventually run all the way through the [1 March Lam Son 719 letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/). The letter is brief and almost relieved in its register: Charlie reads his current Da Nang assignment as a real piece of luck.

## Transcription

> *Feb 3*
>
> Dear Terrie,
>
> It seems that we will be staying here in Danang til March 5 and maybe even longer. It is all too good to be true. I plan to extend as soon as I can so as of now if all goes well I have 131 days left in the army with 28 of them right here. R&R will be postponed til April probably. I love you but this is too good an opportunity to get out of the field and I'll use my R&R after I get back to the field so I can keep out of the field some more.
>
> My address is still here at Danang.
>
> Love,
> Charles

## What the letter records

**The Da Nang stay through March 5.** *"Staying here in Danang til March 5 and maybe even longer. It is all too good to be true."* The Da Nang rear-area position &mdash; an air-conditioned office, no field rotation, library and PX nearby &mdash; was, by January-February 1971, the safest assignment a 101st Airborne soldier with under 200 days left could have hoped for. The line *"too good to be true"* is the small honest read: Charlie expected the assignment to be revoked at any moment, and was treating each additional day as a quiet windfall.

**131 days left, 28 in Da Nang.** The countdown thread again: by the [19 January letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-01-19-ets-extension/) he had been counting at the ETS-extension level; by 3 February the 14-June target had stabilized into 131 days left, of which 28 were already banked at Da Nang.

**R&R after the field, not before.** *"This is too good an opportunity to get out of the field and I'll use my R&R after I get back to the field so I can keep out of the field some more."* The R&R-timing logic is the small strategic note. In the standard Vietnam-tour pattern, R&R was a one-week stand-down used at the soldier's discretion; Charlie was reading the timing as a tool to extend his rear-area assignment by another week, banking the R&R for a later moment when staying in Da Nang would have stopped being possible. The strategic instinct is consistent with the [19 January 1971 ETS-extension calculus](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-01-19-ets-extension/) &mdash; Charlie was, throughout the spring of 1971, working every piece of the Army's procedure he could to keep himself off the firebase rotation.

**The address is still Danang.** The closing line is administrative &mdash; a note to Terrie that mail should keep going to the Da Nang address, not be re-routed to a field address &mdash; but it is also the practical confirmation of the same stay-of-execution he is reading the rest of the letter through.
