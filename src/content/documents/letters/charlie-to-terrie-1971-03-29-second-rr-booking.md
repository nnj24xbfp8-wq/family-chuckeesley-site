## What the letter is

29 March 1971. **Four days after [Operation Lam Son 719](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/) closed** (25 March 1971), Charlie writes a brief practical letter telling Terrie the Hawaii R&R window is locked in: arrival 12 April, departure 19 April. The [1 March letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/) had been doubting whether the R&R would happen at all. The [4 February letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/) had projected an April-or-so window. By 29 March, with Lam Son 719 over and the I Corps operational tempo dropping back, the trip could be booked.

Per Chuck (June 2026), this is the **one Hawaii meet-up** of the whole tour. Charlie may have had a solo 1970 R&R somewhere (Bangkok, Sydney, Tokyo, or in-country) but the one extended time with Terrie was this April 1971 trip. The spring "1970" Hawaii letters &mdash; the [March 15 catch-up letters](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-15/), the [22 March R&R orders](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-22/), the [31 March R&R planning](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-31-rr-planning/), the [April 2-3 parental conflict](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-04-02-parents-against-hawaii/), [April 5 Peggy-suggested marriage](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-04-05-mom-suggested-hawaii-marriage/), and [29 May 17-days-left](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-05-29/) &mdash; are all part of the long spring-1971 lead-up to and aftermath of this trip, not a separate 1970 plan as the archive had previously framed them.

## Transcription

> *March 29*
>
> Dear Terrie,
>
> Please get your ticket under (Mrs. Eesley) to save money. Get it for your arrival to be on the 12th of April and leave on the 19th. Late in the day. I guess that would be best.
>
> I got paid today and have sent you $100 as I said I would. Enclosed is my receipt for you to keep until you get the money.
>
> Everything is fine here with 77 days left.
>
> Love,
> Charles

## What the letter records

**The Mrs. Eesley fare convention.** *"Please get your ticket under (Mrs. Eesley) to save money."* The same paperwork convention from the [22 March R&R orders letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-22/) and the [31 March R&R planning letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-31-rr-planning/) &mdash; Terrie travels under "Mrs. Eesley" to access the reduced military-spouse airfare, even though they were not yet married. Peggy had presumably worked out the trick from her own military-spouse experience and passed it on. Charlie's parenthetical *"(Mrs. Eesley)"* in his own handwriting is the small piece of administrative shorthand he was passing forward to Terrie verbatim.

**The April 12-19 window.** The R&R was now formally booked into a one-week window starting Monday 12 April 1971 and closing Monday 19 April 1971. *"Late in the day. I guess that would be best."* Charlie's preference for a late-day arrival was the practical one &mdash; soldiers on R&R coordinated their leave with the receiving partner's travel, and a late-day arrival gave Terrie the daytime hours to land and check into the hotel before he arrived. The destination is Hawaii, the standard 101st Airborne R&R venue throughout the war and the place the parental-conflict letters had been arguing about for weeks.

**$100 sent, receipt enclosed.** *"I got paid today and have sent you $100 as I said I would. Enclosed is my receipt for you to keep until you get the money."* The standard pattern of soldier money orders home runs through the corpus &mdash; from the [15 March financial-accounting letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-15/) ("$100 Mar 1 or so") to the [29 May 17-days-left letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-05-29/) ($260 check Terrie had trouble cashing) to here. The Vietnam tour was, among other things, an extended exercise in accumulating savings that would let them start their post-tour life with capital in hand.

**77 days left.** *"Everything is fine here with 77 days left."* 77 days from 29 March 1971 puts the ETS at **Tuesday 14 June 1971** &mdash; exactly the [4 February 1971 target](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/) that he had refined from the 19 January ETS-extension strategy. The 14 June ETS held all the way through.
