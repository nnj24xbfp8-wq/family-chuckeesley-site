## What the letter is

29 March 1971. **Four days after [Operation Lam Son 719](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/) closed** (25 March 1971), Charlie writes a brief practical letter telling Terrie that the second R&R window is set: arrival 12 April, departure 19 April. The [1 March letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/) had been doubting whether the R&R would happen at all. The [4 February letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/) had projected an April-or-so window. By 29 March, with Lam Son 719 over and the I Corps operational tempo dropping back, the R&R could be booked.

## Transcription

> *March 29*
>
> Dear Terrie,
>
> Please get your ticket under (Mrs. Eesley) to save money. Get it for your arrival to be on the 12th of April and leave on the 19th. Late in the day. I guess that would be best.
>
> I got paid today and have sent you $100 as I said I would. Enclosed is my receipt for you to keep until you get the money.
>
> Everything is fine here with 77 days left.
>
> Love,
> Charles

## What the letter records

**The Mrs. Eesley fare convention, carried forward.** *"Please get your ticket under (Mrs. Eesley) to save money."* The same paperwork convention from the [22 March 1970 R&R orders letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-03-22/) and the [31 March 1970 R&R planning letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-03-31-rr-planning/) &mdash; Terrie travels under "Mrs. Eesley" to access the reduced military-spouse airfare, even though they were not yet married. The convention had survived the 1970 R&R, the 1970 wedding planning that did not happen, the late-1970 R&R cancellation, and was being recycled for the second R&R. Charlie's parenthetical *"(Mrs. Eesley)"* in his own handwriting is the small piece of administrative shorthand &mdash; a tip Peggy had presumably given them in 1970 and which Charlie was now re-issuing to Terrie verbatim.

**The April 12-19 window.** The R&R was now formally booked into a one-week window starting Monday 12 April 1971 and closing Monday 19 April 1971. *"Late in the day. I guess that would be best."* Charlie's preference for a late-day arrival was the practical one &mdash; soldiers on R&R coordinated their leave with the receiving partner's travel, and a late-day arrival gave Terrie the daytime hours to land and check into the hotel before he arrived. The destination is not specified in this letter; the [4 February 1971 letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/) had projected Hawaii, and the [1 March 1971 letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-03-01-lam-son-719/) had mentioned Cape Hatteras as a Hawaii backup if R&R fell through &mdash; but with R&R now confirmed, the standard 101st Airborne R&R destination of Hawaii is the likely target.

**$100 sent, receipt enclosed.** *"I got paid today and have sent you $100 as I said I would. Enclosed is my receipt for you to keep until you get the money."* The standard pattern of soldier money orders home runs through the corpus &mdash; from the [15 March 1970 financial accounting letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-03-15/) ("$100 Mar 1 or so") to the [29 May 1970 17-days-left letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-05-29/) ($260 check Terrie had trouble cashing) to here. The Vietnam tour was, among other things, an extended exercise in accumulating savings that would let them start their post-tour life with capital in hand.

**77 days left.** *"Everything is fine here with 77 days left."* 77 days from 29 March 1971 puts the ETS at **Tuesday 14 June 1971** &mdash; exactly the [4 February 1971 target](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-04-laos-fore-warning/) that he had refined from the 19 January ETS-extension strategy. The 14 June ETS held all the way through.
