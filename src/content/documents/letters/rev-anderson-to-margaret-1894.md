## What the letter is

On **Monday, 21 May 1894**, [Reverend Abraham Ramsey Anderson](/family/abraham-ramsey-anderson/) &mdash; the Presbyterian minister Chuck's great-great-grandfather, then sixty-two years old &mdash; sat down on a rainy day at home in Pennsylvania and wrote his daughter Margaret a letter. Margaret was away from home; the letter is full of "we miss you" news of the parish, the household, and the neighbors. The most likely reason for her absence: she was preparing for her own college commencement &mdash; her father mentions ordering himself a new coat and vest at **John Wanamaker's clothing establishment** to wear to it.

Margaret was a sister of **[Alice Anderson](/family/alice-anderson-mcmaster/)** &mdash; Chuck's great-grandmother &mdash; and therefore Chuck's **great-great-aunt**. The first name "Margaret" passed down through Alice's branch of the family to Alice's granddaughter [Margaret 'Peggy' McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) (Chuck's grandmother) and onward to Peggy's daughter [Margaret 'Maggie' Eesley](/family/margaret-maggie-eesley/) (Chuck's aunt, the archivist whose deck catalogued this very letter).

The letter is the **second 19th-century letter primary source** in this archive after Charlie Eesley's [Vietnam letters](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) of 1969&ndash;71. Both are fathers writing to family they couldn't see, separated by seventy-five years and a different war's worth of historical distance.

## Partial transcription

Reading Rev. Anderson's hand is doable but not trivial &mdash; Maggie's catalogue note characterised the script as *"elegantly written"* and *"shows the slow pace of the era when writing was the main form of communication between distant family members."* The pages are stiff lined paper, 7¾" by 10". A working partial transcription from the closeup and page A scans, with **[brackets]** marking the uncertain or illegible passages:

> **Monday, 21 May 1894**
>
> Dear Margaret —
>
> It rained today, and so I cannot do much out of doors. [I'll] write you a few lines. **Emma Moore** was at Church yesterday, but took sick during services and went home, so we did not have an opportunity of getting the Sabbath [Lesson?] from Ladies Hall. They did [the Coon home? &mdash; uncertain]. I'll see her brother, or [Dr.?] **Scott**. **Joseph C. Scott** has been very sick for two weeks with [a] carbuncle on his neck &mdash; or more probably a scrofulous tumor. **Dr. McElroy** is fearful of blood-poisoning. The case is a serious one. Have not heard from him today; he was reported [no worse] yesterday. [Margaret? Mrs?] to see him this PM.
>
> **Charles Wallace's** have another baby &mdash; a daughter.
>
> **Mary** has finished washing and scrubbing, and has seated herself on [the] side of [the] table to read this letter when finished. **Marie** is "exercised" about attending **W. M. Society at Thomas Station** on Thursday of this week. If it were not that she is anxious to show her new dress I think she would stay at home.
>
> [...] I visited the [Pittsburg?] [branch?] of **John Wanamaker's Clothing Establishment** last week, and ordered me a coat and vest. Shall wear [...] to Commencement.
>
> [Page break / further passages on page B not yet transcribed]
>
> Your Papa &mdash;

What the letter is *about*, in the genre's terms: weather, illness in the parish (Joseph C. Scott's carbuncle and his doctor's fear of blood-poisoning, Emma Moore taking sick at church), a new baby in the **Charles Wallace** household, the sisters at home (Mary scrubbing the floor on a Monday and **Marie** debating whether to attend the **Women's Missionary Society** meeting at Thomas Station on Thursday "just to show her new dress"), and a coat-and-vest order at **John Wanamaker's** for Margaret's own Commencement. It is exactly the late-Victorian family letter form &mdash; the news of the small lives all around the writer, the small mercies and the small worries.

## Note on the recipient

The letter is addressed only to "Margaret." This is **Margaret Anderson**, one of Rev. Abraham and Nancy Jane Shaw Anderson's six children, and the older or younger sister of Chuck's great-grandmother [Alice](/family/alice-anderson-mcmaster/). Margaret Anderson is the namesake of the **Margaret** that runs as a given name through the family on Alice's side: Alice's granddaughter [Margaret 'Peggy' McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) (1914&ndash;2007), Peggy's daughter [Margaret 'Maggie' Eesley](/family/margaret-maggie-eesley/) (b. 1951, the archivist of this letter), and the long generational line of Margarets the Anderson women began.

Margaret Anderson does not yet have a person entry in this archive. Adding her, with whatever death dates and married name are findable in the GEDCOM, is a natural follow-up to placing this letter.
