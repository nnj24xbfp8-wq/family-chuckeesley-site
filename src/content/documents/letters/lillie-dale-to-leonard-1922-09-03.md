## What this letter is

A short letter from **[Lillie Dale Chenoweth Eesley](/family/lillie-dale-chenoweth/)** &mdash; then 45 years old, living in the Bexley / Columbus area of central Ohio &mdash; to her eldest son **[Leonard David Eesley](/family/leonard-david-eesley/)** (Len), who was 17 years old and in the middle of a *solo cross-country car trip to California.* The letter is dated **3 September 1922** and is the **earliest item currently identified in the [Charles Leonard Eesley letter collection](/docs/charles-leonard-letter-collection/)** that Roberta Burnes keeps. Roberta scanned it and sent it to Chuck by email in June 2026; the scan itself will be linked from this page once uploaded.

## What it carries — Will's leg

The first substantive thread in the letter is the news from home: **eleven-year-old [Wilbur "Will" Eesley](/family/wilbur-eesley/)** (Lillie's third son, Chuck's grandfather) had broken his leg badly. Lillie writes about the *"scare"* the family went through. The letter doesn't name the cause; Roberta's June 2026 family memory does:

> *I am certain somewhere I heard he had fallen off a roof, suffering a very bad fracture.*

So the picture is: an eleven-year-old climbing on a roof in late summer 1922, a fall, a serious fracture, a mother writing to her oldest son on the road about how scared the family had been. Will recovered. The letter is the first surviving documentary evidence of the fall &mdash; for nearly a hundred years the fall existed only in family memory; this 3 September 1922 letter brings it into the documented record.

## What it carries — Chant

The second substantive thread is the request: while Len is out on the West Coast, Lillie wants him to *"be sure to visit Chant."*

**Chant** is **[Chauncey Chenoweth](/family/chauncey-chenoweth/)** &mdash; the son of Lillie's uncle [Thomas Chenoweth](/family/thomas-chenoweth-1828/) (1828&ndash;1908), Lillie's first cousin on the Chenoweth side. By 1922 Chant was settled in California, and the family-network connection ran across the country regularly enough that Lillie expected her teenage son on a solo car trip to track Chant down and visit him. The letter is the documentary trace of that connection &mdash; the earliest one this archive holds &mdash; and it points forward to the second visit on the same Chenoweth-California thread:

Years later, after Charles Leonard's [mental-health episode and Harding Hospital stay](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/), Lillie took her youngest daughter [Helen](/family/helen-burnes/) (Roberta's mother) on a train trip west, and Helen met Chant in person on that trip. So Chant is a recurring family-network anchor across at least two decades and two generations of Lillie Dale's children visiting him on the West Coast.

## Why this letter matters in the collection

Two reasons it earns its own page despite being a short letter:

1. **It is the earliest item identified in the Charles Leonard letter collection.** The collection has long been described as *1940s,* because that is the bulk of the surviving correspondence Roberta has. The 1922 letter pushes the collection back nearly two decades. Whether this is the only pre-1940 item, or whether other pre-1940 letters are also in the box and just haven't been pulled to the top yet, is now an open question for the rest of the collection-deciphering pass.

2. **It establishes the Lillie-Dale-to-her-children writing voice that the rest of the collection sits beside.** The 1940s letters are almost entirely in Charles Leonard's hand (and Roberta has described his handwriting as *"almost indecipherable"*). The 1922 letter is in Lillie Dale's hand and is the family's first surviving sample of what Lillie's letter-writing register sounds like. Two threads in one short letter &mdash; a worried mother's update on a son's recovery, and a casual family-network instruction to visit a cousin on the West Coast &mdash; together give an image of how Lillie wrote to her grown and growing children. Future deciphering passes on Charles Leonard's correspondence can sit next to this baseline.
