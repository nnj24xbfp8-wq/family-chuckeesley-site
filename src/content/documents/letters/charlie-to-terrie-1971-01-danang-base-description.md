## What the letter is

A multi-page letter from Charlie to Terrie, written from Da Nang in roughly January 1971 &mdash; after his transfer out of the field but before the [10 February 1971 short steady letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1971-02-10-danang/). Only page 2 has been identified in the family-archive scan sequence (`dad151.jpg`); page 1 sits somewhere else in the dad-numbered set and would carry the date header.

The page-2 content is Charlie's first sustained civilian-perspective description of the Da Nang base &mdash; the operational dispatches of the 1970 field letters have given way to something closer to a guide-book entry:

- The base holds 500&ndash;800 people and is a headquarters.
- The geography: a peninsula between the river on one side and the ocean on the other, on a narrow strip of land.
- The surrounding Vietnamese villagers are *"pro-govt and mostly Catholic."*
- The main city of Da Nang is across the river, where the Marine and Air Force bases are.
- There is a separate GI section at the beach, a GI bar, a larger PX.
- His plan for the next day: *"I'll go to the beach tomorrow."*

The substantive moment in the letter is an *ARVN incident* &mdash; the kind of small detail that would never have made the field letters but lands in this rear-area letter as the day's news:

> *A ARVN soldier had an argument with another, and one fired a shot in the air, and the other tossed a grenade which exploded 70 meters away from the tower. It is crazy and someone could get killed but no one was injured by it. We hear firing across the river in Da Nang all the time because the ARVN are firing in the air.*

The contrast with the 1970 field letters is the *point* of the letter as a documentary object. Charlie has been pulled out of the operational tempo and is now describing the base he is on with the eye of a tourist &mdash; the same eye, recognizably, that would later read the financial pages in his Marietta retirement.

## Why it is held metadata-only

Per the [collection's curation policy](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/), letters to Terrie default to metadata-only with the body held privately. The substantive war-record paragraphs (the base description and the ARVN grenade incident) are summarized in this entry; the full letter remains in the family archive.
