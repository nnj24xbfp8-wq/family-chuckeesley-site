## What the letter is

31 March 1970. Nine days after Charlie's [22 March 1970 Saigon arrival](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-03-22/), responding to a 24 March letter from Terrie saying she has her ticket for Hawaii R&R already in hand. The earliest R&R-planning letter in the corpus — by the time of the [29 May 'seventeen days left' letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-05-29/) two months later, the trip will be close to imminent, but here it is still ten weeks out and the logistics are being settled.

## Transcription

> *March 31*
>
> Dear Terrie,
>
> I got your letter of March 24 saying you were coming. Also you said you have your ticket. I hope you can get some benefit from the orders with Mrs. Eesley and get a reduced fare somehow.
>
> I know Mrs. Young but that was all very long ago and I don't remember her. Also her daughter (good friend of my parents) has just recently died, so I doubt if we will be with them at all. We will be staying away from everything military which includes the R&R center as much as possible. We will meet there, however.

## What the letter records

The R&R-planning timeline. The 24 March 1970 letter from Terrie that Charlie is responding to — already with her ticket purchased — establishes that the Hawaii R&R was settled, on the calendar, and being logistically organized by late March 1970, eleven weeks ahead of the actual trip in mid-June. This is two letters into Charlie's Vietnam tour. The [2–3 April 1970 letter about the Wildermuth-side parental fight](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-04-02-parents-against-hawaii/) — Terrie's parents objecting to her flying to Hawaii to meet her unmarried boyfriend for a week in a hotel — would land three days later. The R&R was already a settled fact in Charlie and Terrie's correspondence by the time the Wildermuth-side objection arrived.

Peggy's military-spouse orders. *"I hope you can get some benefit from the orders with Mrs. Eesley and get a reduced fare somehow."* The "Mrs. Eesley" here is Charlie's mother [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) — and "the orders" appears to refer to military-spouse travel paperwork that could get a deployed soldier's family a discounted fare. Charlie's family on the [Eesley side](/family/wilbur-eesley/) was helping Terrie organize the logistics in parallel with their own concerns about the trip. The detail is the earliest visible instance in the corpus of the Eesley-Wildermuth-Terrie-the-girlfriend operational coordination that would run through the rest of the tour.

Mrs. Young. A cameo: Mrs. Young's daughter, a *"good friend of my parents,"* has recently died, and Charlie and Terrie will therefore not be staying with the Young family during R&R. The Youngs are not otherwise documented in this archive. The two-sentence aside is the only mention of them in the Vietnam corpus so far. The line *"I don't remember her"* about Mrs. Young herself, while still mentioning her daughter's death as recent and relevant, has the quiet sadness — and brief acknowledgment — of these kinds of letters' compressed registers.

*"Stay away from everything military."* Charlie's framing of the R&R — that they will avoid the official R&R center and "everything military" as much as possible, meeting at the R&R center only because they have to — is the small but real assertion of the privacy of the trip in advance. Hawaii was meant to be a week with Terrie, not a week with the Army.
