## What the letter is

31 October 1968. **The earliest dated letter Charlie wrote to Terrie that this archive carries** &mdash; opening the corpus a year and a half before the [March 1970 Vietnam tour shipping date](/family/charles-eesley/) and twenty-two months before he would be commanding an M-60 machine gun in the I Corps bush. Charlie is a senior at [Marietta College](/family/wilbur-eesley/) majoring in economics; Terrie is a sophomore at the University of Maryland, in the dorm at College Park. The letter is short, light, full of small college-life detail, and dated to Halloween of his senior year.

## Transcription

> *Oct 31, 1968*
>
> Dear Terry,
>
> By now you know I'm a poor correspondent. As I was leaving the airport I saw Nancy Garratson. She had missed her plane (the same one) and had to stand there 5 minutes with the ramp down. They wouldn't let her on. We just barely made it!
>
> I didn't care much for the Vanilla Fudge. They were too loud with no real melody that was familiar. This was the general consensus too.
>
> Jay Ray doesn't want to go home Thanksgiving and I wanted to go over with him. He wants to go over the weekend before. Do you think you could line him up with someone? Don't make anything definite!
>
> [&hellip; continuation through additional pages not yet sampled &hellip;]

## What the letter records

**Halloween 1968 &mdash; the earliest dated letter in the corpus.** The Vietnam letters parent doc had carried the [late-1969 Christmas leave letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1969-christmas-leave/) (Charlie at NCO training, 711 days left) as the earliest dated letter on the site. **This October 1968 letter pushes the corpus opening back by fourteen months** &mdash; into the period before Charlie was in the army at all, before his draft physical and the Vietnam orders, when he was a senior at Marietta College with an active social life and a normal college-correspondence relationship with a girlfriend at the University of Maryland.

**The Vanilla Fudge concert.** *"I didn't care much for the Vanilla Fudge. They were too loud with no real melody that was familiar. This was the general consensus too."* [Vanilla Fudge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanilla_Fudge) was the late-1960s American psychedelic-rock band whose cover of *You Keep Me Hangin' On* had peaked at #6 on the Billboard chart in summer 1968 and whose touring through the autumn was hitting Midwestern colleges. Charlie's read &mdash; *"too loud with no real melody that was familiar"* &mdash; is the assessment of a 21-year-old midwestern economics major whose musical taste ran toward Top-40 melody rather than the heavy-feedback psychedelic register the band was pushing. The detail is small but it locates Charlie in the cultural fall-1968 with precision &mdash; the same season as the King and Kennedy assassinations earlier in the year, the Chicago Democratic Convention violence, the build-up to the Nixon-Humphrey election.

**Jay Ray and the Thanksgiving plan.** *"Jay Ray doesn't want to go home Thanksgiving and I wanted to go over with him. He wants to go over the weekend before. Do you think you could line him up with someone? Don't make anything definite!"* Jay Ray is one of Charlie's Marietta College friends, whose home situation made Thanksgiving in Ohio less appealing than a road trip to the Maryland coast. Charlie wants to come with him &mdash; presumably to see Terrie &mdash; and is asking Terrie to find a girl at Maryland willing to spend the weekend before Thanksgiving with Jay Ray. *"Don't make anything definite!"* &mdash; the small careful note of a college-age boyfriend protecting his friend from social commitment without consent. The Thanksgiving 1968 weekend, on this evidence, was being arranged at the same registrar's-office-and-roommate level that runs through the rest of the courtship correspondence.

**The Nancy Garratson airport miss.** *"As I was leaving the airport I saw Nancy Garratson. She had missed her plane (the same one) and had to stand there 5 minutes with the ramp down. They wouldn't let her on. We just barely made it!"* Nancy Garratson was apparently a Marietta-area acquaintance who happened to be on the same flight, missed her boarding window by a few minutes, and got stranded watching the plane pull away from the gate. Charlie made the same flight by what he reports as a similar margin. The detail is one of those small piece-of-life observations the courtship letters preserve.

**"Dear Terry."** Charlie's spelling of her name in 1968 was *"Terry"* &mdash; the more common American spelling. The *"Terrie"* spelling that runs through the Vietnam letters was a later convention. Whether Terrie wrote her name *"Terrie"* and Charlie didn't yet know, or whether *"Terrie"* was her own later adjustment, is a small open question. The opening *"Dear Terry"* is the period spelling.

**The closing register.** The letter signs *"Love, Charles"* &mdash; the same adult-correspondence signature that runs through the Vietnam letters. Even at 21, in Halloween 1968, Charlie's signed register was the formal one. The childhood nickname *"Chuckie"* survives in other letters of this era to family but in his correspondence with Terrie the signature was always *"Charles."*

## Other pre-Vietnam letters surfaced in the same batch (queued)

The same grandmaE1.pdf batch surfaced several other pre-Vietnam letters which await full ingestion:

- **April 1969 Florida spring break letter** &mdash; Charlie *"burnt to a crisp"* on the beach with cops everywhere, plus the line about a Cleveland interview with the Federal Reserve and a Philadelphia interview with Sears (his senior-year job-application track before the draft).
- **May 26 1969 letter** &mdash; First day of Marietta College finals; he is home painting; finals start Saturday.
- **Summer 1968 letter** &mdash; *"I'm trying to go see my Uncle Len in Washington DC, actually Silver Spring, Maryland, before school."* The same Len from the [1922 cross-country car trip letter](/docs/letters/lillie-dale-to-leonard-1922-09-03/), now in his sixties at his Silver Spring home where he would die in 1976.
- **Undated birthday letter to Terrie** &mdash; *"I won't really love you until you finish school. I told you I would look you up in three years and then if you can support me in the style I'm accustomed then maybe we can get serious and talk business."* The early-courtship Charlie teasing Terrie about her finishing college first. Signed *"Charles."*
- **"Chuckie" letter to Terrie** &mdash; ending with the line *"I don't care if your sister names hampsters after us so long as you don't call one Chuckie."* Sandy Wildermuth, Terrie's sister, was naming pet hamsters after Charlie and Terrie. The only letter in the corpus where Charlie signs as *"Chuckie."*

These belong to the same pre-Vietnam phase the 31 October 1968 letter opens, and will be ingested individually as the rest of the grandmaE1.pdf is mined.
