## What the letter is

**Spring 1970.** Somewhere in his Vietnam routine, Charlie has watched the **documentary *Woodstock*** — released by Warner Bros. on **26 March 1970**, directed by Michael Wadleigh, three hours and four minutes, an Academy Award winner that year for Best Documentary Feature. The film captured the **Woodstock Music & Art Fair** held over three days in August 1969 in Bethel, New York, while Charlie was finishing his Marietta College degree and starting through Army training. By the time he saw the film, he was halfway across the world in the war his generation was protesting from the festival's mud.

He noted it on the back page of a letter, in clearer ballpoint than the surrounding paragraphs: ***"I saw the movie Woodstock today. Quite interesting!!"***

## Why this letter is private

Most of the body of the letter is faded to near-illegibility — heavy blue-ink cursive, bled through both sides of the page, holding only fragments to a careful read. The body is **withheld at the family's request** as private correspondence. The Woodstock line is surfaced here for the record because it locates Charlie precisely on the cultural map of his moment: a serving infantryman in the 101st Airborne watching, with the dry brevity that is his voice, a documentary about the music festival his generation produced.

## Where the screening could have happened

By 1970, U.S. forces in Vietnam had a substantial **rear-area entertainment infrastructure**: USO shows, Armed Forces Vietnam Network television, AFVN radio, USO film prints rotated through division film services. **MACV Special Services** ran the in-country film exchange. *Woodstock*, given its March 1970 release, would have reached overseas military film circuits by April or May. The screening Charlie attended was most likely at a division-level rear-area facility — Phu Bai, Camp Eagle, or one of the smaller rear bases — during a routine rotation out of the field. The film's three-hour runtime and split-screen footage of half a million civilians at a music festival in upstate New York would have been a profoundly weird thing to watch in Vietnam in 1970.

## What the metadata records

- **Where he was**: Vietnam, at a screening site with film projection capability — almost certainly division rear area.
- **Where he was writing to**: Terrie at the University of Maryland.
- **What's preserved**: One sentence, "I saw the movie Woodstock today. Quite interesting!!" — closing line, recorded.
- **What's withheld**: The remaining body of the letter, faded and private.
