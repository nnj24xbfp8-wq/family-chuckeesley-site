> Please don't cry for me, for I have had a **GREAT** life
> We traveled the country for Uncle Sam
> Til settling in Ohio for a life with Fam.
> I had a son and 3 siblings to love
> My books and students, so why move?
> Warmed by God's sun with flowers my best,
> But now it's time for me to rest
> I'll wait for the day when we'll be together again

---

Eight lines. Her whole life: the Air Force childhood that moved her family across the U.S. and to Japan (*"We traveled the country for Uncle Sam"*), her decision to settle in Ohio so her son would have a home rooted in one place (*"a life with Fam"*), her son, her three siblings (Sandy, Rob, and Debbie), the twenty-three years of teaching (*"My books and students"*), and the gardening that Chuck's memorial speech also named &mdash; *"warmed by God's sun with flowers my best."*

The compression is hers. So is the rhyme scheme. So is the casual confidence of the closing &mdash; *"I'll wait for the day when we'll be together again."*

This is the only piece of Terrie's own writing in the archive so far.
