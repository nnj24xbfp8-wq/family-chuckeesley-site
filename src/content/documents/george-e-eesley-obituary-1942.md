## Why this document matters

George E. Eesley's 1942 obituary is the **single surviving narrative source for his life** in this archive, and it is also the key biographical anchor for two larger structures:

1. **It documents [R. Albert Eesley](/family/albert-robert-eesley-jr/)** &mdash; the "A. R. Eesley Jr." of the William Eesley obituary &mdash; living at a specific address (1310 Hayes Avenue, Fremont, Ohio) and identifies him as the head of a Fremont OH branch of the Eesley family, with his other children fanning out to Toronto, Youngstown, and Fremont.
2. **It names the next generation** &mdash; including [Daniel Edburn "Dan" Eesley](/family/daniel-edburn-eesley/), the San Francisco engineer whose own 2018 obituary is the other long-form source this archive holds on the Albert Robert Sr. branch.

The clipping carries the dual banner of its date: ***Buy War Savings Bonds*** runs across both the top and the bottom of the column, the visual mark of six months into the U.S. war effort.

## Transcription

> **GEORGE E. EESLEY**
>
> **Rites Are Sunday**
>
> George E. Eesley, 39, a former Fremont resident, died Thursday while undergoing a major operation in a Kenmore, N. Y., hospital, according to word received by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. R. Albert Eesley, of 1310 Hayes avenue.
>
> Mr. and Mrs. Eesley and daughter, Bess, have gone to Kenmore to attend rites Sunday. The body will be cremated following the funeral. The deceased was a graduate of Fremont Ross high school and Ohio Wesleyan university. He was employed in an airplane factory near Buffalo prior to his illness.
>
> Surviving are the wife, formerly Miss Edna Scaise, of Cleveland; two sons, Richard and Dan; a daughter, Jane; two sisters, Miss Bess Eesley, Fremont, and Mrs. Jack Cassel, of Toronto, Canada; brother, Albert, of Youngstown, and the parents.

## What the clipping tells us about R. Albert Eesley's family

The 1942 obituary establishes:

- **Address**: 1310 Hayes Avenue, Fremont, Ohio (Sandusky County).
- **At least four children** of R. Albert and his wife (alive at the time): George (the deceased), Bess (with the parents in Fremont, unmarried at that date), Mrs. Jack Cassel (in Toronto &mdash; one of the Toronto Eesleys), and Albert (of Youngstown, Ohio).
- **The wife is not named in the clipping** &mdash; an editorial choice typical of the era when newspaper obituaries identified women through their husband's name.

## What the clipping tells us about George's family

- **Wife**: Edna Scaise of Cleveland. Her name is later given as **Edla "Eddie" Scaife** in her son Dan's 2018 obituary &mdash; the 1942 spelling appears to be a printing-room error. See [her stub page](/family/edla-scaife-eesley/).
- **Two sons**: **Richard** and **Dan**. Dan would go on to become a major Bay Area technology engineer and San Francisco civic figure &mdash; his life is documented in his own [2018 obituary](/docs/daniel-edburn-eesley-obituary-2018/).
- **Daughter Jane** &mdash; alive at this date; she would die tragically in her youth per Dan's 2018 obituary phrasing.

## Editorial notes

- The deceased's full name is given here only as **George E. Eesley** &mdash; his middle name "Edburn" surfaces only in Dan's 2018 obituary (where George is named *"George Edburn 'Bus' Eesley"*). "Bus" was the family nickname.
- **The airplane factory near Buffalo** &mdash; June 1942, six months into the U.S. war, the Buffalo-Niagara region was at the heart of wartime aviation production. The two most likely employers: **Curtiss-Wright** (Kenmore plant, P-40 production) or **Bell Aircraft** (Buffalo, P-39 and P-63 production). Kenmore is itself a Buffalo suburb &mdash; George's death in a Kenmore hospital is consistent with him working at the Kenmore plant.
- The clipping's marginal annotation reads ***Fremont News-Messenger 6-5-1942 P. 2 C. 6*** &mdash; that the original print was on page 2 column 6, with the headline carrying over from page 1.
