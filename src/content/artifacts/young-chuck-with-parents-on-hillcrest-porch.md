Young Chuck Eesley on the back porch of [108 Hillcrest Drive](/places/108-hillcrest-drive-marietta/) with his parents [Charlie](/family/charles-eesley/) and [Terrie](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) and his maternal grandmother [Dorothy "Dottie" Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) — Aunt Betty Wolfe's older sister, visiting from Florida. Confirmed by Chuck Eesley June 2026. The collie or sheltie at the steps is the Wolfe family dog of the period.

## The cast

- **Far left, seated on the step**: Charlie Eesley in white shirt with striped tie, mustache, holding what appears to be a small camera or set of cards. Late 1970s / early 1980s look.
- **Middle, very small**: young Chuck Eesley, c. 2–3 years old, in a white shirt with red bowtie and dark shorts, legs crossed, very blonde.
- **Center-right, seated**: Terrie Lee Wildermuth Eesley, Chuck's mother, in a maroon top and dark jeans.
- **Right, seated in blue dress with white collar**: **Grandma Dottie** — [Dorothy Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/), Terrie's mother and Betty Wolfe's older sister.
- **Bottom of the steps**: a collie or sheltie, presumably the Wolfe family dog.

## What this frame records

The Hillcrest back porch in the early Reagan years, with Chuck's immediate family and his maternal grandmother together. Dottie's presence at the Wolfes' house in Marietta is consistent with the broader [maternal Wildermuth-Davis family-care arc](/family/betty-davis/): her younger sister Betty Wolfe was the household host, the two sisters were a year apart, and the Hillcrest house was a natural gathering place for Davis-side family visits. Bob and Dottie made several trips back to Marietta across their Florida retirement years — the [c. 1985–87 van photograph](/archive/wildermuth-grandparents-drove-up-from-orlando/) captures a later one of those trips. This earlier porch frame, with Chuck very small, is probably one of their first visits in the years after he was born in late 1979.

Date based on Chuck's apparent age (born December 1979, so this would be c. 1981–1983).
