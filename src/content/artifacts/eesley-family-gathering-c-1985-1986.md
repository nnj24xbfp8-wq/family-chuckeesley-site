An extended Eesley family gathering outdoors, c. 1985–1986, eight figures grouped before green trees in what reads as a Highland Ridge summer family occasion. Cast partially identified — confirmation queued for Chuck on the un-IDed figures.

## The cast (best read)

**Back row, left to right:**
- Man with dark mustache and hair in light blue short-sleeve shirt — possibly a family-friend or in-law, cast queued
- Woman with light hair and glasses in a sleeveless pale-blue dress — cast queued
- Tall woman in pale blue dress with light hair tied back — likely [Anne Eesley](/family/anne-eesley/), home from Charleston
- Older woman with gray-white hair in tan-and-blue knit suit — [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/)
- Man with sandy hair in light blue short-sleeve shirt — possibly [Charlie Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/)
- Younger woman in red-and-blue striped shirt and pale blue shorts — possibly [Terrie Lee Wildermuth Eesley](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/)
- Older man in white short-sleeve shirt and dark slacks, glasses — [Will Eesley](/family/wilbur-eesley/) the architect

**Front:**
- Kneeling, blonde woman in dark printed sleeveless top and dark skirt — cast queued (possibly Maggie or another family woman)
- Standing in the middle, young blonde boy in blue-and-white striped polo and red shorts with sneakers — **young [Chuck Eesley](/family/charles-eric-eesley/)**, about 5 or 6 years old

## Why this date

Chuck's apparent age (born December 1979) places the photograph at c. **1985–1986**. Will Eesley would die in June 1986; if this is summer 1985 it captures the last family-gathering summer with Will still living. The bright summer light and casual short-sleeve clothes suggest a warm-weather family event, possibly a Will-and-Peggy anniversary, a Charlie-and-Terrie hosting, or a milestone family birthday.

## What this frame records

**Three of Will and Peggy's four children together with their parents and Chuck.** Anne home from Charleston; Charlie and Terrie hosting (presumed); young Chuck the only grandchild in the frame. The eight-figure frame captures the Eesley household at a moment when most of the family could still assemble in one Marietta yard — the kind of frame that becomes precious when one of the figures (Will) dies the following year.
