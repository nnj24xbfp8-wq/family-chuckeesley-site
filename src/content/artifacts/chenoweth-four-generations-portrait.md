## What the portrait shows

A formal four-generation Chenoweth family portrait, late 19th century. Four figures arranged in a roughly pyramidal composition: two bearded patriarchs at left and right of the frame; a standing mustached younger man behind them in the center; and a small child seated in front. The lighting is studio-flat; the framing is the close-cropped style of the 1880s through 1890s.

## Cast — per Roberta Burnes (June 2026)

**Roberta's identifications:**

- **Left (older bearded man, white beard, dark suit)** — **[Joseph Hill Chenoweth](/family/joseph-hill-chenoweth/)** (1832&ndash;1910). [Lillie Dale's father](/family/lillie-dale-chenoweth/). Civil War Union veteran. The face is consistent with the other [Joseph Hill portraits](/family/joseph-hill-chenoweth/) in the archive.
- **Right (older bearded man, dark eyes, dark suit)** — **[Elijah Chenoweth, Jr.](/family/elijah-chenoweth/)** (1806&ndash;1898). [Joseph Hill's father](/family/elijah-chenoweth/), and a man who lived almost the entire nineteenth century in Pleasant Township, Franklin County, Ohio.
- **Back center (standing, mustached, dark coat with patterned tie)** — *uncertain*; Roberta notes she is uncertain about the middle pair. Most plausibly **one of Joseph Hill's sons** &mdash; the third generation in the line.
- **Front center (small child in a ruffled white blouse with bow)** — *uncertain*; most plausibly **a child of the standing man** &mdash; the fourth generation in the line.

If the middle-pair identification holds, the portrait is a literal four-generation frame: Elijah Jr. → Joseph Hill → standing man → child. The Chenoweth family-album label tradition that Roberta inherited would likely place the middle pair if the original mount has a back-of-photo identification still legible.

## Dating

**Elijah Chenoweth, Jr. lived 1806&ndash;1898** &mdash; he is the constraint on the latest possible date for this portrait. Looking at the photograph, his beard is heavy white and his face has the depth of late life; the eighties are the plausible age. Combined with Joseph Hill's apparent age (50s&ndash;60s) and the small-child generation, **the frame most plausibly sits in the late 1880s through the early-to-mid 1890s** &mdash; Elijah Jr. in his late seventies through early nineties, Joseph Hill in his fifties, the standing man in his twenties or thirties, the child in early childhood.

A tighter dating &mdash; and the identification of the middle pair &mdash; would depend on the Chenoweth-album back-of-photo notation, on Egge's [Chenoweth-site](https://www.chenowethsite.com/) family-tree records for which of Joseph Hill's sons had which children at what ages, and on Roberta's deeper family memory of who in the line sat for formal four-generation studio portraits in this period.

## Why it matters

Four-generation portraits are unusual surviving documents. They were typically only sat for in families where (a) the great-grandfather was still living, (b) the great-grandfather was well enough to travel to a studio, and (c) the family had the means and the inclination to record the four-generation line on a single piece of mounted card. The Chenoweths of Franklin County had all three conditions met in the years between Joseph Hill's first grandchildren arriving and Elijah Jr.'s death in 1898 &mdash; a window of perhaps twelve to fifteen years.

The portrait is also, by way of Elijah Jr. on the right, the **only photograph of [Elijah Chenoweth, Jr.](/family/elijah-chenoweth/) currently in the archive**. Before this portrait the [Elijah Jr. page](/family/elijah-chenoweth/) had only structured records and his handwritten family register pages from Roberta's keeping. The face now joins the name.
