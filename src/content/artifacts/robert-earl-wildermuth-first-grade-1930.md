**Robert Earl Wildermuth at six years old**, c. 1930, in his **first-grade school portrait**. The earliest known photograph of him in this archive.

## What the photograph shows

A **sepia-toned head-and-shoulders school portrait** of a smiling boy of about six. He wears a **sailor-collar shirt** with a **small dark medallion or scarf at the throat** — the typical children's clothing of late-1920s and early-1930s American elementary schools. His **light brown hair** is parted at the side; his **pale eyes** are visible in the soft sepia tone; he is **smiling broadly at the camera** — the kind of open-faced smile a six-year-old who likes school gives.

The print is **labeled at the top** in the family's hand: **"CA 1930 / Robert Earl Wildermuth, I / (1924-1999) / 1st grade"** — Robert Earl's full lifespan (he died **25 June 1999** in Venice, Sarasota County, Florida) and his school grade at the time of the portrait.

## In the memoir's frame

[Robert Earl's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) opens with the **413 Harmar Street childhood** the family lived through his early-school years. He started **Harmar Elementary School** — six blocks from home, at the original Fort Harmar site at the junction of the Muskingum and Ohio rivers — at first grade in c. 1930. The school had two rooms per grade and ran first through sixth. His sister **Ruth Irene** was the only year ahead of him. He notes in the memoir that he was *"well above average academically"* and *"loved science, mathematics and the languages and enjoyed a great competition in being better prepared for class than any of the others."*

This portrait catches him at the **beginning of that academic life** — six years old, in his first school year, the smile of a boy who would go on to **Stanford (BA 1948), the Air Force career, the Pentagon, the Wildermuth/Fleming Heritage genealogy**.

## Pairing with later portraits

This is the **third formal portrait of Robert Earl** in this archive — and the earliest by some margin:

- **First-grade portrait, c. 1930** (this artifact) — age 6
- **[Stanford 1948 BA portrait](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-stanford-ba-1948/)** — age 24
- **[First Lieutenant officer portrait, c. 1951](/archive/robert-earl-air-force-officer-portrait/)** — age 27
- **[Lieutenant Colonel formal portrait, c. 1968–1970](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-lt-colonel-portrait/)** — age 44–46

Together the four span **forty years of his life**, from the smiling six-year-old to the Pentagon Lt. Col. The shared element across all four: he is **smiling steadily, slightly to the side, with the same pale-eyed open expression**. The constancy of the face is its own family-archive feature.
