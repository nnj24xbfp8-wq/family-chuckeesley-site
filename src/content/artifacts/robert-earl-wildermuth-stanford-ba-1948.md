Robert Earl Wildermuth's Stanford diploma &mdash; the **first** Stanford degree in the family. Conferred October 1, 1948, in Biological Sciences. Signed by President **J. Wallace Sterling**, the eighth president of Stanford; by Paul C. Edwards, then President of the Board of Trustees; and on the School of Biological Sciences side by **Douglas M. Whitaker**, a developmental biologist who in the same period oversaw the post-war expansion of biology at Stanford.

The path to this document is, in a single sentence, the path of his entire generation: a B-24 navigator from Marietta, Ohio, recently returned from the Pacific theater, finished a Bachelor of Arts at Stanford on the GI Bill in two years.

It is the first piece of paper that makes Robert Earl's later remark &mdash; closing his family history by addressing *"future genealogists"* &mdash; rhyme with [his grandson Chuck](/family/charles-eric-eesley/) joining the Stanford faculty a generation later. The diploma is the document that put both of them on the same campus.
