**Robert Earl Wildermuth and a fellow servicemember in front of palm trees and pyramidal tents, Pacific theater, c. 1944–1945.** Identified by Chuck Eesley in 2026 as featuring his grandfather Robert Earl.

Two young men stand close together in the foreground — the man on the left **shirtless, in fatigue pants, with what appears to be a Browning Automatic pistol holster on his hip and a knife sheath**; the man on the right **in light short-sleeved uniform shirt and pants, smiling broadly into the camera**. Behind them: **palm trees, pyramidal tents, the rough scrub-and-sand ground of a South Pacific island base.** The shadows are sharp and the light is the high overhead light of a tropical afternoon.

**Which of the two men is Robert Earl is open**, pending Chuck's closer identification or comparison against the [July 1944 Marietta uniform portrait](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-july-1944/) of him.

The frame is the off-duty register of Robert Earl's documented Pacific service — the [WWII combat log *The Big One*](/docs/the-big-one/) and the [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) describe the missions; this and its companion [eight-men-tent-camp frame](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-b24-crew-tent-camp/) give the visual texture of the days between them. The two together place Robert Earl in the documentary record of the 90th Bomb Group "Jolly Rogers" Pacific campaign as a young man in his twenties, in the heat, at the camp.

> *Family identification by Chuck Eesley, 2026.*
