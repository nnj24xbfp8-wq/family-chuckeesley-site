The three eldest [Will and Peggy Eesley](/family/wilbur-eesley/) children seated in a row on a patterned rug, c. 1948–1950, before their youngest sister Margaret "Maggie" was born in 1951.

## The cast

- **Far left, small toddler in dark velvet jumper and white blouse, white socks and small brown booties**: [Anne Eesley](/family/anne-eesley/), age about 2 (born 1 March 1946).
- **Center, infant in a high chair in matching dark jumper, mouth open laughing**: [Charles "Charlie" McMaster Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/), age about 1 (born 17 February 1947) — Chuck's father.
- **Far right, slightly older girl seated cross-legged in dark velvet jumper with white blouse and white socks and Mary Janes**: [Jeanne Eesley](/family/jeanne-eesley-kamiab/), age about 4 or 5 — the eldest, who would marry Joe Kamiab in June 1965.

## What this frame records

The three eldest Eesley children together, posed by their parents for what reads as a formal childhood portrait. The dark velvet jumpers all three are dressed in — matching across the older sister, the middle daughter, and even the infant brother — were the dress-up clothes of the late-1940s small-American-town family-portrait studio: dark velvet for formality, white blouses for contrast, white socks and small leather shoes.

This is **the earliest archive photograph of Anne**. It is also one of the earliest archive photographs of Charlie before the 12th-grade autobiography's birth account (*"I was a mess!"* the autobiography opens, talking about the club foot, hernia, and scratched-up face that nearly killed him as an infant). Charlie's laughing face here, at about a year old, is the recovery from that difficult beginning. The frame predates Maggie's 1951 birth — the four-child Eesley family had not quite assembled yet.
