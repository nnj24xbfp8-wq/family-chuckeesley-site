A color church-interior photograph: **bride in a long white dress holding a bouquet, groom in a dark suit with glasses standing beside her**, the two at the altar of a small church with an American flag visible at the side. **Best-guess cast: Jeanne Eesley and Joseph Kamiab on their wedding day, June 1965.** Cast queued for Chuck's confirmation.

## Why the guess

Three signals point to **Jeanne + Joe in June 1965**:

- **The date and venue.** [Charlie Eesley's c. 1964–65 12th-grade autobiography](/docs/charles-eesley-12th-grade-autobiography-1965/) tells us that his eldest sister **Jeanne married Joseph (Joe) Kamiab in Marietta in June 1965**, the year of Charlie's high-school graduation, in the family's Marietta-church-and-family network.
- **The photographic style.** The Kodachrome saturation, the bride's veil, the groom's narrow dark tie, and the bouquet form &mdash; daisies and white flowers &mdash; match mid-1960s American wedding-photograph conventions.
- **The household context.** This is among the only mid-1960s wedding photographs surfacing from the Charlie-and-Will-and-Peggy household archive that has not already been placed; the recently-completed [autobiography](/docs/charles-eesley-12th-grade-autobiography-1965/) framing of Jeanne's June 1965 wedding makes her the most likely subject.

## What the frame records

If confirmed, this is **the only photograph of Jeanne Eesley Kamiab's wedding in the archive** &mdash; a documentary frame of the eldest of the four Will-and-Peggy Eesley children entering the marriage that took her out of the 651 Sixth Street household into her own life. Charlie's autobiography names the wedding briefly; this photograph would supply its visual record.
