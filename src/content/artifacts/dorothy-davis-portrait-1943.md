A studio head-and-shoulders portrait of [Dorothy Marie Davis](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) at eighteen, the year she graduated from Marietta High School and began work as a bookkeeper at Citizen's Bank, Marietta. The portrait shows her in dark formal dress with a white peter-pan collar, hair set in soft 1940s waves. The typed caption Robert Earl set beneath the print in the 1990 Heritage reads simply: *"Dorothy Davis in 1943."*

## What it documents

The 1943 portrait is the *graduation-year* portrait — the small, formal photograph that documented the closing of high school and the entry into wartime working life. The U.S. was deep into World War II; Dorothy's future husband [Robert Earl](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/), three years older and already in the Army Air Forces, was on the path that would put him in B-24 navigation training and then in the Pacific. They had not yet met. The portrait sits in the archive as the *before* image of that meeting.

It is paired in the [1990 Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/) with the [1944 portrait](/archive/dorothy-davis-portrait-1944/) on the same page — the two consecutive years carrying a small documentary of one young woman's transition through the war years.
