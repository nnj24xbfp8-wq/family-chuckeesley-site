**Charlie Eesley in Vietnam** — likely 1970 — at a U.S. military club somewhere in country. Foreground: Charlie in olive Army utility uniform with **"EESLEY"** name tape on his right chest, **"U.S. ARMY"** tape on the left, **Screaming Eagle patch of the 101st Airborne Division** on his right shoulder, **boonie hat** on. He looks straight into the camera, faintly amused.

Background: a Vietnamese woman in a **green áo dài** at the corner of the frame on the left; behind her a wooden-panelled bar wall with a club-rules placard (partially legible: *"...RULES...", "...WILL NOT...", "...LEAVE...", "...FLOOR SHOW...", "...AGE TO ENTER...", "...GUEST CARD..."*), a large **"B"** logo sign behind Charlie's head (perhaps an "EM CLUB" or service-club designation), and another soldier and figure further back at the bar. In the foreground sit several **Budweiser cans** — the U.S. military's beer-of-record across Vietnam.

This is **the only photograph of Charlie in uniform in Vietnam currently placed in this archive.** It catches him in the off-duty register of his tour — not on patrol, not in the field, but at the club between deployments — and his **101st Airborne** patch is the unit identification the eulogy and the [Letters from Vietnam](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) had previously left implicit. The 101st Airborne was operating in I Corps (Northern South Vietnam) through 1970 and 1971, with major bases at Phu Bai and Camp Eagle near Huế.

The image complements the [Vietnam letter sequence](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) — the letters carry his voice across the year; this frame carries his face. He is twenty-three or twenty-four years old.

> *Source: Eesley family archive; identification by Chuck Eesley, 2026.*
