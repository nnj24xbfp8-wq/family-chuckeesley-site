The earliest known image of [Dorothy Marie Davis](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) — Chuck's maternal grandmother — and the earliest image of the three Davis sisters together. Three small girls in light dresses standing in front of a porch railing or fence, with a faint exterior wall behind. The typed caption beneath gives the line-up left-to-right: *Mary Davis age 5, Dorothy Davis age 4, Betty Davis age 3.* The ages place the frame at c. 1929 — Mary born January 1924, Dorothy February 1925, Betty March 1926.

## What it documents

It is the document of the *complete childhood Davis-sister set,* one year and ten months apart at the extremes. Three girls under five, in the years just before the Unionville house fire and the family's recovery in a one-room garage through the cold winter that followed (also described in the same [1990 Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/)). None of the three knew yet that Dorothy would later be the one who left Ohio for the West Coast with [Robert Earl](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/), Mary would stay in southeastern Ohio, and Betty would marry [Norm Wolfe](/family/norman-graham-wolfe/) — the *"Uncle Norm"* of Chuck's childhood and one of the *"Betty and Norm Wolfe"* named in [Terrie's 2017 obituary](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) as *"close relatives by way of long entertainment of visiting family."* The kinship the obituary preserves was a literal sister-bond at its origin.

The photograph is the first of three frames of the sisters that survive in the Heritage; the next two are the [c. 1930 "why so glum?"](/archive/davis-sisters-1930-why-so-glum/) frame and the [c. 1931 puppies-and-bare-feet](/archive/davis-sisters-puppies-1931/) frame.
