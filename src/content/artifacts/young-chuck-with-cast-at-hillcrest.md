A color indoor photograph of **young Chuck Eesley on a couch in a Marietta living room**, in a blue Phillips-logo sweatshirt with what Chuck recalls was **most likely a sprained ankle** rather than a cast on his arm. Confirmed by Chuck Eesley 2026 as a frame of himself; injury type and exact venue queued. A springer-spaniel-sized dog sits at the foreground, an older couple at the right edge of the frame.

## The injured-day texture

Chuck's own memory of his childhood: *"when I was sick and had to stay home from school, I was often either at the Wolfes' house at 108 Hillcrest or at [Faye Angel](/family/faye-angel/)'s house"* (his godmother). A sprained ankle is the kind of stay-home-and-be-watched-over situation that would have routed young Chuck to one of those small-town-extended-family care houses &mdash; the [Betty and Norm Wolfe](/family/betty-davis/) house at Hillcrest being the most likely setting given the older-couple-and-dog edge of the frame.

## Why c. 1985–1990

Chuck born 1979; a 6-to-11-year-old in the frame puts the photograph at **c. 1985 to 1990**. The blue Phillips sweatshirt is consistent with a youth-sports or camp branding of the late 1980s.

## What this frame records

A documentary frame of the **sick-and-injured-day caregiver network** Chuck has named across [Betty's](/family/betty-davis/), [Norm's](/family/norman-graham-wolfe/), and [Faye Angel](/family/faye-angel/)'s pages &mdash; the actual lived texture of a Marietta boy resting his sprained ankle with a family dog at his feet while the elder couple of the household kept him company. The kind of family-care arrangement that runs three generations on the Davis side and that Betty's obituary frames as her lifelong arc.
