## Why the guess

Three converging signals point to **Chuck + Norm + Betty** at **Hillcrest**:

- **The boy's age and the arm-in-cast.** Chuck's own memory of his childhood records that *"when I was sick and had to stay home from school, I was often either at the Wolfes' house at 108 Hillcrest or at Faye Angel's house."* A broken arm is the kind of stay-home-from-school injury that would have sent young Chuck straight to one of the two care-network houses.
- **The older couple's appearance.** The older woman is consistent with [Aunt Betty Davis Wolfe](/family/betty-davis/) (b. 1926, so age 60-ish in this frame); the older man is consistent with [Uncle Norm](/family/norman-graham-wolfe/) (same age cohort, soft-spoken, glasses).
- **The interior.** The couch, the pattern, and the lamp suggest a 1980s small-town-Ohio home interior consistent with [the 2011 Thanksgiving photograph](/archive/norm-betty-wolfe-thanksgiving-2011/) of the same room at Hillcrest.

## Why the date range

Chuck born 1979; a 6-to-11-year-old in the frame puts the photograph at **c. 1985 to 1990**. The blue Phillips sweatshirt is consistent with a youth-sports or camp branding of the late 1980s.

## What this frame would record

If the cast is confirmed, this photograph is **a documentary frame of the sick-day caregiver network Chuck has named on Betty's, Norm's, and Faye Angel's pages** &mdash; the actual lived texture of an injured Marietta boy resting on his great-aunt and great-uncle's Hillcrest couch with their dog at his feet. The kind of family-care arrangement that runs three generations on the Davis side and that Betty's obituary frames as her lifelong arc: caring for her mother in the 1940s, raising her three daughters in the 1950s and 60s, hosting her sister's grandson at her Hillcrest table from the 1980s on.
