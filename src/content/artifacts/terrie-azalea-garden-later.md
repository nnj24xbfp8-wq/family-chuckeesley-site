Terrie in front of the same pink azalea, on the same Marietta street, in a year past the [2011 print](/archive/terrie-azalea-garden-2011/). White-haired, in a striped sleeveless top, standing where the *"flowers my best"* line of her [poem](/docs/please-dont-cry-for-me-terrie-bain/) had its annual fact.

The pair — *Terrie in the garden, May 2011* and *Terrie in the garden, a few years later* — is the family's own then-and-now of the same person and the same azalea: continuity made out of the smallest possible variable, a single bush in flower, a single year apart from the next.
