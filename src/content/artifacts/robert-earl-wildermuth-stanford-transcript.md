**Robert Earl Wildermuth's Stanford University registrar's card** — the official academic record of his **1946–1948 GI Bill undergraduate years at Stanford**, the period he transitioned from B-24 navigator to civilian and earned the **BA in Biological Sciences** conferred 1 October 1948.

## What the document records

The card carries Robert Earl's identifying information at the top: **WILDERMUTH, Robert Earl**, with his Marietta, Ohio home address and his father **Earl A. Wildermuth** listed as parent. The Stanford registrar's office filed it through the standard transcript-card format of the era: ruled rows for each course title, columns for unit count, grade, and semester.

Visible course entries include **English, Mathematics (Algebra)**, **Chemistry**, **Spanish (or another language)**, and other lower-division work consistent with a **pre-medical track of the late 1940s**. The handwritten ink in some cells records grades and quarter-credit annotations; the column headers point to Stanford's then-distinctive quarter-based curriculum.

The card is the **structured academic counterpart** to the [Stanford BA diploma](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-stanford-ba-1948/) already in this archive — together the two documents anchor Robert Earl's Stanford years in primary-source form. The diploma celebrates the conferral; this card records the work that earned it.

## In the memoir's frame

Robert Earl's [1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) records that he came to Stanford on the **GI Bill** in 1946 after his Pacific tour and his marriage to Dorothy Davis. *"Stanford University turned out to be a dream,"* he wrote. *"Academically Stanford was rated number three in the United States when I attended and it is known as the 'Harvard of the West.' Millionaire's sons and daughters go there and movie star's children strived for admission and here I was a young guy from rural Ohio and from the wrong side of town at that."* He applied to medical school during his Stanford senior year and was denied; took the BA anyway and went to work in industry. The course grades on this card are the record of the work he did in that period of denied-medical-school disappointment.

## Why it matters

The Stanford transcript is the **earliest piece of structured American higher-education documentation** in this archive's Wildermuth-side records. It documents the **academic Robert Earl** — the disciplined post-war GI-Bill student whose grades determined whether he could go on to medical school. The denial of that medical-school admission is what redirected his life into industry, Korea, the Pentagon, the radar observer schools, and ultimately the Air Force career he made instead. **The grades on this card are the precise moment the life turned.**
