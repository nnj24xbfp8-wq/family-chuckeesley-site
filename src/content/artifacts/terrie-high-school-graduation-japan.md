Terrie Lee Wildermuth at her **high school graduation in Japan**, flanked by her parents — her father [**Robert Earl Wildermuth**](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) on her right in **U.S. Air Force dress blues** with command pilot wings and ribbons, and her mother [**Dottie**](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) on her left in a pale jacket pinned with a leaf brooch. Terrie holds an engraved plaque whose inscription is legible to the camera: ***TERRIE WILDERMUTH · 1ST PLACE.***

This is the visual record of the *"Terrie traveled the U.S. and Japan until graduating high school"* line of her [obituary](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) — the closing image of the Air Force-childhood years before she came back to Ohio for [Marietta College](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/). The American flag and the formal drapery behind the family mark it as a school ceremony on a U.S. military post; Robert Earl's uniform is the official one, which means the photograph was either taken at the ceremony itself or in a portrait studio session just after.

A companion frame from the same event survives — Terrie alone, **in cap, tassel, and gown, receiving her diploma** in the moment of presentation:
