A formal couple's portrait of [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) and [Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) taken at [Itazuke Air Base, Fukuoka, Japan](/places/itazuke-air-base/) in 1968 — the last year of the family's three-year posting to Itazuke (1965–1968), which gave [Chuck's mother Terrie](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) her high-school years in Japan named explicitly in Terrie's 2017 obituary. Robert Earl stands in a dark suit with a boutonnière on the lapel; Dorothy beside him in a pale formal dress, holding a small bouquet. The typed caption Robert Earl set beneath the print in the 1990 Heritage names the place precisely: *"Picture taken at Itazuke Air Base, Fukuoka, Japan in 1968."*

## What it documents

The Itazuke portrait is the *adult* image of the 1943 and 1944 Marietta portraits — the same Dorothy Davis, now twenty-five years married, the mother of four, on a foreign Air Force base on the other side of the world from Waterford, Ohio. The boutonnière suggests an event — possibly the formal occasion of [Terrie's high school graduation in Japan](/archive/terrie-high-school-graduation-japan/) the same year, or a squadron / base function from the closing months of Robert Earl's tour as commander.

The frame is also a piece of the [Japan-side](/places/itazuke-air-base/) of the [family-threads essay's #8 thread](/docs/family-threads/) — the Japan-and-China place-and-language curiosity that runs through three generations of the Wildermuth-and-Eesley families.
