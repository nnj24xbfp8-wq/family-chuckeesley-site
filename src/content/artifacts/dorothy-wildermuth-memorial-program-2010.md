The two-page printed program from Dorothy Marie (Davis) Wildermuth's memorial service, held **Friday, 13 August 2010 at 7:00 p.m.** at the **Church of Christ, 534 Sixth Street, Marietta, Ohio**, five months after her death on 5 March 2010 in Brookhaven, Georgia at age 85. The program carries her full biographical sketch in a single panel and the order-of-service on the facing panel.

## What the program records

The biographical panel on the left settles or confirms several facts:

- **Born 24 February 1925 in Waterford, Ohio** &mdash; the second daughter of [**Bessie Marie Davis**](/family/bessie-hill-davis/) and [**Homer Edward Davis**](/family/homer-davis/). Grew up in **Waterford / Unionville, Ohio**, and **graduated from Marietta High School in 1943** &mdash; the same Marietta school district [her own daughter Terrie](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) would later teach in for twenty-three years.
- **Married [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) on 20 April 1946 in Marietta, Ohio**. The marriage lasted **fifty-three years**, including the Air Force decades that took them across Florida, California, Oklahoma, Ohio, Colorado, Texas, Maryland, and Japan.
- **Career**: **bookkeeper at Citizen's Bank, Marietta**; **the South East Bank in Orlando, Florida**; the **Hoover Library at Stanford University**; and the **Maitland Public Library in Maitland, Florida**. Two of those four positions place her on the Stanford campus (alongside her husband's 1948 BA) and in the Florida public-library system &mdash; both of which would have meant something specific to her granddaughter Terrie's later children's-librarian and Title-I-teacher career in Marietta.
- **Faith**: attended the **Methodist Church** &mdash; a different denominational seat than the [First Congregational UCC](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) which served as the Eesley-side family church across four generations. *Dottie was the Methodist of the maternal line, not the Congregationalist of the paternal line.*
- **What she enjoyed**: *"flowers, singing, square-dancing, and quilting and creating many beautiful needlework projects for her children and grandchildren."* The list runs parallel to the **azalea-garden, library-desk, classroom-reading** texture of Terrie's own life in the next generation &mdash; the household-as-creative-space practice passed from mother to daughter to granddaughter.
- **Survived by** her four children &mdash; [**Terrie L. Bain (Benjamin)**](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) of Marietta; [**Sandra S. Clement (Dunwoody, Georgia)**](/family/sandra-sue-wildermuth-clement/); [**Debra J. Massaro (Angelo)**](/family/debbie-wildermuth/) of Pembroke, Virginia; and [**Robert E. Wildermuth, Jr. (Dawn)**](/family/rob-wildermuth/) of Sarasota, Florida &mdash; and her **grandchildren: [Charles Eric Eesley](/family/charles-eric-eesley/), Giancarlo Salvatora Massaro, Briana Marie Massaro, "Trey" Robert Earl Wildermuth III, Thomas Mathew Wildermuth, and Sarah Marie Wildermuth**.
- **Died**: **5 March 2010 in Brookhaven, Georgia, age 85**. Brookhaven is just outside Atlanta &mdash; the same metropolitan area where [her daughter Sandy](/family/sandra-sue-wildermuth-clement/) was living at the time (and is named as living in Terrie's own 2017 obituary).

## What the program names of the service

The right-hand panel records the order of the **Friday, 13 August 2010, 7:00 p.m.** service at the **Church of Christ**, Marietta:

- **Message by Roger Rush.**
- **Solo:** *"Ave Maria"* &mdash; **Julia Dryer**, Junior, Marietta College.
- **Family remembrances:** [**Terrie L. Bain**](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/), [**Sandra S. Clement**](/family/sandra-sue-wildermuth-clement/), [**Debra J. Massaro**](/family/debbie-wildermuth/) &mdash; the three Wildermuth sisters, in birth order, each speaking for their mother.
- **Solo:** *"Great Is Thy Faithfulness"* &mdash; Julia Dryer.
- **Concluding thoughts by Roger Rush.**
- **Solo:** *"Peace Like A River"* &mdash; Julia Dryer.

The closing line of the program, printed below the music: ***"This is a time for gathering all the best of a good life into the pages of memory, to be held in the heart forever."***

The program sits in this archive as the most complete single document on Dottie that survives.
