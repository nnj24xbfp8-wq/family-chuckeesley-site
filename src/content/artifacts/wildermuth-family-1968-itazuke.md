The Wildermuth family of six in one frame — Robert Earl, Dorothy, and all four children — at [Itazuke Air Base, Fukuoka, Japan](/places/itazuke-air-base/) circa 1968, the closing year of the family's three-year Itazuke tour. The typed caption Robert Earl set beneath the print in the 1990 Heritage gives the line-up:

> *Left to right in back: Sandra Sue and Terrie Lee Wildermuth. In front: Debra Jean, Robert Earl Sr., Dorothy Marie (Davis) and Bobby Jr., circa 1968.*

[Terrie Lee](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) (Chuck's mother) and her older sister [Sandra Sue](/family/sandra-sue-wildermuth-clement/) stand behind their parents in the older-teen positions; [Debra Jean](/family/debbie-wildermuth/) and toddler [Bobby Jr.](/family/rob-wildermuth/) flank Robert Earl and Dorothy in front. The pose is the deliberate family-of-six composition Robert Earl had been carrying with him through the Air Force postings.

## What it documents

It is the *Japan-tour family portrait* — the document of who was in the Wildermuth household during the three Itazuke years (1965–1968) — and one of the rare frames in the archive that holds all four Wildermuth children together at one time. By the year of the print Terrie was finishing her [high-school graduation in Japan](/archive/terrie-high-school-graduation-japan/) and preparing for the move back to Ohio that would land her at Marietta College a year later, where she would meet [Charlie Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/).

The frame pairs with the [parallel mid-1960s Wildermuth family portrait](/archive/wildermuth-family-portrait-1960s/) on Dottie's page — the same six people, photographed two or three years apart on opposite sides of the Pacific.
