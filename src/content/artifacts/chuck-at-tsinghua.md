[Chuck](/family/charles-eric-eesley/) at the famous **清華園 (Tsinghua University) main gate** in Beijing &mdash; the white classical-arched gate with the Qing-era inscription *Tsinghua Garden* in calligraphy above the keystone. Chuck in a light blue oxford shirt and grey trousers, standing centered between two flower-filled urns; a small Chinese child in a navy skirt and pale top stands in profile at the left-hand urn, unposed.

Tsinghua is one of the two most prestigious universities in China &mdash; the rough Chinese counterpart of Stanford on the engineering side, and a frequent academic-exchange partner for the Stanford School of Engineering where Chuck holds his faculty appointment. The gate itself dates from the Republican period and is one of the most photographed academic landmarks in the country.

The photograph belongs in the archive as the **closing parenthesis on a small geography**: it pairs implicitly with [Lijie at St Michael's Cathedral in Qingdao](/places/qingdao/) on the other side of the Zhou&mdash;Eesley join &mdash; her schoolyard, his academic counterpart, both photographed two decades after they met. The Eesley faculty appointment at Stanford and the gate at Tsinghua share enough institutional shape that the frame is itself a quiet record of where the next chapter of the family's academic line sits.
