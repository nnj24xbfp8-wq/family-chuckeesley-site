[Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) at her sewing machine in the Orlando house, around 1988 — the second-to-last image of her in the [1990 Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/), placed just below the [1968 Itazuke family group](/archive/wildermuth-family-1968-itazuke/) as a small *now* counterpart to the *then* of the Japan years. The typed caption Robert Earl set beneath the print reads: *"Dorothy Marie busy at her hobby making quilts, circa 1988."*

## What it documents

Quilting and needlework were one of the recurring practices the [2010 memorial program](/archive/dorothy-wildermuth-memorial-program-2010/) named two decades later as among the things Dottie *"enjoyed"*: *"flowers, singing, square-dancing, and quilting and creating many beautiful needlework projects for her children and grandchildren."* The hobby ran for years; this 1988 frame is the only documentary image of her at the work itself.

Robert Earl placed the frame at the end of his Heritage's Davis section partly as a portrait of his wife in her late life, partly as a small acknowledgment that the *"many long hours waiting for me to finish my poring over old court records"* — the line he used in the document's dedication to her — were not idle ones. The quilting was the parallel work she was doing while he was at the genealogy desk.
