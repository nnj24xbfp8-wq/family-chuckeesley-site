**[Fay Elsie Heter Angel](/family/faye-angel/) at her house in Marietta**, with **young Chuck Eesley in the background** and **Chuck's father [Charlie Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/)** also present &mdash; confirmed by Chuck Eesley 2026.

This is **a documentary frame of the second small-town extended-family-care household of Chuck's childhood** &mdash; the [Angel house on Washington Street](/family/faye-angel/) where Chuck spent sick-and-injured days with his godmother across his childhood, with the picture-framing basement, the Marietta College Book Store Manager's hospitality, and the Sunday-school-teacher warmth Faye's [2011 obituary](/docs/fay-elsie-heter-angel-obituary-2011/) describes. **This is the Angel house, the way Chuck remembers it &mdash; the kitchen Faye standing in.**

## The triangulation of family

The photograph holds **three figures across two generations of Chuck's life**:

- **[Faye Angel](/family/faye-angel/)** &mdash; Chuck's **godmother**, the **mother-in-law of his great-uncle [William Harvey Davis Sr.](/family/william-harvey-davis-sr/)** through Becky's 1963 marriage, the Marietta College Book Store Manager who hosted his mother Terrie on her college courtship visits to Charlie, the Sunday-school teacher of twenty years at Christ United Methodist Church.
- **[Charlie Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/)** &mdash; Chuck's father, husband to Terrie, the man whose college courtship Faye had hosted his future wife through, decades earlier.
- **Young Chuck** &mdash; the godchild Faye had taken responsibility for at his baptism, the child of the courtship she had hosted, sitting in her kitchen as a small boy.

The single Kodachrome captures **the godmother arc opened and continued**: Faye hosted Charlie's bride; the bride had Chuck; Faye hosted Chuck. Three generations of family closeness photographed at once.

## The date

If young Chuck is school-age (visible in the background but small) the photograph plausibly dates to **the 1990s**. Faye would have been **in her 70s or early 80s** in the frame; her [2011 obituary](/docs/fay-elsie-heter-angel-obituary-2011/) frames her ninety-three years of activity through to nearly the end.
