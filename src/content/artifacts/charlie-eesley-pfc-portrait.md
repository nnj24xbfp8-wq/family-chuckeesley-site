The official Army portrait of **Charlie Eesley** as **Private First Class** &mdash; **"PFC EESLEY"** typed on the name plate at the bottom of the frame. Black-and-white head-and-shoulders studio shot, the standardised U.S. Army induction photograph form of the late 1960s.

This is **the earliest documented military photograph of Charlie in this archive** &mdash; predating the [c. 1970 Vietnam EM club photo](/archive/charlie-eesley-vietnam-club/) and the [July 1970 hooch photograph](/archive/charlie-eesley-july-1970-vietnam/) by roughly a year. He looks **clean-shaven, fair-haired, in olive utility uniform** &mdash; the standard cleanup that followed basic training. Most likely taken at **Fort Benning, Georgia** or **Fort Knox, Kentucky** during the U.S. Army's induction-portrait routine.

The contrast with the July 1970 Vietnam frame is documentary: a year before that photograph, this is Charlie at the beginning of a tour he had not yet begun.

> *Source: Eesley family archive. File name "Charles_M_Eesley.jpeg" suggests this was retained as the canonical portrait of his Army-era identity.*
