**Formal sepia cabinet-card studio portrait** of the **[Jacob and Elizabeth Wildermuth family](/docs/wildermuths-possibly-related/)**, c. 1885–1895. **Eleven figures arranged in three rows**, with Chuck Eesley's June 2026 identification by left-to-right order.

## The cast

> *Wildermuth Family.*
>
> *Back Men — LtoR — Charles E, John "Leb", Harry J, Jesse W.*
>
> *Women Middle LtoR — Maude B, Lucy, Zella M, "Lanessa" Louise.*
>
> *Jacob and Elizabeth sitting. Troy B middle.*

**Eleven figures across three rows:**

- **Back row**: Four adult sons — **Charles E** (far left, in pinstriped suit), **John "Leb"** (with mustache), **Harry J**, and **Jesse W** (in dark suits and bow ties).
- **Middle row**: Four adult daughters — **Maude B**, **Lucy**, **Zella M**, and **"Lanessa" Louise** (the eldest-looking woman at the right edge, wearing a striking checked/plaid skirt).
- **Front row seated**: The patriarch **Jacob Wildermuth** (with substantial mustache, three-piece dark suit, watch chain visible) at center; their **young son Troy B** in a dark child's dress with a bow at his neck between his parents; the matriarch **Elizabeth Wildermuth** (in a long dark dress with high collar) at the right.

## Why this is an open case in the archive

**This family is not in Dale Eesley's FamilySearch tree** — none of the nine documented children's given names matches anyone in the structured record of Robert Earl Wildermuth's Marietta-Ohio Wildermuth line. The artifact stands as the **central case** of the [Wildermuths possibly related](/docs/wildermuths-possibly-related/) research thread, which lays out the three plausible readings: (1) a genuinely separate Wildermuth-surname family of German-American immigrants, (2) a distant-cousin branch sharing a deep common Württemberg ancestor, or (3) a photograph Robert Earl collected during his 1971–1989 genealogy research from a fellow Wildermuth-surname correspondent.

The c. 1885–1895 dating (cabinet card style, women's clothing, Jacob's apparent age) suggests Jacob himself was born c. 1835–1845 — the same generation as [Catharina Boeshar Wildermuth](/family/catharina-boeshar-wildermuth/) (b. 1840), Robert Earl's actual great-grandmother.
