A group portrait of **[Charles Leonard Eesley](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/)** with five of his adult sons against a hedge in formal dark suits, with a young grandson at his right hand in shorts and a sweater. **The patriarch is the elder man at the far right** &mdash; lighter suit, white hair, the only one of the seven figures whose age places him as the father rather than a son.

## The date window

The photograph has to have been taken **between July 1939 and July 1942**. The reasoning: it shows Charles Leonard with **five adult sons**, but he had seven adult sons born by 1916 &mdash; Leonard David (1904), Thomas Leonard (1906), Mike (1908), Donald Stuart (1908), Will (1910), Lyle (1914), and Dale Dudley George (1916). **Dale died at twenty-two on July 12, 1939**; **Lyle died at twenty-seven on July 25, 1942** at Cabanatuan. The frame shows five sons. That puts it after Dale's death (six sons living) but with one son not present in the frame, or before Lyle's death (so Lyle would be one of the five). The presence of Lyle in this photograph confirms it was taken **before July 1942**, and the five-rather-than-six count puts it after July 1939.

## The grandson

The young boy at Charles Leonard's side is one of his grandsons; the most likely candidates by age and presence at the Bexley house in the late 1930s/early 1940s are children of his eldest sons. Identification pending family confirmation.

## Why this frame matters

This is the **only photograph of [Lyle Eesley](/family/lyle-eesley/) (1914&ndash;1942) currently placed in the archive.** Until this image was added, his page held only the bare line from Bean's register and the Cabanatuan death date. He died at twenty-seven in a Japanese prison camp four thousand miles from this hedge in Bexley; the frame here gives the archive a face for him in the years he was still living at home, alongside his father and brothers, in formal dress on what must have been a particular family occasion the deck doesn't name.

The other sons in the frame &mdash; Leonard David, Thomas Leonard, Mike, Don Stuart, Will, depending on which five are present &mdash; will be identified by name in a future update as family memory and other photographs allow them to be matched up.
