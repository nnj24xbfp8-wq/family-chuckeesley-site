The full **Wildermuth family** in a single frame, all six of them: [**Robert Earl**](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) and [**Dottie**](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) with their four children — [**Terrie Lee**](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) and [**Sandra Sue**](/family/sandra-sue-wildermuth-clement/) as teenagers, [**Debra ("Debbie")**](/family/debbie-wildermuth/) the school-age younger sister, and [**Robert Jr. ("Rob")**](/family/rob-wildermuth/) the toddler. From the children's ages this is the **mid-1960s**, the Air-Force years that took the family across the U.S. and Japan — the same stretch that produced the [Stanford return visit to the White Memorial Fountain on 24 August 1965](/places/stanford-university/) and Terrie's [high school graduation in Japan](/archive/terrie-high-school-graduation-japan/).

It is, alongside the two Stanford-fountain frames, the clearest portrait of all four Wildermuth siblings together — the generation that would later scatter to Marietta (Terrie), Atlanta (Sandy), Venice, FL (Rob), and elsewhere (Debbie).
