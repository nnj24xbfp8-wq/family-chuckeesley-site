**Robert Earl Wildermuth's professional credentials card from the American Medical Record Association** (now the **American Health Information Management Association**, AHIMA) — the structured documentation of his **second career** as a medical records administrator, after his 1970 retirement from the Air Force.

## What the document records

The card is the **AMRA professional standardized form** of the era, with sections for **Academic Honors**, **Membership in Student Organizations**, **Student Activities**, and **Scholarship and/or Grants**. Robert Earl's photograph (small black-and-white head shot) appears at the upper left. The handwritten entries below include a chronological list of memberships and registrations, with dates and locations.

Legible entries include:

- **7-14-75** &mdash; *"Am. Med. Recerd. Asso. Chicago, Ill"* — his American Medical Record Association registration
- **8-18-72** &mdash; *"Hlth Tech. U. Delaware"* or similar — Health Technology / University connection
- **9/10/40** &mdash; *"Self. Ormond Beach, Fla"* — likely his Florida residence
- **1-18-62** &mdash; *"Self Tampa, Florida"* — his McDill AFB Tampa years
- **10-19-56** &mdash; *"Onif Oklahoma"* — likely McConnell or another Oklahoma assignment
- **8/26/56** &mdash; *"Onif Midwest City, Okla"* — Tinker AFB area

The dates trace **his Air Force career assignments through 1970** and then his **post-retirement medical-records career through the 1970s**.

## In the memoir's frame

[Robert Earl's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) records his second career: after the 1970 Air Force retirement and a year of unsatisfying high-school teaching at Seabreeze High School in Daytona Beach, *"I inquired at a new University that had just opened in Orlando, the Florida Technological University later to become the University of Central Florida. I found that this university had a School of Health and one of the degrees offered in that school was a degree in Medical Record Administration. So at the rather advanced age for students, 48, I enrolled in college and went to work on another degree."*

He worked part-time at a local hospital while studying and earned the degree in **two years (1972–1974)**. He then went to work for **Blue Cross of Florida as Hospital Representative and later as Charge Auditor**, *"making sure that the Blue Cross patients got the health care that Blue Cross was being charged for."* He took early retirement from Blue Cross in **1986** at age 62 to pursue genealogy.

The credentials card visible here is the **professional documentation of that second career** — the structured record of how a B-24 navigator and Air Force Lt. Col. became a medical records administrator in his fifties.
