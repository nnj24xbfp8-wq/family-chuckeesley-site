Terrie Lee at the **White Memorial Fountain** in the heart of the Stanford campus — the same bronze "Claw" by Aristides Demetrios that her mother Dottie had stood at on 24 August 1965 holding the youngest of Terrie's siblings — her brother [Uncle Rob](/family/rob-wildermuth/) as a toddler — in the [family return-visit photograph](/places/stanford-university/). Terrie thirty-some years on, in a green blouse, Memorial Court arches behind her — a return to the campus her father had crossed on the GI Bill and her son would later join as faculty.

It is the single photograph in the archive of **Terrie alone on the Stanford campus** — the visit photographs of her father (1948–49), of the family in the 1960s, and of her at the Alumni Center with her father's yearbook are the rest of the family's [Stanford record](/places/stanford-university/); this one is the one without anyone else in the frame, the campus simply visited.
