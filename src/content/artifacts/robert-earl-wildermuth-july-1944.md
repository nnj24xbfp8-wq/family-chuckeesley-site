**Robert Earl Wildermuth, July 1944, age twenty** — standing in U.S. Army utility uniform in front of a clapboard-sided house. The caption affixed to the print reads:

> *July 1944*
>
> *Robert Earl Wildermuth, I (1924–1999)*

(The *"I"* designating him as Robert Earl Sr., to distinguish from his son and grandson who would also bear the name across the next two generations.)

This is **the earliest photograph of Robert Earl in this archive**, predating both his [WWII combat log *"The Big One"*](/docs/the-big-one/) and his [1948 Stanford BA portrait](/archive/robert-earl-wildermuth-stanford-ba-1948/). At twenty, he was either in the early period of his Army Air Forces training or shortly into it; the camera plane B-24 missions for which he is documented as navigator came in 1945, the year after this photo.

**The house behind him** is almost certainly the Wildermuth family home in **Marietta, Ohio** — his last address before shipping out for the Pacific. The photograph would have been taken either at his enlistment leave or during a training-base leave home. It is the photograph that introduces the Robert Earl whose B-24 navigator career, MGM camera-plane mission over Hiroshima and Nagasaki, 1948 Stanford BA on the GI Bill, lieutenant-colonel Pentagon years, retirement to Orlando, and [eighteen-year genealogical project](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/) would together constitute the documentary spine of the maternal side of this archive.

> *Source: Eesley/Wildermuth family archive; family-affixed caption.*
