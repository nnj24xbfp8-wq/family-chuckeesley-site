A color photograph of [Anne Eesley](/family/anne-eesley/) standing with her mother [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) on the steps of a grand classical public building in Madrid — Peggy visiting Anne during the years Anne was living in Spain. Cast and city confirmed by Chuck Eesley June 2026.

## The cast and setting

- **Older woman, white hair, dark-rimmed glasses, dark purse, navy slacks**: Peggy McMaster Eesley, in her late fifties to early sixties.
- **Younger woman, auburn hair, dark turtleneck under matching red trench coat, brown shoulder bag**: Anne Eesley, in her late twenties to early thirties.

The architecture is classical Madrid — large stone urn on a column above the steps, fluted columns and a portico behind, paving and dressed stone at street level — fitting one of central Madrid's major museums or government buildings (the Museo del Prado, the Real Academia de Bellas Artes, or a similar Paseo del Prado / central-Madrid landmark). The crowd around them — a man in tan coat at left, a man in dark suit at right with a small boy in red, a man in light shirt cropped at the bottom of the frame — is the regular Madrid street crowd of the period.

## What this frame records

**Peggy visiting Anne in Madrid.** Per Chuck's family memory, Anne lived in Madrid during the early-to-mid 1970s — one of the independent chapters her unmarried life made room for. Peggy travelled out to spend time with her there, almost certainly more than once. The matching red trench coats — Peggy's lighter, Anne's brighter — read as a mother-and-daughter trip-shopping decision: matching outerwear for a cool Madrid spring or autumn day.

Anne's Madrid years are the kind of biographical fact that turns the thin obituary line *"of Evart, Michigan"* into the life it sat inside: a Marietta-born Eesley girl who lived abroad in Spain in her twenties, came back through Charleston, ended in northern Michigan, kept her own counsel and her own surname through all of it. The Madrid chapter is the cosmopolitan piece her later geography did not advertise.

The date is c. **1972–1975** based on Peggy's apparent age and the trench-coat styling. Whether Will joined Peggy on the Madrid trip or stayed home with his architect's practice is open research; the photograph captures only the mother and the daughter.
