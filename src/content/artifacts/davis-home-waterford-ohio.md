The single surviving photograph of the Waterford, Ohio home where [Dorothy Marie Davis](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) was born on 24 February 1925 to [Homer Davis](/family/homer-davis/) and [Bessie Hill Davis](/family/bessie-hill-davis/). A one-and-a-half-story frame house with a covered porch fronting the road; a single mature tree, branches reaching almost the full width of the lot, dominates the foreground. Robert Earl's typed caption beneath the print reads simply: *"Home in Waterford, Washington County, Ohio where Dorothy Marie Davis was born."*

## What it documents

The Davis household lived in Waterford for the early years of the three sisters' childhoods — Mary (b. 1924), Dorothy (b. 1925), and Betty (b. 1926) — before moving a few miles south to Unionville, where the house caught fire and burned to the ground. The Waterford print is the only existing photograph in the archive of the *earlier* Davis address, before the fire. The whole [Unionville fire account](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) Robert Earl wrote into the 1990 Heritage sits a few pages later in the same document — the two are paired in the original layout as a small before-and-after of the Davis-side childhood.

The photograph is one of three Davis-side images Robert Earl chose to reproduce in the Heritage: the [birth certificate](/archive/dorothy-davis-birth-certificate-1925/), this house, and the [three sisters as small children](/archive/davis-sisters-three-mary-dorothy-betty-c-1929/) that comes next.
