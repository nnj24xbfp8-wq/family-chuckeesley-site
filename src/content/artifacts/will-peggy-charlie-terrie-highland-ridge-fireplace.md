The **four of them** &mdash; standing together against the Highland Ridge stone fireplace wall, the architectural centerpiece [Will Eesley](/family/wilbur-eesley/) had designed for the house he built in Lowell, Ohio. Chuck's **paternal grandparents and his parents in a single frame**, four months or four years away from his own birth.

## The cast, left to right

- **[Wilbur "Will" Eesley](/family/wilbur-eesley/)** &mdash; Chuck's paternal grandfather, the architect, in a white short-sleeve shirt with a striped tie hung loose at his open collar, plaid trousers, holding eyeglasses in his right hand. His characteristic small mid-century-modern eyeglasses hang from a temple. **Late sixties to early seventies.**
- **[Margaret "Peggy" McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/)** &mdash; Chuck's paternal grandmother, in a red-and-white floral print blouse and dark trousers, hair styled up gray. **Mid-to-late sixties.**
- **[Terrie Lee (Wildermuth) Eesley](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/)** &mdash; Chuck's mother, blonde feathered hair, in a navy short-sleeve polo and tan or khaki trousers. **Early-to-mid thirties.**
- **[Charles "Charlie" Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/)** &mdash; Chuck's father, in a pale-blue short-sleeve shirt and dark trousers, characteristic red-orange mustache and beard, dark belt, wristwatch. **Early thirties.**

## The fireplace and what it carries

The **stone fireplace wall behind them** is the [Highland Ridge house's defining interior feature](/places/highland-ridge-road/) &mdash; Will's architect's eye for natural stone, brought to the family room of the home he designed. A small wooden ledge supports a basket-and-broom folk-art arrangement at the right; a small carved figure (possibly Asian-inspired, fitting the Eesleys' Japan-and-Hong-Kong travel architectural taste) sits at the lower left.

## Dating — the late seventies to early eighties

The frame reads as **late 1970s to early 1980s** by clothing and hair: Will's plaid trousers, Charlie's bushy mustache and broad-collar polo, Terrie's blonde feathered cut, Peggy's printed blouse. Charlie was born 25 March 1948; Terrie was born 23 May 1946. They appear here as a couple in their early-to-mid thirties &mdash; **possibly before Chuck's December 1979 birth, possibly in the first few years after.** The c. 1978-1982 window is the broadest defensible dating; the specific occasion is open research.

What the photograph carries that the [c. 1985-1986 extended family gathering](/archive/eesley-family-gathering-c-1985-1986/) does not: **the four of them alone, as the core line.** Two generations of Eesley/Wildermuth marriage, framed against the stone wall Will built. The architectural ambition of Highland Ridge and the marriage that made Chuck arrive within a few years of this picture &mdash; together in one frame.
