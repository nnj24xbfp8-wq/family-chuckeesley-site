Jennie Eesley was one of [Albert Robert Eesley Sr](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/)'s daughters — **almost certainly named for her Scottish-emigrant mother Jennie Goldie**, in the standard 19th-century convention of naming a daughter for her mother. She married a man surnamed **Baxter** and lived in **Toronto** alongside her siblings Anna (Mrs. J. B. Scoon), Dr. George the physician, and Mrs. George Sinclair.

She is identified in [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/) as *"Mrs. Jennie Baxter ... of [Toronto]"* and in the **1899 family group portrait caption** as *"Jennie."*

She is **Chuck's great-great-aunt** on the paternal side.
