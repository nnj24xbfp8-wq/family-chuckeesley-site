Dorothy Hauser was a friend of [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) — Charlie Eesley's mother — and a member of *Mothers for Peace*, an organized American anti-Vietnam-War mothers' group active in the late 1960s and early 1970s. She is the likely recipient of [Charlie's 1 January 1971 letter from Da Nang](/docs/letters/charlie-to-dorothy-1971-01-01-mothers-for-peace/), whose closing line — *"Say hi to the Mothers for Peace for me. Charlie."* — anchors her affiliation with the group.

The letter sits in a wider Eesley-side anti-war network of the period that also included Peggy's [10 February 1971 congressional letter](/docs/letters/peggy-to-terrie-1971-02-10/) to Congressman Clarence Miller and [Don McMaster's bail-money offer](/docs/letters/charlie-to-terrie-1970-monsoon-5th-day-out/) for Terrie at the post-Kent-State Maryland campus. Dorothy Hauser's role in that network was the grassroots-organized women's-peace-movement piece.

Her dates, her home address, her years in the Mothers for Peace organization, and the texture of her friendship with Peggy are open research. The 1 January 1971 letter is the primary-source document this archive holds.

> *Source: Eesley family memory, transmitted by Chuck Eesley June 2026; [Charlie's 1 January 1971 letter](/docs/letters/charlie-to-dorothy-1971-01-01-mothers-for-peace/).*
