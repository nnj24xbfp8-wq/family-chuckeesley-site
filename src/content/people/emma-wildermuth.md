Born **3 October 1893 in Marietta, Ohio**, the **youngest** of [William Clifford Wildermuth](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)'s three known children. Visible in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** as **the "Small Girl in Rear"** — the typed caption singles her out specifically — held high on a young woman's shoulder, about three years old.

## The Marietta life

Of the three William-Clifford-and-Flora children, **Emma is the one who stayed in Marietta her whole life**. She was born there, lived there, and died there. She died **10 December 1958** at age **65** — outliving both her parents (Flora d. 1919, William Clifford d. 1943) and both her siblings (Charles Daniel d. 1945 Kansas, Margaret Zelma d. 1943 Los Angeles) by years. By the time of her death she was the **last surviving child of William Clifford and Flora** by more than thirteen years.

Robert Earl Wildermuth — born **1924 in Marietta**, two miles from where Emma was living — would have known her as a **Marietta-resident older cousin** through his Wildermuth-side childhood. She was an adult woman in her thirties when he was a small boy at 413 Harmar Street, a woman in her late fifties when he came back from Stanford as a married man with daughters, and **she died the year Robert Earl was on a B-47 at McDill in Tampa**. Whether they kept up the connection across the war and post-war years would be the kind of detail Robert Earl's surviving Marietta relatives might still know.

Her marriage (FAMS @F119@ in the GEDCOM) and any children are queued research.
