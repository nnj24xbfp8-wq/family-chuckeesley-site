Martha Kelley was born **1844 in Barbour County, Virginia** &mdash; the central-Virginia mountain county that would become West Virginia in 1863. Her father [Johnson B. Kelley](/family/johnson-kelley/) (b. 25 July 1810, Randolph County, Virginia) and her grandfather [Ebenezer Kelley](/family/ebenezer-kelley/) (b. 2 October 1763, Somerset County, New Jersey) place her family in the New-Jersey-to-Appalachian-Virginia migration that the late-18th-century Scotch-Irish-and-other-Ulster stream produced.

She married [Seth Grosvenor Dunbar](/family/seth-grosvenor-dunbar/) of Kennebec County, Maine in the late 1860s. Their daughter [Verona Belle Dunbar](/family/verona-sheppard-fleming/) was born 11 June 1869 in Wood County, West Virginia. **Seth died 26 October 1870**, sixteen months after Verona's birth.

Martha was twenty-six and widowed with an infant daughter. Her line continued; the Kelley great-grandmother surname runs forward to Verona as a middle name carried through the rest of her life (*"Verona Belle Kelley"* in Robert Earl's 1989 lineage chart).

## The deeper Kelley line — back to County Wicklow

The GEDCOM carries the Kelley line through three more generations to **John Patrick Kelley** (b. 2 December 1725, Wicklow, County Wicklow, Ireland) &mdash; the Irish-born great-great-great-grandfather of Verona. Earlier still, **John Aaron Kelley** (b. 1702, Kildare, County Kildare, Ireland) sits as the deepest documented Kelley ancestor. The Kelley-Dunbar-Sheppard-Fleming chain that runs to Chuck through Sadye carries Irish, Maine-New England, and the broader Scotch-Irish Appalachian streams in one line.
