**Faye Angel was Chuck's godmother.** She and her husband [Ted Angel](/family/ted-angel/) ran a **picture-framing business** out of their Marietta house — **their basement was filled with thousands of picture frames**, stacked and sorted and waiting for customers' orders. The Angel home was one of the small-town family-friend houses Chuck's childhood ran through, alongside [Aunt Betty and Uncle Norm Wolfe's house at 108 Hillcrest Drive](/family/betty-davis-wolfe/).

## Hosting young Terrie on her college visits

The Angels' role in the family started before Chuck was born. **Faye hosted [Terrie Lee Wildermuth](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) on her visits to Charlie during their college years** — the late 1960s through the early 1970s, when Charlie was a Marietta College senior and then a Vietnam-tour-and-after, and Terrie was a University of Maryland student traveling out to Marietta to see him. The detail is visible in the [Vietnam letters](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) and other pre-marriage Charlie-and-Terrie correspondence: Faye and Ted Angel's house was a **trusted Marietta-side hosting household** for Charlie's then-girlfriend when she was in town. A young woman from a Maryland college visiting her boyfriend in his hometown needed somewhere to stay; the Angel home was where she stayed.

That role &mdash; hosting Terrie before she was a wife or mother &mdash; is one piece of why Faye became Chuck's godmother when Chuck was born in 1979.

## The sick-day caregiver

Faye Angel and Aunt Betty Wolfe were the **two sick-day caregivers of Chuck's childhood**. When Chuck had to stay home from school, he was often at Faye and Ted's house or at the Wolfes' on Hillcrest. The two households together formed the small-town extended-family-care network around him.

The Angel home for a sick child meant: **the picture-framing business going on around him**, the basement full of frames, Ted working in his shop, Faye watching after him, the soup or the tea or the day-on-the-couch that sick days always meant. Whatever the specific texture of those days, Faye Angel was the woman whose hands Chuck's childhood passed through on his bad-health mornings, the same way she had hosted his mother on her courtship visits a decade before.

## The genealogical connection — through Becky and the Davis line

The earlier framing on this page treated Faye as *"a close family friend"* with an open question about whether her family connected to any documented family lines. **The c. 1992 [Davis-Angel family portrait](/archive/davis-angel-family-portrait-1992/)** surfaced in this archive in 2026 closes that question concretely:

**Faye and Ted Angel had a daughter, [Rebecca "Becky" Lynne (Angel) Davis](/family/rebecca-angel-davis/), who married William Harvey Davis Jr.** &mdash; **the son of William Harvey Davis Sr., Betty and Dorothy Davis's younger brother**.

**Through Becky's marriage, the Angel and Davis families are in-laws.** The chain runs:

> Chuck's grandmother [Dorothy Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) &rarr; her younger brother William Harvey Davis Sr. &rarr; his son William Harvey Jr. &rarr; William Jr.'s wife [Becky Angel Davis](/family/rebecca-angel-davis/) &rarr; Becky's mother **Faye Angel** &rarr; **Chuck's godmother**.

Faye being Chuck's godmother is therefore not random closeness &mdash; it is the natural pick of a **mother-in-law-of-the-cousin-by-blood** to be the spiritual sponsor at the next-generation's baptism. The picture-framing-house childcare and the holiday-table closeness of the Angel home to Chuck's family are the lived expressions of a **family-by-marriage** relationship that the structured genealogical record had not yet picked up.
