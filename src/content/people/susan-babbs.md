Susan Babbs married John Eesley at Charlecote in May 1819 and almost certainly never left the Warwickshire–Oxfordshire borderland; the 1841 and 1851 censuses find her in Old Stratford with what was, by Victorian standards, a large surviving brood. Her maiden surname is sometimes written **Babbs** in marriage records and (mistakenly) as **Bubb** in the family memory — the [Oxford library research notes](/docs/eesley-line-research-notes/) settle the spelling as Babbs.

Two of her sons crossed the Atlantic into the milling trade: **Albert Robert Eesley** went to Scotland to work in the Goldie flour mill, and **John F. Eesley** went to America, eventually founding the Sunshine Flour Mill (J. F. Eesley Milling Co.) in Plainwell, Michigan.

Her parents and earlier life are not yet documented in this archive.
