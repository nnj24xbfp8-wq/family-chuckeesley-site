The Bean register called her **Hannah Bubb**. The Oxford library volunteer, working a generation later, wrote her down as **Susan Babbs**. Dale's FamilySearch tree, working from the parish registers themselves, settles both readings: she was **Susannah Bubb**, born 4 December 1796 in Charlecote, Warwickshire — and the proof is that one of her sons was named **George *Bubb* Eesley**, carrying his mother's maiden name as his middle name in the old custom.

She married [John Eesley](/family/john-eesley-1800/) at Charlecote on 10 May 1819 and bore him eleven children: Hannah (1819), Mary Polly (1821), Eydth (~1824), Emma (1828, who died the same year), Evess (1830), William (1834), George Bubb (1836), **Albert Robert (1837)** — the emigrant whose son Charles Leonard would father Will — John (d. 1874), Jane (1841), and Henry (1842). The 1851 census found her at fifty, still working, at Rother Street in Old Stratford.

The conflation in Bean's register comes from her daughter Hannah Eesley (b. December 1819) — also a Hannah, also a Bubb-descendant by name. The line carried two Hannahs in adjacent generations; one survived in family memory as the wife of John, when she was really his daughter.
