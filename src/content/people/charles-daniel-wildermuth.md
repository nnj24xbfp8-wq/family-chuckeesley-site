Born **4 February 1889 in Marietta, Ohio**, the **eldest son** of [William Clifford Wildermuth](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/) — and **second-eldest of their nine children** (after Mae Clara, b. 1886). Visible in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** at about age seven — most likely the small boy in dark clothes in the front row. **Older brother of [Earl Adam](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/)** (b. 1899) — making Charles Daniel **Robert Earl Wildermuth's paternal uncle**.

## The Kansas move

Of the three William-Clifford-and-Flora children, **Charles is the one who left Ohio for the West**. He died **10 October 1945 in Kansas** at age **56** — just weeks after the Japanese surrender that ended WWII — and was buried at **Hoisington Cemetery in Hoisington, Barton County, Kansas**. **Hoisington** is a small farming town in central Kansas, the kind of place a Marietta-born midwesterner could have ended up after working a railroad or wheat-belt career.

Per the GEDCOM, his marriage record shows him with **Eva "Effie" Barbara Schmidt** — a German-surname spouse continuing the Schlicher-and-Wildermuth tradition of German-American family lines. Children and the date and circumstances of the Kansas move are queued research; the GEDCOM gives the bare frame.
