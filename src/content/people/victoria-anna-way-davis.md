Victoria Anna Way was born **27 January 1874** at **Crooked Tree, Jackson Township, Noble County, Ohio** — one of the nine children of [Edward Taylor Way](/family/edward-taylor-way/) (1812-1879) and his second wife [Ann Ellison Way](/family/ann-ellison-way/). She **died at age 29 on 26 June 1903 in Ohio**, and was buried at **Crooked Tree Cemetery**, Noble County, beside her parents and siblings.

She is **Chuck's maternal great-great-grandmother** through the Davis line: Victoria → Homer Edward Davis (her son, b. 1900) → Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth → Terrie Lee → Chuck.

## The Way family connection enters the Davis line through her

Victoria's birth surname **Way** is the documentary connection back to the **Edward Taylor Way family of Noble County** that the newspaper clipping ["Way family among early settlers in Noble County"](/docs/way-family-noble-county-clipping/) — preserved by Chuck in the family papers, with handwritten margin annotations by an earlier-generation family genealogist — describes in full. The clipping records Edward T. Way's nine children with his second wife Ann Ellison: *Elizabeth, David R., Mary A., Abagail, Edward E., Henry, Sarah E., Emily J. and William M.* Victoria Anna Way (born 1874) is part of that same Way-Ellison line — a generation younger than the children the 1887 *Watkins's History of Noble County* enumerates — and is most plausibly the daughter of one of those nine children or a near relation.

(The handwritten note on the clipping — *"Elizabeth, the oldest, was married twice. Her first husband was Lindley Yarnall"* — annotates the Way-side oldest daughter. Victoria, born in 1874, was either a younger sister of that Elizabeth or a niece in the next generation. The exact branch-and-parent for Victoria within Edward T. Way's posterity is open research; the Way-side family papers Chuck preserves should settle it.)

## Death at 29 — Homer was three

Victoria died **26 June 1903**, **less than three and a half years after Homer was born** (8 February 1900). Homer was a toddler losing his mother. The c. 1902 family portrait that survives — of William, Victoria, and Homer together, the three of them in a tight studio close-up, Homer about two years old in a salmon-colored Edwardian-era child's dress — is **one of the last photographs taken of her**, possibly the only family portrait of the three together.

![Victoria Anna Way Davis (left) with her son Homer Edward Davis (center, age ~2) and her husband William Armstrong Davis (right), c. 1902 — within a year of her death at age 29.](../../assets/family/originals/william-victoria-homer-davis-c1902-portrait.jpeg)

After her death, William Armstrong Davis remarried (the GEDCOM records a second family, F45). Homer grew up in the second household but his earliest years were spent with the mother who appears in this frame.
