Victoria Anna Way was born **27 January 1874** at **Crooked Tree, Jackson Township, Noble County, Ohio** &mdash; the eldest of the ten children of [Edward E. Way](/family/edward-e-way/) (1851-1924) and [Tacy Elizabeth Matthews](/family/tacy-elizabeth-matthews/) (1848-1902). She **died at age 29 on 26 June 1903 in Ohio**, and was buried at **Crooked Tree Cemetery**, Noble County, beside her parents and siblings.

She is **Chuck's maternal great-great-grandmother** through the Davis line: Victoria &rarr; Homer Edward Davis (her son, b. 1900) &rarr; Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth &rarr; Terrie Lee &rarr; Chuck.

## The Way family connection enters the Davis line through her

Victoria's birth surname **Way** is the documentary connection back to the **English-immigrant Way family of Noble County**. Her father Edward E. Way was the Noble County-born son of [Edward Taylor Way](/family/edward-taylor-way/) (1812-1879, of the Isle of Wight, England) and [Anna Ellison](/family/anna-ellison/) (b. 1821, Coppull, Lancashire, England) &mdash; the two English-immigrant founders of the Way family in Ohio. The newspaper clipping ["Way family among early settlers in Noble County"](/docs/way-family-noble-county-clipping/) &mdash; preserved by Chuck in the family papers, with handwritten margin annotations by an earlier-generation family genealogist &mdash; records Edward T. Way's nine children with Anna Ellison: *Elizabeth, David R., Mary A., Abagail, Edward E., Henry, Sarah E., Emily J. and William M.* Victoria's father Edward E. Way is the *Edward E.* in that list. The clipping is therefore the biographical record of **Victoria's grandparents**.

(The handwritten note on the clipping &mdash; *"Elizabeth, the oldest, was married twice. Her first husband was Lindley Yarnall"* &mdash; annotates Victoria's aunt Elizabeth Way, the eldest of the nine Way-Ellison children. Victoria, the eldest grandchild of that 1845-marriage line, was born in 1874.)

## The 1901 wedding portrait — Victoria with her parents and her son

The [Belle Way and Howard Wickens wedding portrait](/family/belsora-way-wickens/) of **25 September 1901** is the only known photograph that gathers Victoria with her parents Edward E. Way (figure #8, *"great-grandfather"* from Dorothy's perspective) and Tacy Matthews (figure #7, *"great-grandmother"*), her husband William A. Davis (figure #5, *"grandfather"*), her son Homer at twenty months old (figure #11, *"father"*), her younger sister Belle Way as bride (figure #3), and a half-dozen other Way-side relatives. **Victoria herself is figure #6, labeled "Anna Way Davis (grandmother)"** from her granddaughter Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth's perspective. The portrait is **the only known photograph of Victoria with her parents** &mdash; and one of the last photographs taken before her 1903 death.

## Death at 29 — Homer was three

Victoria died **26 June 1903**, **less than three and a half years after Homer was born** (8 February 1900). Homer was a toddler losing his mother. The c. 1902 family portrait that survives — of William, Victoria, and Homer together, the three of them in a tight studio close-up, Homer about two years old in a salmon-colored Edwardian-era child's dress — is **one of the last photographs taken of her**, possibly the only family portrait of the three together.

![Victoria Anna Way Davis (left) with her son Homer Edward Davis (center, age ~2) and her husband William Armstrong Davis (right), c. 1902 — within a year of her death at age 29.](../../assets/family/originals/william-victoria-homer-davis-c1902-portrait.jpeg)

After her death, William Armstrong Davis remarried (the GEDCOM records a second family, F45). Homer grew up in the second household but his earliest years were spent with the mother who appears in this frame.
