Joseph Hill Chenoweth was born **30 December 1832 in Pleasant Township, Franklin County, Ohio** &mdash; the third child and third son of [Elijah Chenoweth, Jr.](/family/elijah-chenoweth/) and Nancy Chenoweth, in the same Pleasant Township just south of Columbus where his daughters Lillie Dale (1877) and Scioto Mafry (1871) would also be born. He married **[Mary O. Timmons](/family/mary-ohio-timmons-chenoweth/) in late 1864** in Franklin County (this archive records 25 November 1864; Jon Egge's structured record gives the date as 1 December 1864 &mdash; the two are close enough to plausibly be the marriage license vs. ceremony dates, but the question is open). Together they were the parents of at least five children: [Lillie Dale](/family/lillie-dale-chenoweth/) (the Chenoweth matriarch who married into the Eesley line), [Scioto Mafry](/family/scioto-mafry-chenoweth/) (the medical pioneer of family memory), Howard Glen, Elsie, and Dwight Kennedy.

## Civil War service — Union

Egge's structured record names Joseph Hill as a **Union veteran of the American Civil War** &mdash; the *Military: Civil War: Union* field on his page. He was 28 when the war started in April 1861 and 32 when it ended in April 1865; his marriage to Mary Ohio Timmons in late 1864 falls in the final winter of the war. He was one of three of Elijah Jr.'s surviving sons to serve on the Union side &mdash; alongside his older brothers Thomas (1828–1908) and John Wesley (1831–1889). Specific regiment, dates of enlistment and discharge, and any wartime correspondence are open research — the structured field confirms the service without the detail. A pass against the Ohio Civil War rosters or the FamilySearch military records collection could fill the dates in.

## Later life and burial

He died in **June 1910**, age 77, and is buried at **Otterbein Cemetery in Blendon Township, Franklin County, Ohio** (the cemetery is in Blendon Township; Westerville sits inside the same township). The same cemetery would hold his daughter Scioto twenty years later, and his wife Mary &mdash; who outlived him by nine years (d. 2 May 1919 at Grove City, per Egge) &mdash; is also buried there.

He is **Chuck's great-great-grandfather** along the path that runs through his daughter Lillie Dale to Will Eesley and onward.

The only image of him in the family archive is a formal portrait dating roughly **1865&ndash;1870**, scanned from the Chenoweth family album his great-granddaughter [Roberta Burnes](/family/roberta-burnes/) inherited through the Burnes-Eesley line. The scan reached Aunt Maggie Eesley's *Four Generations* deck from that album. The resolution, in Maggie's note, is "barely good enough to enlarge image" &mdash; he is at the edge of what photographic preservation reaches for this generation. Roberta has also noted that the album holds a photograph of **his father [Elijah Chenoweth](/family/elijah-chenoweth/)** &mdash; queued for the next round of digitization.
