William Clifford Wildermuth was born **17 September 1866 in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio** — the same river-town his great-grandfather [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) had reached as an immigrant shoemaker from Grossaspach, Württemberg, in 1847 — and he stayed there for all seventy-seven years of his life, dying in Marietta on **10 September 1943** and being buried two days later. The Marietta-Ohio Wildermuths he represents are the cluster that produced [Earl Adam](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) and [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) in the next two generations — Robert Earl's [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) opens with a Marietta birth at 123 Franklin Street and the Marietta dream house at 413 Harmar Street, the same town William Clifford lived his whole life in.

## The marriage to Flora Schlicher

He married **[Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)** (b. 24 August 1870 Marietta, d. 18 November 1919 Marietta) — a Marietta-born daughter of the local **Schlicher** family, the same surname Robert Earl would still be trying to research in his 1989 memoir's closing pages:

> *"I hope to return to the home towns of grandma Catherina Boeshar and hopefully that of Johann Peter Schlicher at some later date."*

Flora's father in Dale Eesley's tree is **Theobald "David" Schlicher**, the Schlicher patriarch Robert Earl was almost certainly trying to trace back. The marriage of William Clifford and Flora joined two of the earlier German-immigrant Marietta families into the single household visible in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** in this archive.

## Three children

William Clifford and Flora had at least **three children** documented in the GEDCOM, all born in Marietta:

- **[Charles Daniel Wildermuth](/family/charles-daniel-wildermuth/)** (4 February 1889 – 10 October 1945) — born Marietta, died Kansas; buried Hoisington Cemetery, Hoisington, Barton County, Kansas. The one of the three children who left Ohio for the West.
- **[Margaret Zelma Wildermuth](/family/margaret-zelma-wildermuth/)** (6 November 1891 – 25 October 1943) — born Marietta, died Los Angeles, buried Forest Lawn Memorial Park, Long Beach. The one who reached California.
- **[Emma Wildermuth](/family/emma-wildermuth/)** (3 October 1893 – 10 December 1958) — born Marietta and died Marietta. The one who stayed.

The three children's ages at the 1896 family portrait — seven, five, and three — match what's visible in the photograph (a small boy, a slightly smaller girl seated front, and a toddler held high in the back row).

## Relationship to Robert Earl Wildermuth's line

William Clifford was **about the same generation as Robert Earl Wildermuth's grandfather**. He was born 1866; Robert Earl's father [Earl Adam](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) was born a generation later; Robert Earl himself in 1924. The most parsimonious reading is that **William Clifford was a brother of Robert Earl's grandfather John Wildermuth**, making William Clifford **Robert Earl's great-uncle** and **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the maternal Wildermuth side. Reconciliation against Dale's tree for the precise Wildermuth sibling-of-John relationship is queued.

## Dates settled

His **1943 death date** (just six days short of his 77th birthday) places him in Marietta in the same year as his daughter Margaret Zelma's death in Los Angeles. Whether their deaths in the same year were related, or coincidence, is the kind of detail the Marietta Times obituary archives could probably settle.

He appears as the **far-left figure** in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** — older man with substantial mustache, dark suit, hands at his sides — visibly the patriarch of the household at age thirty.
