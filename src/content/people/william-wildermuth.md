William Wildermuth is identified by Chuck Eesley in 2026 as the **central figure in the c. 1900s [William Wildermuth family portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-portrait/)** &mdash; a formal seated patriarch with his wife standing at his right shoulder and two boys (their sons, presumably) flanking them.

His exact relationship to [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/)'s documented Wildermuth line is not yet established in this archive. The Robert Earl line runs:

> [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) (1847 emigrant from Grossaspach, settled in Marietta as a shoemaker) &rarr; *intermediate* &rarr; **John Wildermuth** (Robert Earl's grandfather) &rarr; **[Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/)** &rarr; **[Robert Earl](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/)**.

William is most plausibly:
- **A son of Johann Michael Wildermuth** (other than John), making him **Robert Earl's great-uncle** and **Chuck's great-great-uncle**, or
- **A son of John Wildermuth** (other than Earl Adam), making him **Robert Earl's uncle** and **Chuck's great-uncle**.

The c. 1900s photograph dates William's prime to roughly the same period as Robert Earl's grandparents' generation, which is most consistent with the great-uncle reading. Reconciliation queued against the Marietta Wildermuth records and the LDS International Genealogical Index that Robert Earl consulted in the 1980s.

Page is a stub. Dates, wife's name, sons' names, and the relationship to Robert Earl's line pending family memory or research.
