Maria Margaretha Schmidgall was born **25 October 1789** in **Röhrach** (the small Württemberg village near Aspach, in what is today the Rems-Murr-Kreis of Baden-Württemberg). Her father [Georg Wilhelm Schmidgall](/family/georg-wilhelm-schmidgall/) was a Röhrach farmer; her mother [Anna Catherina Landai](/family/anna-catherina-landai/) of the same village.

She married [Johann Christian Wildermuth](/family/johann-christian-wildermuth/) of Großaspach on 25 April 1842 in Ristenau &mdash; a late-life marriage for both of them. Johann Christian died six months later on 7 October 1842. Maria Margaretha outlived him by seven years, dying 30 December 1849 (Robert Earl's [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-little-histories/) misprints the year as 1947 &mdash; an obvious typographical error; the GEDCOM has 1849).

The "Little Histories" sketch is short and bare-fact &mdash; Robert Earl's typical entry for ancestors three or four generations behind the emigrant. The Röhrach farming-village origin is the deepest Württemberg place-name in the maternal-Wildermuth half of the line that does not retrace to Rielingshausen or Großaspach themselves.
