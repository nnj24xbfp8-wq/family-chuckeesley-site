## A parallel Wildermuth branch

This Jacob Wildermuth and his wife Elizabeth lead a family that **does not appear in Dale Eesley's FamilySearch tree**. None of their nine documented children's given names — **Charles E, John "Leb", Harry J, Jesse W, Maude B, Lucy, Zella M, "Lanessa" Louise, or Troy B** — match anyone in the structured record of Robert Earl Wildermuth's Marietta-Ohio Wildermuth line.

Three readings are plausible:

1. **A genuinely separate Wildermuth-surname family** of German-American immigrants, parallel to Robert Earl's [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) branch but not descended from him. The Wildermuth surname appears in multiple Württemberg villages of the 17th–19th centuries; multiple emigration waves brought separate Wildermuth families to America. Jacob's branch may be one of those.

2. **A more distant cousin branch** — possibly descending from a Württemberg Wildermuth from a different village (Großaspach was Johann Michael's; Rielingshausen produced many earlier-documented Wildermuths) and emigrating separately. The common ancestor would lie deep enough that Dale's research hasn't yet bridged the two trees.

3. **A photo Robert Earl collected during his 1971–1989 genealogy research** — exchanged with a fellow Wildermuth-surname genealogist, retained without a documented family-tree connection, and now part of the Wildermuth archive image set without being part of the Wildermuth direct lineage.

## The c. 1885–1895 family group portrait

The photograph is a **formal sepia cabinet-card studio portrait** showing eleven figures:

- **Seated front center**: **Jacob Wildermuth**, with a substantial mustache, in a three-piece dark suit with a watch chain visible, hands relaxed.
- **Seated front center right**: **Elizabeth Wildermuth**, in a long dark high-collared dress with a fitted bodice, her hair pulled back, hands folded in her lap.
- **Between Jacob and Elizabeth at the front**: **Troy B Wildermuth** — a small boy of about 4–6, in a dark child's dress with a large bow tie at the neck (children's clothing of the 1880s–90s included unisex dark dresses for very young children).
- **Back row, men, left to right**: **Charles E**, **John "Leb"** (the second-eldest looking man; "Leb" presumably short for Lebrecht or a similar German given name carried into English use), **Harry J**, **Jesse W**.
- **Middle row, women, left to right**: **Maude B**, **Lucy**, **Zella M**, **"Lanessa" Louise** (the eldest-looking woman at right edge, wearing a striking checked/plaid skirt).

The composition is the canonical late-Victorian American family portrait of a large household: **parents seated with the youngest child between them; the older children arranged by gender and age behind**.

## The dating

The clothing style — the women's high-collared dark dresses with fitted bodices and the men's narrow lapels and bow ties — points to **c. 1885–1895**. If Jacob is in his late 40s to early 50s in the photograph, he would have been born **c. 1835–1845**, putting him in the same generation as [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) (b. 1830) — a contemporary Wildermuth, not a descendant.

## Open research questions

- **Where did the photograph come from?** If Robert Earl's 1989 memoir and 1990 Heritage acknowledge an exchange with another Wildermuth-surname family, the connection might be traceable through his research correspondence.
- **Are Jacob and Elizabeth Wildermuth in any U.S. Wildermuth census records?** A late-19th-century Jacob Wildermuth with eight named children in **Pennsylvania, Ohio, West Virginia, or another midwestern state** would be searchable through Ancestry.com census records.
- **Does the Württemberg side of Dale's tree, deepening backward, eventually connect Jacob to Robert Earl through a common 18th-century ancestor?** Possible — the Rielingshausen and Großaspach Wildermuths intermarried over generations, and a Jacob Wildermuth surname-bearer of the right age would likely fall within the broader Württemberg family network.
