Edward Taylor Way is **Chuck's maternal four-times-great-grandfather** on the Way line and the **English-immigrant founder** of the Way family branch that brings the Way surname into the Davis line through his grandson [Edward E. Way](/family/edward-e-way/) and great-granddaughter [Victoria Anna Way Davis](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/).

## From the Isle of Wight to Noble County

Edward Taylor Way was born **11 September 1812 on the Isle of Wight, England** &mdash; a thirty-mile-wide chalk-down island off the south coast of Hampshire, four miles across the Solent from Portsmouth. He emigrated at some point before 1845 to Morgan County, Ohio (the county directly west of Noble County), where on **27 January 1845** he married **[Anna Ellison](/family/anna-ellison/)** (b. 28 June 1821, christened 22 July 1821 at Coppull, Lancashire, England), another English-born young immigrant. She was eight years his junior.

The couple settled in Noble County, Ohio &mdash; specifically in the Crooked Tree / Jackson Township area &mdash; and raised at least nine documented children. The eldest known, **[Edward E. Way](/family/edward-e-way/)** (b. 14 May 1851), is Chuck's direct ancestor through Victoria Anna Way Davis &rarr; Homer Davis &rarr; Dorothy Davis Wildermuth &rarr; Terrie Lee &rarr; Chuck.

## The Way family newspaper biography

The clipping ["Way family among early settlers in Noble County"](/docs/way-family-noble-county-clipping/) &mdash; preserved in Chuck's family papers with handwritten margin annotations by an earlier-generation family genealogist &mdash; is the substantive biographical record of Edward Taylor Way and his family. It enumerates his children as *Elizabeth, David R., Mary A., Abagail, Edward E., Henry, Sarah E., Emily J. and William M.*, and carries the genealogical note:

> *"Elizabeth, the oldest, was married twice. Her first husband was Lindley Yarnall."*

The 1887 *Watkins's History of Noble County* records the Way family as part of the early English-immigrant farming generation that broke ground in southeastern Ohio in the 1840s and 1850s. Edward Taylor Way is the **founding generation of the Way family in Ohio**.

## Death and the Way-name line going forward

Edward Taylor Way died **in 1879 at Noble County, Ohio**, age 66 or 67. By that date his son Edward E. Way had been married to [Tacy Elizabeth Matthews](/family/tacy-elizabeth-matthews/) for seven years (since 1872), and his granddaughter [Victoria Anna Way](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/) had been born (1874) and was five years old. Edward Taylor lived to know the granddaughter through whom the Way name would enter Chuck's direct maternal Davis line, even if she would not survive to know her own son Homer past the age of three.

The Way connection runs from this man &mdash; English emigrant, Isle of Wight to Noble County, Ohio &mdash; **forward in time five generations** to Chuck.
