Helen Louise Eesley was born **January 16, 1924** in Columbus, Ohio — the youngest of Charles Leonard and Lillie Dale Chenoweth Eesley's eight children &mdash; **delivered at home by her aunt [Dr. Scioto Mafry "Aunt Ota" Chenoweth Smith](/family/scioto-mafry-chenoweth/)**, her mother Lillie Dale's older sister and the family's physician. Ota's birthday-delivery for Helen is the earliest documented instance of Aunt Ota's medical practice working *within* the Eesley household, and it began the six-year relationship between Helen and her aunt that would later produce the family's most striking medical-deathbed story (see *[Scioto Mafry Chenoweth Smith](/family/scioto-mafry-chenoweth/)*'s page). She married **Edwin William Burnes** in 1946 and lived in Worthington, Ohio.

She and Edwin had at least seven children — a son Brian and six daughters. Four of the six daughters kept the Burnes name through adulthood:

- **Brian Carlson Burnes** (b. March 6, 1948).
- **[Barbara "Barb" Lynn Burnes Hester](/family/barb-burnes-hester/)** (b. September 13, 1950), the oldest of the daughters; married Phillip Hester (took his name). Barb made photocopies of all of Grandpa Charles Leonard's 1940s letters; the photocopied set is the most likely entry point for any future deciphering pass through that collection.
- **[Elizabeth "Liz" Burnes](/family/liz-burnes/)** (b. May 15, 1955); married Donald Faux but kept her own surname.
- **[Cindy Burnes](/family/cindy-burnes/)**; kept her own surname.
- **[Shelley Burnes](/family/shelley-burnes/)**; kept her own surname.
- **[Roberta Marie Burnes](/family/roberta-burnes/)** (b. August 6, 1963); married John Walker but kept her own surname. The keeper of the 1940s Eesley letter collection in its original form and one of the four paternal-side keepers credited on this archive's [homepage](/).
- **[Katherine "Katie" Burnes McKeel](/family/katie-burnes/)** (b. February 15, 1965); married into the McKeel family.

Four of Helen and Edwin's six daughters kept the Burnes surname when they married — Liz, Cindy, Shelley, and Roberta — and two (Barb and Katie) took their husbands' names. The pattern is itself a small piece of mid-twentieth-century-into-modern women's-naming history in this family.

Helen and Mary Bean were the two sisters who survived Will in 1986.

## Helen as the family's Aunt Ota carrier

Helen had **six years and twenty-four days** with her aunt Ota &mdash; from her 16 January 1924 birth (which Ota delivered) to Ota's 9 February 1930 death at the Bexley house on Cassidy Avenue (which Helen, age six, was present for). Across those six years Helen also watched, as a curious four- or five-year-old peeking through an upstairs bedroom door, her aunt give her final anatomy lesson to Helen's older brother [Don Eesley](/family/don-eesley/) &mdash; Aunt Ota unwrapping her radiation-burned abdomen as the textbook for Don's possible medical career. *Helen never forgot.* She told the stories often to her daughters across the rest of her life, and Roberta &mdash; in a June 2026 email to Chuck &mdash; passed them on to this archive. The full set of Aunt Ota stories is now on [Scioto Mafry Chenoweth Smith's page](/family/scioto-mafry-chenoweth/); the chain of family memory ran from Aunt Ota to Helen to Roberta to here.

Helen was also her sister Aunt Jean's eldest-surviving sibling-witness &mdash; though Jean's October 1925 death (when Helen was 21 months old) is not something Helen could have remembered firsthand. The Jean-car-crash story Helen passed down came from her mother Lillie Dale, who told her daughter the story of the older sister Helen had barely known.

> *Sources: Mary Eesley Bean, *[Eesley Family History](/docs/eesley-family-history-1985/)*, p. 8; Roberta Burnes, email June 2026 (Aunt Ota delivered Helen, the anatomy-lesson story, the Cassidy Avenue death scene, the Jean-car-crash story).*
