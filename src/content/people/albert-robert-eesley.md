Albert Robert Eesley was born **25 December 1837** &mdash; Christmas Day &mdash; in Stratford-on-Avon, the eighth of [John Eesley](/family/john-eesley-1800/) the journeyman miller and [Susannah Bubb](/family/susan-babbs/)'s eleven children. He went to **Canada**, not Scotland, to work in the milling operation of the Scottish-Canadian Goldie family. The older brief framing &mdash; "Albert came to Scotland to work in the Goldie flour mill" &mdash; was wrong on the geography; the Goldies had themselves emigrated from Ayrshire to Ontario in 1841, sixteen years before he reached them.

He married **[Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/)** in Ontario in 1861. Together they had ten children. Two of those children would matter most for what reaches the present:

- **[John Franklin Eesley](/family/john-f-eesley/)** (b. 11 Dec 1859 Hamilton, Ontario; d. 1929 Plainwell, MI) &mdash; founder of the Sunshine Flour Mill in Plainwell, the building now on the National Register of Historic Places.
- **[Charles Leonard Eesley](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/)** (1879&ndash;1972) &mdash; husband of Lillie Dale Chenoweth, father of Will, the bridge of the line into the 20th-century American Eesleys.

The cover of [Bean's 1985 family history](/docs/eesley-family-history-1985/), drawn by Will Eesley, shows the Goldie mill &mdash; the architectural witness of where Albert's working life was made, on the Canadian rather than the Scottish side of the Atlantic.

He died **5 April 1902**, age sixty-four.
