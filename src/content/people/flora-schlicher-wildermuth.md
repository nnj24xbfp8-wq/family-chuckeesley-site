Flora Schlicher was born **24 August 1870 in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio**, to the local **Schlicher family** of Marietta — a German-immigrant family of the same generation as the [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) line her future husband descended from. Her father was **Theobald "David" Schlicher**, the Schlicher patriarch.

## Why the Schlicher name matters in this archive

In 1989, Chuck's grandfather [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) closed his [memoir's German-trip travelogue](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) with a line about the family research he still hoped to complete:

> *"I hope to return to the home towns of grandma Catherina Boeshar and hopefully that of Johann Peter Schlicher at some later date."*

The **Schlicher** of that sentence is Flora's family line. Robert Earl was still trying to find **Johann Peter Schlicher's German hometown** when the memoir was written. The Schlicher emigration from Germany is part of the same mid-19th-century wave that brought Johann Michael Wildermuth to Marietta — both families settled in Washington County, Ohio, and merged through Flora and William Clifford's marriage.

## The 1896 family portrait

Flora is the **seated woman in the light dress at front-row center** in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** — about 26 at the time, photographed with her young children at her side in the canonical Victorian young-mother family-portrait pose. Her husband [William Clifford](/family/william-wildermuth/) stands at the back-left of the group, her mother-in-law [Catharina Boeshar](/family/catharina-boeshar-wildermuth/) is at the back holding her youngest daughter Emma high on her shoulder, and her sister [Emma Schlicher](/family/emma-schlicher/) stands at the back-right with William's younger brother Edward Frederick. The portrait is the earliest dated Wildermuth-side photograph in this archive.

## The 1919 death

She died **18 November 1919** in Marietta at age **49** — a death well before her time by modern standards, twenty-four years before her husband would die in 1943. Cause of death is open in the GEDCOM; the late-1919 timing is consistent with the **1918–1919 influenza pandemic**'s final wave, which continued to claim American victims into late 1919 and early 1920, but other causes are equally possible. By 1919 her three children were all adults: Charles 30, Margaret 28, Emma 26.

She is **Robert Earl Wildermuth's grandmother** and **Chuck's great-great-grandmother** on the maternal Wildermuth side. Her sixth child Earl Adam Wildermuth (b. 1899) is the line that runs forward to Robert Earl, to Terrie, to Chuck. Robert Earl was born in 1924, five years after Flora's 1919 death; he never met her.
