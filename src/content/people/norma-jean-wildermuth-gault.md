**Norma Jean (Wildermuth) Gault** was the **fourth** of [Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) and [Sadye Irene (Fleming) Wildermuth](/family/sadye-fleming-wildermuth/)'s five children &mdash; born **14 September 1930 in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio**, eight years after her eldest sister [Ruth Irene](/family/ruth-irene-wildermuth-ridenour/), six years after her older brother [Robert Earl](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) (Chuck's grandfather), and two years after her older brother [Carl Edward](/family/carl-edward-wildermuth/).

She lived through the Marietta-Wildermuth household's 1930s and 1940s arc &mdash; the Depression years, the [1937 flood that came through the family's 413 Harmar Street house](/places/413-harmar-street-marietta/), and the war years that took her brother Robert Earl to the South Pacific on B-24 missions.

She **married a Gault**; her husband's first name, the date of marriage, and any children she had are open research. She died on **26 May 2007 in Ohio**, age 76, and was buried at **Fairmount Cemetery in Jacksontown, Licking County, Ohio**. Her **brother Carl Edward had died in Licking County eighteen years earlier**; the family's later-generation Licking County connection is consistent across both siblings.
