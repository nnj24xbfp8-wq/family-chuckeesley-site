Jennie Goldie was born **12 December 1836** in Ayrshire, Scotland. She came to Canada with her family in **1841**, age five &mdash; the Goldies relocated their milling operation to Ontario in that year, settling around Hamilton. She married [Albert Robert Eesley](/family/albert-robert-eesley/), the journeyman miller's son who had crossed from Stratford-on-Avon to work for them, in **Ontario in 1861**.

She lived the rest of her life in Canada and died at **424 Sackville Street, Toronto, Ontario** on **12 August 1911**, age seventy-four. She was buried in Toronto two days later. The Goldie milling family she came from is the one whose flour mill is on the cover of [Bean's 1985 family history](/docs/eesley-family-history-1985/) &mdash; drawn by Will Eesley a generation later, an architect's homage to where his grandmother's family had worked.

The Bean text reads *"Jeanie was blessed with a very remarkable memory. She frequently went to church, and having heard the sermon, would return home and repeat it almost word for word. She was a woman of superior intelligence. Those who knew her say that she was a wonderful story teller, and if this trait is carried in genes, it showed up in some of her children and grandchildren."* Dale's tree corrects the spelling of the first name to Jennie; everything else of her stands.

> *Source: [Dale Eesley / FamilySearch &mdash; Jennie Goldie (L2J9-GM1)](https://www.familysearch.org/tree/person/details/L2J9-GM1).*
