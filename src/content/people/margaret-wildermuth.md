Margaret Wildermuth was one of the **nine children of [William Clifford Wildermuth](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher Wildermuth](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)**, born in Marietta, Ohio. Her name appears in [Earl Adam Wildermuth's 17 January 1972 family-history letter](/docs/letters/earl-adam-to-robert-earl-1972-01-17/) in the birth-order roster:

> *yes all of my brothers and sister were born in Marietta. May, Charlie, William, Margrette, Blanche, Emma, Earl and Pearl and George.*

She sits **fourth** in Earl Adam's recollected order &mdash; between her older brother William and her younger sister Blanche &mdash; which would put her birth roughly in the middle of the 1890s, but the exact year is open research. The *"Margrette"* spelling is Earl Adam's; the formal name was almost certainly Margaret.

Earl Adam provided no further detail about her in the 1972 letter, but **Robert Earl's own typed research notes from the same year** named her with her married name and residence:

> *"my Aunt Margaret Wildermuth Clark who lived in Newport, Ohio"*

Margaret married into the **Clark** family and lived in **Newport, Ohio** &mdash; the small Ohio-River town just east-southeast of Marietta on the old Route 7 corridor. Robert Earl mentioned her in the context of family driving trips: he and his parents would drive east on Route 7 along the Ohio River, stopping at his Aunt Margaret's in Newport on the way south toward St. Marys, West Virginia &mdash; the area his mother [Sadye Irene Fleming](/family/sadye-fleming-wildermuth/) had grown up across the river from.

Her sisters [Blanche Wildermuth](/family/blanche-wildermuth/), [Mae Clara Wildermuth](/family/mae-clara-wildermuth/), [Emma Wildermuth](/family/emma-wildermuth/), and [Pearl Wildermuth](/family/pearl-wildermuth/) all have their own pages on this site. Margaret's husband's first name, marriage year, and any children are open research.

> *Sources: [Earl Adam Wildermuth's 17 January 1972 letter](/docs/letters/earl-adam-to-robert-earl-1972-01-17/), p. 2; [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1990 Wildermuth/Fleming Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/) (the earlier 1972 typed research notes from which the Heritage was developed name her with the Clark married name and Newport residence).*
