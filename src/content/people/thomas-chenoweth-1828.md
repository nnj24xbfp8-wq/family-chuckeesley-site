Thomas Chenoweth was born **6 September 1828 in Harrisburg, Franklin County, Ohio** &mdash; the older brother of [Joseph Hill Chenoweth](/family/joseph-hill-chenoweth/) (Chuck's great-great-grandfather) and therefore [Lillie Dale Chenoweth Eesley](/family/lillie-dale-chenoweth/)'s uncle. Their father was [Elijah Chenoweth, Jr.](/family/elijah-chenoweth/), the Franklin County farmer-patriarch of the Ohio branch of the Cornish-American Chenoweth line. Thomas died **17 July 1908 in Harrisburg**, age 79, and is buried in Westerville, Franklin County, Ohio.

He served in the Union Army during the Civil War with the rank of **Corporal**, one of three Elijah Jr. sons to serve on the Union side (the others were [Joseph Hill](/family/joseph-hill-chenoweth/) and John Wesley Chenoweth). The specific regiment, the dates of enlistment and discharge, and any wartime correspondence are open research; Ohio Civil War rosters and the FamilySearch military-records collection could fill in the detail.

He married twice: first in **Madison, Ohio on 25 January 1853** (the GEDCOM does not yet name the first wife), and second in **Pickaway, Ohio on 1 February 1876**. His son [Chauncey "Chant" Chenoweth](/family/chauncey-chenoweth/) &mdash; Lillie Dale's cousin &mdash; eventually moved to California; Roberta Burnes's June 2026 email surfaces the September 1922 Lillie-to-Len letter in which Lillie asks her son Leonard to visit *"Chant"* during his solo cross-country car trip to the West Coast.
