**Flight engineer** of Robert Earl Wildermuth's B-24, 400th Squadron, 90th Bomb Group, Pacific theater 1944–45. From **Great Bend, Kansas** — the central-Kansas wheat-belt town. The **oldest man on the crew** at age **23** when the crew formed at Gowen Field — most of the others were 19 or 20.

The flight engineer on a B-24 sat on the **flight deck behind the pilots**, monitoring **fuel flow, hydraulic systems, engine instruments**, and the dozen other systems that needed continuous attention through a long-range bombing mission. He acted as **backup pilot in emergencies** and could fly the aircraft if both pilots were incapacitated.

He is named in Robert Earl's [1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) and the [combat log *The Big One*](/docs/the-big-one/). His pre-war Kansas life and post-war return are open research.
