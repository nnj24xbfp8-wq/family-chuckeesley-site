Dr. Angelo Battista Massaro was Chuck's uncle by marriage on the maternal Wildermuth side &mdash; the husband of [Debra (Wildermuth) Massaro](/family/debbie-wildermuth/), Terrie's younger sister and Chuck's aunt. The 2016 [Kendall Funeral Home obituary](https://www.kendallfuneralhome.com/obituaries/drangelo-massaro) is the primary source for the biographical detail that follows.

## The Italian-Tampa childhood

He was born **7 November 1935 in Tampa, Florida** &mdash; the son of Italian immigrants **Gian Battista Massaro** and **Adelina Cacciatore Massaro**. The Battista name forms the bridge: his given name was his father's, in the Southern Italian patronymic pattern, and would have been used in the family to mark the Italian inheritance through three names in a row (Gian Battista → Angelo Battista → his own son Giancarlo, the Giancarlo name itself an Italian compound). He had **two brothers, John and Luigi**, both of whom predeceased him, and a sister, **Lily Fazio**, who survived him.

The family ran **White Rose Dairy** &mdash; their own farm just outside Tampa &mdash; and Angelo grew up living and working on it through his teen years. He went to **Hillsborough High School in Tampa, class of 1953**, in the Tampa-Italian neighborhood. The Hillsborough Terriers were the school mascot; the class of 1953 is the standard fit for a Tampa-born November 1935 birth.

## The University of Florida and the medical track

He went on to the **University of Florida**, where he was a member of **ROTC** and graduated in 1957. He continued at the **University of Miami School of Medicine** &mdash; the path to a medical degree in Florida that was the natural one for an in-state Italian-American family of the period. He did his **internship and surgical residency at Jackson Memorial Hospital in Miami**, the city's largest teaching hospital and the standard surgical-training pipeline of the postwar Southeast.

## Bunker Hill Air Force Base, Indiana — Vietnam-era General Surgeon

After medical school he served as a **General Surgeon at Bunker Hill Air Force Base in Indiana** during the Vietnam era. Bunker Hill (later renamed Grissom Air Reserve Base) was a strategic-bomber facility in Peru, Indiana, and the Air Force's response to the doctor draft of the mid-to-late 1960s was to assign newly-licensed surgeons to its bases. Angelo's term there was the parallel-track answer to [Charlie Eesley's 1969&ndash;1971 ground tour in Vietnam](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) &mdash; the same draft, the same war, the same year-cohort of young American men, two different uniforms.

## Florida Hospital, Orlando — three decades of surgery

His main career was at **Florida Hospital in Orlando**, where he practiced as a General Surgeon for **nearly three decades**. He served the hospital in three formal roles: **Chief of Staff**, **Chief of the Surgical Department**, and **educator with the family practice residency program**. The Orlando connection is the geographic resonance with Robert Earl Wildermuth's own Orlando retirement &mdash; both branches of this side of the family had a presence in central Florida in the 1980s and 1990s.

## Two marriages, six children

He was married twice. His first wife, **Ellie Diez-Massaro**, is the mother of his four eldest sons:

- **Michael Massaro** (married Angie)
- **Mark Massaro** (life-partner Sean)
- **Timothy Massaro**
- **Christopher Massaro**

His second wife, **[Debra Wildermuth Massaro](/family/debbie-wildermuth/)** &mdash; Chuck's aunt &mdash; married him in approximately 1988 (the obituary says they were married 28 years at his death). With Debbie he had two children:

- **Giancarlo "Gian Carlo" Massaro** (married Cara) &mdash; named in [Terrie Lee Bain's 2017 obituary](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) as Chuck's first cousin on the maternal side.
- **[Briana Massaro Lockett](/family/briana-massaro-lockett/)** &mdash; also named in Terrie's obituary; the [Briana Massaro Lockett page](/family/briana-massaro-lockett/) on this site already carries her later married name and a brief biographical sketch.

He had **three grandchildren**: **Nicholas, Olivia, and Camille**.

## Giles County, Virginia — the retirement years

Following his retirement from Florida Hospital, he and Debbie relocated to **Giles County, Virginia**, in the New River Valley region of southwestern Virginia near the West Virginia line. They brought their two children &mdash; Giancarlo and Briana &mdash; and built what the obituary describes as their *"southern plantation style dream home,"* which Angelo himself remodeled.

In Giles County he gave **ten years of his retirement to volunteering at the New River Valley free clinic** &mdash; using the surgical and clinical skills of his Florida-Hospital career to serve patients who couldn't afford the standard medical economy. He was active in the **Eggleston Ruritan Club** and in **Holy Family Catholic Church in Pearisburg**, where he was an **RCIA Leader** &mdash; the Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults, the program by which adult converts are guided into the Catholic Church. The community-medicine work and the lay-Catholic leadership are the two complementary registers of his retirement life.

His interests were **reading, antique collecting, traveling with the family, and cooking** &mdash; the last of which is the Italian-Tampa register surviving into Virginia. He was active and loved being outdoors. The closing line of the obituary: *"As a man full of wisdom and unconditional love, Angelo will be sorely missed by friends and loved ones. But it was the simple things in life; his family, friends, and cooking that brought him the most happiness."*

## Death and inurnment

He **died at home on 29 March 2016 in Giles County**, age 80. A memorial service was held **2 April 2016 at Holy Family Catholic Church in Pearisburg, Virginia**, the church where he had been an RCIA Leader. His **inurnment was later held in Tampa, Florida, next to his parents** &mdash; Gian Battista and Adelina &mdash; closing the geographic arc that had taken him from White Rose Dairy in the Italian-Tampa neighborhood of his childhood to Indiana, Miami, Orlando, and Giles County, and home.

Memorial contributions went to **Holy Family Catholic Church** and **Young Life New River Valley**. The Kendall Funeral Home in Pembroke, Virginia handled the arrangements.

He predeceased his sister-in-law [Terrie Lee Bain](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) by fourteen months; Terrie's [obituary in May 2017](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) names Debbie as her sister and Angelo as already-deceased.
