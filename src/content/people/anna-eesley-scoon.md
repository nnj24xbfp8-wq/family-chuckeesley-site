Anna Eesley was the **second of [Albert Robert Eesley Sr](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/)'s ten children** — and one of the two siblings (with her older brother [John R. Eesley](/family/john-r-eesley/)) born in Canada during their parents' Ontario years, before Albert Robert began his Michigan mill-building period. [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/) names her in this position:

> *They were married there [in Ayr, Canada] and two children were born to them, J. R., who follows milling, and Mrs. J. B. Scoon, of Toronto.*

She married **J. B. Scoon** and settled in Toronto, where she lived alongside her siblings Dr. George the physician, Mrs. Jennie Baxter, and Mrs. George Sinclair.

She is identified in the **1899 family group portrait** (caption: *"Anna"*) alongside her nine siblings and their parents.

She is **Chuck's great-great-aunt** on the paternal side.
