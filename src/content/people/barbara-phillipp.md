Barbara Phillipp &mdash; the GEDCOM names her *Anna Barbara Phillipp* &mdash; was born **9 October 1712** in [Großaspach](/places/grossaspach/). She married [Johann Georg Werner](/family/johann-georg-werner/) on 11 February 1738. Their daughter Anna Maria Werner was born 9 June 1739.

She died **26 March 1742**, age thirty-one &mdash; only four years after her marriage and three years after Anna Maria's birth. Robert Earl's [1989 "Little History"](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-little-histories/) names her early death directly:

> *Barbara Phillipp, wife of Johann Georg Werner, was born on 9 October 1712 and died on 26 March 1742. Her age at death was only thirty-one.*

The "only" in that sentence is the rare moment in the sketches where Robert Earl lets the mortality of the early-18th-century Württemberg generations land as something other than a record of dates &mdash; a thirty-one-year-old woman dying in 1742, with a three-year-old daughter, is the kind of fact that ran silently behind every long pedigree of that era.

> *Source: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's "Little Histories"](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-little-histories/); GEDCOM &mdash; Dale Eesley / FamilySearch, sync 1 June 2026.*
