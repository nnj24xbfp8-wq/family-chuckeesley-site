Robert Earl Wildermuth was born in 1924 in Marietta, Ohio &mdash; the river town where his grandfather, Johann Michael Wildermuth, had set up shop as an immigrant shoemaker in the 1840s. He flew combat missions out of Biak, Leyte, and Mindoro in 1944&ndash;45 as the navigator on a ten-man B-24 crew. After the war he came to Stanford on the GI Bill, served again in Korea, worked at the Pentagon, retired as a lieutenant colonel, and spent the rest of his life teaching and researching the family back to the early 1700s in Württemberg.

He closed his last manuscript by addressing "future genealogists." This site is one of them answering.
