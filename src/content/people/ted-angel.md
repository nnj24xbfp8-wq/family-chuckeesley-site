**Ted Angel** was the **husband of [Faye Angel](/family/faye-angel/)**, Chuck Eesley's godmother. Together they ran a **picture-framing business** out of their Marietta house &mdash; **the basement filled with thousands of picture frames**, stacked and sorted and waiting for the customer orders that came in over decades.

A small-town family business at this scale &mdash; not a chain, not a factory, but the kind of skilled-trades home-business that operated out of a basement and a workshop &mdash; was a staple of Marietta-area economic life from the 1960s through the 2000s. The Angels' frame business sat in that pattern. **Ted was the maker side**, Faye was the host-and-keeper side; the basement of the house was the workshop.

The Angel home was one of the two **sick-day caregiver households** for young Chuck Eesley &mdash; alongside the Wolfes at [108 Hillcrest Drive](/family/betty-davis-wolfe/). When Chuck couldn't go to school, he was often at Ted and Faye's house, and the picture-framing operation went on around him.

## The Davis-family connection

Ted and Faye's daughter **[Rebecca "Becky" Lynne (Angel) Davis](/family/rebecca-angel-davis/)** married **William Harvey Davis Jr.** &mdash; making Ted and Faye the in-laws of the Davis-Wildermuth line through their daughter. The [c. 1992 Davis-Angel family portrait](/archive/davis-angel-family-portrait-1992/) is the documentary record of that connection, with Ted and Faye, Becky, and the Davis side all together. **Ted is Chuck's first cousin twice removed by marriage** &mdash; not blood, but family by Becky's marriage into the Davis-Wildermuth line.

Ted's pre-framing trade, the year he and Faye opened the framing business, and his dates are queued for further family-memory input from Chuck.
