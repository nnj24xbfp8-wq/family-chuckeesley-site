She was the **only sister** among the six children of Flora Schlicher Wildermuth's parents — the **Schlicher** family of Marietta, Ohio. Her siblings per Dale Eesley's GEDCOM (family record @F56@): **George** (b. 8 November 1861), **Daniel** (b. 8 January 1864), **David F** (b. 21 February 1866), **Edward** (b. March 1869), **[Flora](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)** (b. 24 August 1870 — Robert Earl Wildermuth's paternal grandmother), and Emma herself (b. June 1874, the youngest by four years).

## In the 1896 family group portrait

She is the **young woman standing next to [Edward Frederick Wildermuth](/family/edward-frederick-wildermuth/) in the back row** of the **[c. 1896 William Clifford Wildermuth family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** — confirmed by Chuck Eesley 2026. Emma Schlicher was 22 in 1896; Edward Frederick was 23. The unmarried young-adult cousins of the family stood together at the back of the gathering — her older sister Flora's brother-in-law beside her own future... well, not quite. Edward Frederick would not marry until 1918, and he would marry Arminda Hayes (not Emma), so the back-row pairing in the portrait is **two unmarried young adults standing companionably**, not a courting couple.

The namesake question for little Emma Wildermuth (b. October 1893) is still open: she may have been named after her **aunt Emma Schlicher** (b. June 1874) — the woman now identified standing next to Edward in the photograph — or after another Emma in the family.

## What's open

- Whether she married and had children.

(There's a confusing GEDCOM artifact where Eva Schmidt is also recorded as Charles Daniel Wildermuth's wife at @I145@; possibly the same record is reused or there is a transcription tangle in Dale's tree that future research can untangle.)

She is **Chuck's great-great-grand-aunt** on the maternal Wildermuth-Schlicher side.
