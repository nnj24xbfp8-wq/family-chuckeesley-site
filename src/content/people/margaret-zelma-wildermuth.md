Born **6 November 1891 in Marietta, Ohio**, the **middle child** of [William Clifford Wildermuth](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/), between her elder brother [Charles Daniel](/family/charles-daniel-wildermuth/) and her younger sister [Emma](/family/emma-wildermuth/). Visible in the **[c. 1896 family group portrait](/archive/william-wildermuth-family-group-portrait/)** at about age five — almost certainly the small girl seated front-row, in white, beside the smaller children.

## The California life and a hard 1943

She died **25 October 1943 in Los Angeles**, age **51**, and was buried at **Forest Lawn Memorial Park, Long Beach** — meaning by the end of her life she had moved from Marietta Ohio across the country to southern California. Whether she traveled the same Marietta-to-California route Robert Earl Wildermuth would take a few years later, or arrived via her brother Charles's Kansas relocation, or by some other path, is open research.

Her death came **six weeks after her father William Clifford's** death in Marietta on 10 September 1943 — the daughter dying soon after the father, in the middle of WWII. Both 1943 deaths in the same year are striking; the Marietta Times obituary archive might document either or both.

## Two children — Walter and Eva Catherine "Kate" Clark

A labeled c. 1913 Front Street, Marietta photograph that surfaced in this archive in June 2026 names Margaret Zelma's two children: [Walter Clark](/family/walter-clark/) (b. 6 April 1911) and [Eva Catherine "Kate" Clark](/family/eva-catherine-kate-clark/) (b. 24 December 1912). The photograph is the [c. 1913 Wildermuth-sisters' children portrait on Front Street](/archive/wildermuth-sisters-children-front-street-1913/), with Margaret Zelma standing alongside the stroller holding Kate, with Walter on a small tricycle, and with her older sister [Mae Clara](/family/mae-clara-wildermuth/)'s children [Ruth Mae Seyler](/family/ruth-mae-seyler-mcintyre/) and [Robert Marion Seyler Sr.](/family/robert-marion-seyler-sr/) beside them. The print confirms that **Margaret Zelma married a Clarke** — her married surname appears on the print as *"Margaret Wildermuth Clarke"* — and that her children's surname was Clark (the family appears to have dropped the final *e* in the children's spelling, a common Anglo-spelling convention of the period).

Margaret's husband's first name, the date of her marriage, and the path of her later life from Marietta to Los Angeles are all open research. Walter and Kate are her two confirmed children as of this archive's June 2026 update.
