William Eesley was born in **Detroit, Michigan**, one of the ten children of [Albert Robert Eesley](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) (Stratford-on-Avon, England; eventually of Toronto) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/) (Ayrshire, Scotland; of Ayr, Ontario). He is **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the paternal side &mdash; specifically, the brother of Chuck's great-grandfather [Charles Leonard Eesley](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/).

He educated at the Detroit public schools and then traveled for several years as a journeyman miller, the same trade six of his brothers also took up. In **1909** he took up residence at **College Corner**, a small town straddling the Ohio-Indiana state line in southwestern Ohio's Butler County (and southeastern Indiana's Union County). His milling property sat on the Indiana side of the state line; his residence on the Ohio side. He was also a third owner in the **Oxford mills** at nearby Oxford, Ohio &mdash; the home of Miami University. By the time of his obituary he was "accounted one of the substantial business men of his locality."

## The Sir Robert Peel marriage

He married **[Clara Peel](/family/clara-peel-eesley/)** of Topeka, Kansas &mdash; daughter of James and Ella Peel of Mount Sterling, Kentucky. Clara was, by the obituary's account, **a lineal descendant of Sir Robert Peel (1788&ndash;1850)** &mdash; the British Prime Minister, founder of the modern Conservative Party, founder of the Metropolitan Police of London (the "Bobbies" / "Peelers" still take his name), and architect of the repeal of the Corn Laws in 1846. Whether the Peel lineage is correctly remembered by the obituary or family-folkloric is open; the connection appears nowhere else in this archive's structured genealogy and is queued for follow-up at the Peel Society or in 19th-century Kansas Peel family records.

One child of the marriage is named in the obituary:

- **Frances Eesley** (b. 1901) &mdash; m. **H. Toney**, a farmer near College Corner. They had one son, **William E. Toney**, named for his maternal grandfather.

## Civic life

The obituary records William as a member of the **Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks** &mdash; a national fraternal order at its peak in the early 20th century, with strong Ohio-Indiana chapters along the Miami River corridor.

## Why this page matters to the rest of the archive

William's obituary is the **single most detailed surviving biographical sketch of his father Albert Robert Eesley** &mdash; the source of the Shakespeare's-birthplace schoolteaching detail, the Newark-Ayr-Detroit-Toronto migration route, and the full list of A. R. and Jennie's ten children. The full text of the obituary is held as its own document in this archive: [William Eesley obituary, c. 1916](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/).
