Clara Peel was born in **Topeka, Kansas**, daughter of **James and Ella Peel** of Mount Sterling, Kentucky. She married [William Eesley](/family/william-eesley-college-corner/) (Chuck's great-great-uncle, one of Albert Robert and Jennie Goldie's ten children) and settled with him at **College Corner**, on the Ohio-Indiana state line. They had one child:

- **[Frances Eesley](/family/frances-eesley-toney/)** (b. 1901) — m. **H. Toney**, a farmer near College Corner; one son named William E. Toney for his maternal grandfather.

## The Sir Robert Peel claim

William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary in a Butler County, Ohio memoirs volume describes Clara as **"a lineal descendant of the eminent English statesman, Sir Robert Peel."** [**Sir Robert Peel** (1788&ndash;1850)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Peel) was twice Prime Minister of the United Kingdom (1834&ndash;35, 1841&ndash;46), founder of the modern Conservative Party, founder of the Metropolitan Police of London (whose officers still take their colloquial names *"Bobbies"* and *"Peelers"* from his), and the prime mover behind the 1846 repeal of the Corn Laws &mdash; the act that split his party but is now broadly credited with averting Irish-famine-era starvation in Britain and with founding the United Kingdom's free-trade era. He is one of the most consequential British statesmen of the 19th century.

Whether Clara's descent is documented or family-folkloric is so far an open question in this archive. Late-19th and early-20th century American family histories routinely embedded a connection to a famous British statesman or aristocrat without surviving the rigor of late-20th-century genealogy. The claim is recorded here as the obituary records it, and it is queued for research at the [Peel Society](https://www.peelsociety.org/) and in 19th-century Kansas Peel-family records.

If true, the line Sir Robert Peel &rarr; ? &rarr; James Peel &rarr; Clara Peel &rarr; Eesley would be the most consequential English-statesman thread on Chuck's paternal side.
