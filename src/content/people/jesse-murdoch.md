He was **tail gunner** of Robert Earl Wildermuth's B-24 — the rearmost position in the aircraft, sitting in the tail turret with twin .50-caliber machine guns covering the rear quarter sphere. The position was nicknamed **"tail end Charlie"** through the WWII Army Air Corps.

From **Tyler, Texas**.

His more lasting role in the family record: **crew photographer**. Per Robert Earl's [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/):

> *"Cpl. Jesse Murdoch from Tyler, Texas was tail end Charlie, the tail gunner. Jesse also became our unofficial photographer in the jungles of the South Pacific."*

The crew's **Pacific photographs** — whatever survives in the [Wildermuth wartime photo set](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) — were most plausibly **Jesse's work**. He was the documentary eye of the ten-man crew in 1944–45, the man who pointed the camera at the others.

He is named in Robert Earl's [combat log *The Big One*](/docs/the-big-one/). His pre-war Texas life, his post-war return, and what happened to his photographic record (and whether any of his shots beyond what's in the family archive survive elsewhere) are open research.

> *Sources: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/); [The Big One](/docs/the-big-one/).*
