Robert Earl Wildermuth's **best friend on the B-24 crew** — per Robert Earl's own [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/), where Fuzzy is the only crewman explicitly named that way. He was the **radio operator** on the crew and held the rank of **Corporal** at first, **Technical Sergeant** by war's end.

## Logansport and Indiana University

From **Logansport, Indiana** — the small Wabash-River town in northern Indiana — and a **second-year pre-med student at Indiana University** before he enlisted. He was one of the hundreds of aviation cadets who had washed out of pilot/bombardier/navigator training at the **Nashville Classification Center**:

> *"Cpl. Kuszmaul was a second year pre-med student at Indiana University and he was one of the hundreds of men eliminated from cadet training at the now infamous Nashville Training Center."*

Robert Earl, who had **passed** Nashville and gone on to navigator school at Selman Field, met Fuzzy after Nashville when the two were assigned to the same B-24 crew at Gowen Field, Boise, Idaho. Their shared **pre-med background** — Robert Earl had been pre-med at Marietta before the war and would return to it at Stanford after — was almost certainly part of what made them best friends in the Pacific. Two would-be doctors, both interrupted by the war, both flying out of Biak and Tacloban and Ie Shima.

## The trip home

When the war ended and the crew flew back to **McClellan Field, California**, the ten men said their goodbyes and scattered:

> *"We left the airplane at McClellan Air Field, got railroad tickets to the nearest military installation to our homes; said our goodbyes to each other and to this day none of us has ever met again."*

But Robert Earl and Fuzzy lived close enough together — Logansport and Marietta — that their nearest military installation was the same: **Wright-Patterson Field in Dayton, Ohio**. They traveled together across the country by train, had what Robert Earl described as a **"scrumptious last meal together in the swankiest restaurant in Dayton, Ohio,"** and parted at Wright-Patterson on **30 November 1945**. It is the only post-war farewell of the crew Robert Earl describes in detail.

He is named in Robert Earl's [combat log *The Big One*](/docs/the-big-one/). His post-war life, whether he finished pre-med and became a doctor, and whether he and Robert Earl ever met again after the Dayton dinner are open research.

> *Sources: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/); [The Big One](/docs/the-big-one/).*
