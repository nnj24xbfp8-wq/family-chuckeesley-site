**Pearl Wildermuth** was the **twin sister of [Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/)** &mdash; Chuck's great-grandfather. Per the family GEDCOM, **Pearl was born 5 October 1899 and Earl Adam was born 6 October 1899**, the two birthdates one day apart in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio. **The two-birthdate split is consistent with twins born across midnight**, or with a small GEDCOM transcription difference.

The Wildermuth-side family record &mdash; previously framed Earl Adam as a single child with siblings &mdash; can now be reread with **Pearl as his twin sister**: the seventh and eighth of [William Clifford](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)'s nine children, two siblings sharing the first twenty months of life and growing up together through Marietta's 1900s and 1910s.

## The 1899 birth context

The Wildermuth family in early October 1899: William Clifford (32), Flora (35-ish), their four older surviving children &mdash; **[Mae Clara](/family/mae-clara-wildermuth/)** (13), **[Charles Daniel](/family/charles-daniel-wildermuth/)** (10), **[Margaret Zelma](/family/margaret-zelma-wildermuth/)** (7-8), **[Emma](/family/emma-wildermuth/)** (5-6) &mdash; and a recent infant loss in **[William Jr.](/family/william-wildermuth-jr/)** who had died December 1897 not even two years old. **The twin birth of Earl Adam and Pearl in October 1899 came as a double-blessing after the infant loss two years before.** Twin survival in 1899 small-town America was not guaranteed; that **both twins survived their infancy** is the first piece of luck the family had had since the late-1897 loss.

## Pearl's later life

Pearl's later life is open research. **Whether she lived to adulthood, married, had children, or died in childhood or young adulthood is not yet recorded in this archive.** Her dates of death and burial are open in the GEDCOM. If she lived to adulthood, she would have been Chuck's great-grandfather's twin sister; her descendants (if any) would be among Robert Earl Wildermuth's first cousins on the paternal Wildermuth side.
