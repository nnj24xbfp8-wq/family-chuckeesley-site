Charles McMaster Eesley was born February 17, 1947 in Columbus, Ohio, and grew up the son of Will Eesley the architect and Peggy McMaster Eesley. He served in the U.S. Army from 1969 to 1971 — a Vietnam veteran — and was a member of American Legion Post 64. He took his economics degree at Marietta College and worked in sales and human resources at Sears before turning to finance, first as a stockbroker for The Ohio Company and later for Hazlett, Burt &amp; Watson. He was a longtime, active member of First Congregational United Church of Christ in Marietta, where he served on boards and helped manage the church's endowment. He studied the Civil War and military strategy; he believed strongly in the responsibility of voting. He later remarried, to Diana Hoffer, in 1999. He died November 26, 2015 at 68, in Marietta.
