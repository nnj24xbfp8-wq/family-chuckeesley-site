Andreus Wildermuth was born around **1748** in **[Rielingshausen](/places/rielingshausen-church/)**, the small village in the **Württemberg** region of southwestern Germany, about 20 kilometers northeast of Stuttgart. He is the **deepest documented Wildermuth ancestor** in this archive, surfaced by [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/)'s eighteen-year genealogical research and reported in his [1990 Wildermuth/Fleming Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/).

## Occupation: Gartner (vineyard gardener)

Robert Earl recorded his occupation as ***Gartner* — vineyard gardener** &mdash; the trade of cultivating and tending the vineyards that produced the wines of the Neckar river valley. The Württemberg Neckar wine country was already a centuries-old vinicultural region by Andreus's lifetime; the small Rielingshausen vineyards around the Marbach area are still in cultivation today. To be a *Gartner* in Rielingshausen in the late 18th century was to belong to the village's agricultural craft layer &mdash; tenant or smallholder vineyard work, the steady trade of every wine-producing village in southwest Germany.

## His son

The only documented child in this archive is **[Adam Wildermuth](/family/adam-wildermuth/)** (b. 1768 Rielingshausen), who followed his father into the *Gartner* trade in the same village.

## What is not yet documented

Robert Earl's 1990 research notes the limit of what he could trace:

> *I have not attempted to go back any farther than Andreus &mdash; my goal at the start was to find the first Wildermuth in America. My research about Andreus is informational only; nothing more.... I don't know who his father was or his mother. Andreus, our fourth great-grandfather. I do know that he was the father of Adam Wildermuth, and that he was a gartner (vineyard gardener) in Rielingshausen.*

He is **Chuck's six-times-great-grandfather** through the line:

> Andreus &rarr; [Adam Wildermuth](/family/adam-wildermuth/) (b. 1768) &rarr; *unconfirmed intermediate* &rarr; **[Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/)** (b. ~1818 Grossaspach, the 1847 emigrant to America) &rarr; *unconfirmed intermediate* &rarr; **John Wildermuth** &rarr; **[Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/)** (Robert Earl's father) &rarr; **[Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/)** &rarr; **[Terrie Lee Wildermuth Eesley](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/)** &rarr; Chuck.

The two intermediate generations between Adam and Johann Michael, and between Johann Michael and Earl Adam's father, are queued for research follow-up against the LDS International Genealogical Index records that Robert Earl consulted.
