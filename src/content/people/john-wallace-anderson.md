He was the **second-eldest** of the six Anderson children and the **first son** — born at **Venice, Washington County, Pennsylvania on 21 June 1864**, three weeks before Sherman's army would close on Atlanta. His father [Rev. Abraham](/family/abraham-ramsey-anderson/) was thirty-two; his mother [Nancy Jane](/family/nancy-jane-shaw-anderson/) had been pregnant with him through the Battle of the Wilderness, Spotsylvania, and Cold Harbor. He carried his Presbyterian-minister father's **first name** and the family's distinctive **Wallace** middle (his elder sister [Anna Janett](/family/anna-janett-anderson-kelso/) would marry a Kelso who used the same middle name — "Wallace" was a regional Scots-Presbyterian family name in southwestern Pennsylvania, not coincidence).

## The Emporia marriage

His first marriage took him **west**. On **21 May 1888 in Emporia, Lyon County, Kansas**, he married **[Mary Ellen Lloyd](/family/mary-ellen-lloyd-anderson/)** — born **1859 in Ontario, Canada**. The Kansas wedding location is the surprise of his record. **Emporia in 1888** was a railroad town on the Kansas Pacific line, a center of cattle-shipping and the home of the State Normal School (now Emporia State University). What John Wallace was doing there at 23 — whether he was working the railroad, teaching at the Normal, or simply visiting family who had moved out — is the queued question. Mary Ellen herself had been born in Ontario but appears to have come into Pittsburgh's orbit by the time of her death; she died in **Pittsburgh, Allegheny County, on (year only) 1896**, age 37. The death-in-Pittsburgh-after-a-Kansas-marriage pattern suggests they had moved east to a Pittsburgh-region life after Emporia, possibly when his father aged in Venice and his sister Anna Janett married William Kelso in 1891.

## The second marriage

He was 32 when Mary Ellen died. He waited **four years**. On **27 April 1900 in Pittsburgh**, he married **[Inez Lucretia Keefer](/family/inez-lucretia-keefer-anderson/)** — born **13 July 1878 in Perry Township, Clarion County, PA**. She was **fourteen years younger** than he was. Clarion County is in **western Pennsylvania**, north of Pittsburgh near the Allegheny River; the Keefer family was from one of the small townships of the county's farming and oil-region geography. Inez died **21 November 1946 in Oil City, Venango County** — Pittsburgh's oil country, the heart of the **Pennsylvania oil rush** that had defined the regional economy since Drake's 1859 strike at Titusville.

## The Anderson Farm

John Wallace died **1 July 1953 in Pittsburgh** at age **89** — outliving Inez by almost seven years and his first wife by **fifty-seven**. His burial place is recorded in Dale's tree as **"Anderson Farm, Leeper, Clarion County, Pennsylvania"** — meaning by the end of his life he had family land in Clarion County, near Inez's birthplace, that became the family's burial ground. That this farm bears the **Anderson** name suggests it was either passed down to him from a Pennsylvania Anderson line or established by him; either way, it is the one family-property reference of his record. **Leeper** is a small unincorporated community in Farmington Township, Clarion County.

## Position in the family

He was the **only son** of Rev. Abraham's six children to leave a documented family line through the GEDCOM. The other son, [William James](/family/william-james-anderson/) (b. 1875, twelve years younger), has no marriage or descendants documented in Dale's tree. Of the daughters, only [Alice Gertrude](/family/alice-anderson-mcmaster/) — Chuck's great-grandmother — has the continuing line to the present.

He is **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the paternal side.
