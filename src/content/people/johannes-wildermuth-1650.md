Johannes Wildermuth was born in **1650** in Württemberg, Germany — **just two years after the Treaty of Westphalia (1648) ended the Thirty Years' War**, which had killed an estimated one-third of the German-speaking population and left Württemberg's villages devastated. He was born into the rebuilding generation. He died in **1694**, age **44**.

He married **[Anna Metzger](/family/anna-metzger/)** (1650&ndash;1728) and was the father of **[Johannes Wildermuth Jr.](/family/johannes-wildermuth-1682/)** (b. 1682).

He is **the deepest documented Wildermuth ancestor in this archive**, surfaced from the FamilySearch family tree (Chuck transmitted the tree screenshot in June 2026). Reaching back to 1650 places the documented Wildermuth line within two years of the founding peace of the modern European state system. The mid-17th-century Württemberg world he was born into was rural, devastated, in slow recovery; the Wildermuths were almost certainly in [Rielingshausen](/places/rielingshausen-church/) or one of the neighboring Neckar-valley villages from this point forward continuously, until [Johann Michael Wildermuth](/family/johann-michael-wildermuth/) emigrated to America in 1847 — **197 years downstream**.
