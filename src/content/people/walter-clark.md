Walter Clark was the son of [Margaret Zelma (Wildermuth) Clarke](/family/margaret-zelma-wildermuth/) and her husband Clarke — born **6 April 1911**, older brother of [Eva Catherine "Kate" Clark](/family/eva-catherine-kate-clark/). The family appears to have used the spelling Clark (without the final -e) for the children while Margaret's married name was recorded as Clarke.

He appears at about age 2 in the [c. 1913 photograph of his mother and his aunt Mae Clara Seyler with all four of their then-small children](/archive/wildermuth-sisters-children-front-street-1913/), in front of Mae Clara's house on Front Street, Marietta — Walter on a small tricycle, pale clothes, between his mother and his older cousin [Ruth Mae Seyler](/family/ruth-mae-seyler-mcintyre/).

Walter is Chuck Eesley's first cousin twice removed on the paternal Wildermuth side. His marriage, his trade, his children, and the date of his death are open research.

> *Sources: [c. 1913 Wildermuth-sisters' children portrait on Front Street](/archive/wildermuth-sisters-children-front-street-1913/) family-labeled print, transmitted by Chuck Eesley June 2026.*
