Dale Dudley Eesley was born **5 August 1906 in Pleasant Township, Franklin County, Ohio** &mdash; the same county and same year as Lillie Dale's sister Scioto's marriage. He died **14 July 1939**, age **32**, and is buried at Grove City, Franklin County, Ohio. His engagement to **[Thelma Haughn](/family/thelma/)** was unfulfilled by his death; Thelma stayed close to the family until her own death in the 1970s &mdash; counted as family even though no wedding ever happened.

His birth date is sometimes recorded in family memory as **1916**, but the GEDCOM and FamilySearch record MCZ8-WYX give **1906**, which is consistent with his position as one of Charles Leonard and Lillie Dale's older children and with his age in the group portrait. Bean's 1985 register also gives his middle name as **"George,"** but the GEDCOM has just "Dale Dudley"; the third name may be a Bean expansion or a family-memory variant.

He appears in the [c. 1937&ndash;1939 group portrait of Charles Leonard with five of his adult sons and grandson Tommy](/archive/charles-leonard-and-sons-late-1930s/), as the figure on the right end of the brothers' line &mdash; the only definitive photograph of him in this archive's keeping, taken in the last window before his death at thirty-two.

## How he died

Per the family memory: **he took the boat out on Black Lake, Michigan to go fishing on 14 July 1939 and never returned. The boat was later found; he had drowned in the lake.** Black Lake was where his older brother [Will](/family/wilbur-eesley/) had been photographed with their nephew Tommy in 1933 (Aunt Maggie's deck, slide 4); the lake was a family-known recreation place. Dale was thirty-two, on what should have been an ordinary summer fishing trip. His body was returned home and is buried at **Grove City, Franklin County, Ohio**.

Dale's fiancée Thelma was the one who would have built a household with him; she did not. Charles Leonard and Lillie Dale lost their first son this way; in three more years they would lose Lyle to a Japanese prison camp on the other side of the Pacific. Within five summers the Bexley household had lost two of its adult sons.

Listed as a spouse of Thelma here as a convention for keeping the connection visible on both pages; in fact they were engaged, not married.
