**Reverend Abraham Ramsey Anderson** was born **3 February 1832** in **Chartiers Township, Washington County, Pennsylvania**, married [Nancy Jane Shaw](/family/nancy-jane-shaw-anderson/) on **11 October 1860** in Washington, Pennsylvania, and served as a **Presbyterian minister for over fifty years**. He died **17 July 1919** in Venice, Washington County, Pennsylvania, and was buried there. Age 87.

Maggie Eesley's catalogue note on his young-man cabinet card &mdash; an oval portrait pasted on sepia-colored card stock, about 1⅜" by 1⅞", dated around 1860:

> *Sepia-toned photo shows a serious expression on this young man with pronounced sideburns, modeled after General Burnside of the Civil War. Abraham went on to become the beloved minister for over 50 years of the Presbyterian Church.*

He and Nancy Jane raised **six children** — [Anna Janett](/family/anna-janett-anderson-kelso/) (b. May 1862, m. William Kelso 1891, d. 1949), [John Wallace](/family/john-wallace-anderson/) (b. 21 June 1864, m. Mary Ellen Lloyd 1888 in Kansas, m. Inez Keefer 1900, d. 1953), [Mary Belle](/family/mary-belle-anderson/) (b. 6 October 1866, no marriage or death date documented), [Margaret Florence](/family/margaret-florence-anderson/) (b. 23 March 1869 — the recipient of the 1894 letter), [Alice Gertrude](/family/alice-anderson-mcmaster/) (b. 9 July 1871, m. Robert T. McMaster, d. 1962, **Chuck's great-grandmother** through [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/)), and [William James](/family/william-james-anderson/) (b. 9 February 1875, no marriage or death date documented). Two of the four sisters married, only one son left a documented descendant line, and only one daughter — Alice — connects forward to the present. On the close-up of the 1860 wedding-year double portrait of him and Nancy Jane, Maggie's caption recorded the family memory of them as a couple:

> *My great-grandparents were known for their sense of humor, kindness, and love of children.*

## The 1894 letter

On **Monday, 21 May 1894**, he wrote a long letter to his daughter [Margaret Florence Anderson](/family/margaret-florence-anderson/) on stiff lined paper, 7¾" by 10". The letter is one of two surviving multi-page letter primary sources in this archive &mdash; the other being [Charlie Eesley's Vietnam letters](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) seventy-five years later. The full document and partial transcription: [Letter from Rev. Abraham Anderson to his daughter Margaret, 21 May 1894](/docs/letters/rev-anderson-to-margaret-1894/).

He is **Chuck's great-great-grandfather** through Alice &rarr; [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) &rarr; [Charles McMaster Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/) &rarr; Chuck.
