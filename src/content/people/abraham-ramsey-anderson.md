**Reverend Abraham Ramsey Anderson** was born in **1832** in Pennsylvania, married [Nancy Jane Shaw](/family/nancy-jane-shaw-anderson/) around **1860** in Washington, Pennsylvania, and served as a **Presbyterian minister for over fifty years**. He died in **1919**, age 87.

Maggie Eesley's catalogue note on his young-man cabinet card &mdash; an oval portrait pasted on sepia-colored card stock, about 1⅜" by 1⅞", dated around 1860:

> *Sepia-toned photo shows a serious expression on this young man with pronounced sideburns, modeled after General Burnside of the Civil War. Abraham went on to become the beloved minister for over 50 years of the Presbyterian Church.*

He and Nancy Jane raised **six children**, one of whom was [Alice Anderson McMaster](/family/alice-anderson-mcmaster/) (1871&ndash;1961), Chuck's great-grandmother through [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/). On the close-up of the 1860 wedding-year double portrait of him and Nancy Jane, Maggie's caption recorded the family memory of them as a couple:

> *My great-grandparents were known for their sense of humor, kindness, and love of children.*

## The 1894 letter

In **May 1894** he wrote a long letter to his daughter Margaret Anderson on stiff lined paper, 7¾" by 10". Both pages survive in the family archive along with a closeup of his "elegantly written script." Maggie's catalogue note:

> *Elegantly written script shows the slow pace of the era when writing was the main form of communication between distant family members.*

The letter is one of two surviving multi-page letter primary sources in this archive &mdash; the other being [Charlie Eesley's Vietnam letters](/docs/letters-from-vietnam/) seventy-five years later. (A dedicated document page for the 1894 letter is queued.)

He is **Chuck's great-great-grandfather** through Alice &rarr; [Peggy McMaster Eesley](/family/margaret-mcmaster-eesley/) &rarr; [Charles McMaster Eesley](/family/charles-eesley/) &rarr; Chuck.
