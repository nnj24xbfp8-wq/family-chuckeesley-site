Jacob Schmidgall is the line-end on the Schmidgall side. Robert Earl's [1989 "Little History"](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-little-histories/) for him is a single paragraph:

> *Jacob Schmidgall, the father of Georg Wilhelm and one of our fourth great-grandfathers, was a farmer in Großhöchberg. His wife's name is unknown as is his birth and death dates.*

The village of *Großhöchberg* sits in the Backnang district, in the *Spiegelberg* sub-region of the Württemberg hills &mdash; further inland and higher than the Röhrach his son [Georg Wilhelm Schmidgall](/family/georg-wilhelm-schmidgall/) later farmed. The move from Großhöchberg to Röhrach is the Schmidgall-side intra-Württemberg migration of the late 18th century.

The Schmidgall surname is locally associated with the Schwäbisch Hall area of Württemberg and traces back through *Schmiedgrall / Schmidgaul* spellings in the 17th-century parish records. Beyond Jacob, the line is open research.

> *Source: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's "Little Histories"](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-little-histories/); GEDCOM &mdash; Dale Eesley / FamilySearch, sync 1 June 2026.*
