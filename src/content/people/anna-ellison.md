Anna Ellison is **Chuck's maternal four-times-great-grandmother** on the Way line. She is the **second of the English-immigrant founders** of the Way family branch in Noble County, Ohio &mdash; alongside her husband [Edward Taylor Way](/family/edward-taylor-way/) of the Isle of Wight.

She was born **28 June 1821 at Coppull, Lancashire, England**, in the cotton-mill belt south of Preston in the English Midlands. The parish church record at Coppull records her christening as **22 July 1821**, three and a half weeks after her birth. She would have grown up in the Lancashire mill country of the 1820s and 1830s &mdash; the decade Engels would later document &mdash; before crossing the Atlantic to southeastern Ohio.

## The 1845 Morgan County marriage

On **27 January 1845, at Morgan County, Ohio**, age 23, Anna married **[Edward Taylor Way](/family/edward-taylor-way/)** &mdash; the Isle of Wight-born farmer eight years her senior. The Morgan-Noble County area of southeastern Ohio was settled in this period by a cluster of English-immigrant farming families &mdash; the Way, Ellison, Matthews, and adjacent surnames recurring across the 1840s and 1850s county records. Her marriage to Edward T. Way is the Lancashire-and-Isle-of-Wight crossing that anchors the Way line in Ohio.

## The Way children

Anna's nine documented children with Edward Taylor Way are enumerated in the ["Way family among early settlers in Noble County"](/docs/way-family-noble-county-clipping/) newspaper clipping preserved in Chuck's family papers: *Elizabeth, David R., Mary A., Abagail, Edward E., Henry, Sarah E., Emily J. and William M.* The eldest, Elizabeth, the clipping's family-papers annotation records, *"was married twice. Her first husband was Lindley Yarnall."* The middle son **[Edward E. Way](/family/edward-e-way/)** (b. 14 May 1851) is the one Chuck descends from &mdash; through Victoria Anna Way &rarr; Homer Davis &rarr; Dorothy Wildermuth &rarr; Terrie Lee.
