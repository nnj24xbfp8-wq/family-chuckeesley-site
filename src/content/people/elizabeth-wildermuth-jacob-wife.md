## The matron of the c. 1885–1895 family portrait

Elizabeth Wildermuth, **wife of [Jacob Wildermuth](/family/jacob-wildermuth/)**, is the matriarch of a Wildermuth-surname family that does **not appear in Dale Eesley's FamilySearch tree**. She sits at front-center of the **c. 1885–1895 studio cabinet-card portrait** in a long dark high-collared dress with a fitted bodice — the canonical late-Victorian matron's pose — with her hands folded in her lap and her young son **Troy B** held between her and her husband.

Her **maiden name, birth date, death date, and place of origin** are all open. From the photograph she appears to be in her late 40s to early 50s, placing her birth around **1835–1845** — the same generation as [Catharina Boeshar Wildermuth](/family/catharina-boeshar-wildermuth/) (Robert Earl Wildermuth's actual great-grandmother), born 1840 in Breitenbach, Pfalz. Whether the two women knew each other is open speculation; they were Wildermuth-surname women of the same generation in America, and if the families ever met or corresponded, it would be at the genealogically tantalizing periphery of the broader Wildermuth-in-America network.

## The nine children

Per Chuck Eesley's identification of the family portrait, Elizabeth and Jacob had **nine children** visible in the photograph: **Charles E, John "Leb", Harry J, Jesse W** (the four adult sons in the back row); **Maude B, Lucy, Zella M, "Lanessa" Louise** (the four adult daughters in the middle row); and **Troy B** (the small boy between the parents). Whether any further children were born and lived but were absent from this portrait is open.
