Edward E. Way is Chuck's **maternal three-times-great-grandfather** through the Davis line: Edward E. Way &rarr; [Victoria Anna Way Davis](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/) &rarr; [Homer Edward Davis](/family/homer-davis/) &rarr; [Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) &rarr; [Terrie Lee](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/) &rarr; Chuck.

He was born **14 May 1851 in Noble County, Ohio**, the eldest known son of [Edward Taylor Way](/family/edward-taylor-way/) (1812-1879, Isle of Wight, England) and [Anna Ellison](/family/anna-ellison/) (b. 1821, Coppull, Lancashire, England, United Kingdom). The Way and Ellison families were both English-immigrant farming families that crossed the Atlantic in the early-to-mid nineteenth century and settled in Noble and Morgan counties, southeastern Ohio.

## The Matthews marriage and ten children

On **11 April 1872 at Noble County, Ohio** &mdash; one month before his 21st birthday &mdash; Edward married **[Tacy Elizabeth Matthews](/family/tacy-elizabeth-matthews/)** (1848-1902), of Washington County, Ohio. Tacy was three years older.

The marriage produced **ten children** in the GEDCOM record, the older ones bearing the brunt of the late-nineteenth-century farm-family pattern of large rural households:

- [Victoria Anna Way](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/) (b. 27 January 1874; d. 26 June 1903) &mdash; Chuck's direct ancestor, who married William Armstrong Davis
- [Nora Angie Way](/family/nora-angie-way/) (b. 23 February 1876; d. 1939)
- A child (I214) (b. ?)
- [Belsora "Belle" A. Way](/family/belsora-way-wickens/) (b. 19 December 1880; d. 1912) &mdash; the bride of the 1901 Wickens wedding portrait
- A child (I225) (b. ?)
- [Emma Maude Way](/family/emma-maude-way/) (b. 3 July 1883; d. 1917) &mdash; would die in Lost Springs, Converse County, Wyoming
- [Angie Nora Way](/family/angie-nora-way/) (b. 26 July 1884; d. 1938)
- Three further siblings (I220, I227, I229)

Tacy died on **29 January 1902**, four months after Belle's September 1901 wedding to Howard Wickens &mdash; making the 1901 wedding portrait one of the last photographs taken of her. Edward E. survived her by **twenty-two years**, dying on **2 November 1924, age 73**, and was buried two days later at Crooked Tree Cemetery, Noble County &mdash; the same ground where Tacy and several of his daughters (Victoria, Belle, Emma, Nora Angie) had been buried.

## In the 1901 wedding portrait

Edward E. Way is **figure #8** in the family's identification legend for the [Belsora-Howard Wickens 1901 wedding portrait](/family/belsora-way-wickens/) &mdash; labeled there as *"Edward E. Way (great-grandfather)"* from his granddaughter Dorothy Davis Wildermuth's perspective. The legend mistakes that have circulated on this page in earlier passes &mdash; conflating him with his father Edward *Taylor* Way &mdash; were corrected on the basis of the GEDCOM trace and Dorothy's family-papers annotation, which is unambiguous.

## Why the 1924 death matters

Edward E. Way outlived his daughter Belle by twelve years, his daughter Victoria Anna by twenty-one years, and his wife Tacy by twenty-two years. By the time of his own death in 1924, his great-grandson [Homer Edward Davis](/family/homer-davis/) was twenty-four and had just had his first child [Mary Davis](/family/homer-davis/) (b. ~1923) &mdash; Edward E. Way had lived to see his first **great-great-grandchild**. The Crooked Tree Cemetery line that ends with him on the Way side begins again two generations later with Chuck.
