Sarah Ruf was William Clifford Wildermuth's **second wife**, after his first wife [Flora Schlicher](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/) died on 18 November 1919. The GEDCOM (FS ID **G3C8-MK7**) records her **birthplace as Williamstown, Wood County, West Virginia** — directly across the Ohio River from Marietta — and gives no birth date, no death date, and no children of the marriage.

## The local-community connection

**Williamstown, West Virginia** sits on the south bank of the Ohio River **opposite Marietta**, in **Wood County**. The two towns are connected by a road bridge today (and were connected by ferry in earlier eras). A Williamstown-born woman marrying into a Marietta Wildermuth household after that household's first wife had died is a **local-community-bonds second marriage** of the kind common in early-20th-century river-town life. The Ruf surname is German, fitting the broader German-immigrant community of Washington County, Ohio and adjacent Wood County, WV.

The marriage took place at some point after Flora's November 1919 death; William Clifford was over 50 by then. He died on 10 September 1943; Sarah's outcome after his death is undocumented in this archive.
