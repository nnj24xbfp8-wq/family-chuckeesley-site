R. Albert Eesley was the second of [Albert Robert Eesley](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/)'s ten children &mdash; born of the same generation as Chuck's great-grandfather [Charles Leonard Eesley](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/), and a brother of [William Eesley](/family/william-eesley-college-corner/) (the College Corner miller), [John Franklin Eesley](/family/john-f-eesley/) (the Plainwell mill founder), and the rest of the Eesley milling generation in late-19th-century Ohio and Ontario.

He appears in two documented sources in this archive:

- **[William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/)**, which names him as **"A. R. Eesley Jr."** &mdash; one of the six Eesley brothers, all millers.
- **[His son George E. Eesley's 1942 Fremont News-Messenger obituary](/docs/george-e-eesley-obituary-1942/)**, which identifies him as **"R. Albert Eesley"** living at **1310 Hayes Avenue, Fremont, Ohio**. The convention of "R. Albert" rather than "Albert R." was the formal newspaper style of the era.

He and his wife had at least four children:

- **[George Edburn "Bus" Eesley](/family/george-e-eesley/)** (1903&ndash;1942) &mdash; Ohio Wesleyan graduate; worked in an airplane factory near Buffalo; died at 39 during a major operation; father of [Daniel Edburn "Dan" Eesley](/family/daniel-edburn-eesley/).
- **Bess Eesley** &mdash; of Fremont, Ohio; named in George's 1942 obituary as accompanying their parents to Kenmore for the funeral.
- **Mrs. Jack Cassel** &mdash; of Toronto, Canada (her given name not in the 1942 clipping).
- **Albert** &mdash; of Youngstown, Ohio.

The **Fremont, Ohio &rarr; Buffalo &rarr; Toronto &rarr; Youngstown** geographic spread of his children is consistent with a Lake Erie corridor extended family from the Albert Robert Sr generation's milling-and-emigration patterns.
