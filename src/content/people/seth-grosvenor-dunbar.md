Seth Grosvenor Dunbar was born **7 October 1836 in Kennebec County, Maine** &mdash; a long way from the Ohio-Virginia border country his daughter [Verona Belle Dunbar](/family/verona-sheppard-fleming/) would grow up in. His move from coastal Maine to the new state of West Virginia in the 1860s most likely traces to the Civil War (he was twenty-four when it started; West Virginia split off from Virginia in 1863) or to the post-war labor migration that brought New Englanders south for railroad, mining, or oil work in the years right after Appomattox.

He married [Martha Kelley](/family/martha-kelley/) (b. 1844, Barbour County, then Virginia) sometime in the 1860s. Their daughter Verona was born 11 June 1869 in Wood County. **Seth died 26 October 1870, age thirty-four**; Verona was sixteen months old; Martha was twenty-six.

His burial is in Wood County, West Virginia. His Maine-side family &mdash; the parents and earlier generations of the Dunbar line back in Kennebec County &mdash; are open research; the GEDCOM carries them as Family @F101@, which will be worked through in a later pass.
