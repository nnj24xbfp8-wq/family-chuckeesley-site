**Nose turret gunner** of Robert Earl Wildermuth's B-24, 400th Squadron, 90th Bomb Group, Pacific theater 1944–45. From **Buffalo, New York**.

The **nose turret** was at the very front of the B-24 with twin .50-caliber machine guns covering the forward arc. Per Robert Earl's [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/), Robert Earl as navigator was the man **physically closing Charles into the turret** before each combat takeoff:

> *"The nose turret gunner and the man who climbed into his turret at the nose of the aircraft and then I would close the doors behind him was Cpl. Charles Bojt of Buffalo, New York."*

That detail — the navigator (Robert Earl) sealing in the nose gunner (Charles) before every combat mission — is the kind of small physical ritual a ten-man crew built around. Robert Earl probably opened those doors back up at the end of every mission, too.

He is named in Robert Earl's [1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/) and the [combat log *The Big One*](/docs/the-big-one/). His pre-war Buffalo life and post-war return are open research.

> *Sources: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/); [The Big One](/docs/the-big-one/).*
