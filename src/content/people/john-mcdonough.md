**Original bombardier** of Robert Earl Wildermuth's B-24, 400th Squadron, 90th Bomb Group, trained with the crew at **Gowen Field, Boise, Idaho** in late 1944. From **St. Louis, Missouri**.

Before the crew shipped overseas, **F.O. McDonough was reassigned to additional bombing training at Langley Field, Virginia** — per Robert Earl's [memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/), the crew was *"informed that we would be assigned a new bombardier and that F.O. McDonough would be put in some new bombing training at Langley."* He was therefore not part of the Pacific tour itself, but is part of the documented Gowen Field crew roster.

His subsequent Air Corps assignments and post-war life are open research.

> *Sources: [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/); [The Big One](/docs/the-big-one/).*
