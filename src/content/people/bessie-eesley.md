She was the **fifth-eldest of the ten children** — born **16 September 1874 in Bloomfield Township, Oakland County, Michigan** during the family's Michigan-mills years — and she married **[George Alexander Sinclair](/family/george-alexander-sinclair/)** (b. 9 May 1874 Ontario, d. 1951 Toronto) in **Toronto in 1899**, the same year as the family group portrait.

This identification resolves a question that had been open in this archive: whether the **"Bessie"** named in the 1899 family-portrait caption and the **"Mrs. George Sinclair"** named in [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/)'s Toronto-daughters list were the same person or two separate siblings. Per Dale's tree (FamilySearch ID **MWTZ-397**) they are: Bessie *is* Mrs. Sinclair, with the **1899 marriage to George Alexander Sinclair** documented under **family record @F128@** in the GEDCOM. The earlier archive note treating Mrs. Sinclair as potentially Bessie's married name has been confirmed.

A small spelling curiosity: **the GEDCOM records her birth surname as "Easley"** — not the family's standard "Eesley." Whether this was a different transcription tradition, a clerk's spelling choice on a Michigan birth record, or an actual variant Bessie used herself is open. The variant spelling does appear elsewhere in the broader Eesley/Easley family records of the era.

## The Toronto life

She and George Sinclair were married in **Toronto in 1899**. By the time of the c. 1916 William obituary she was Mrs. George Sinclair of Toronto, one of the **three Toronto sisters** — alongside [Anna Scoon](/family/anna-eesley-scoon/) (Hannah Goldie) and [Jennie Baxter](/family/jennie-baxter-eesley/), plus their brother [Dr. George Henry](/family/george-eesley-toronto/) the physician. The same Scottish-Presbyterian middle-class Toronto culture the family had collected into by the turn of the 20th century — streetcar-belt neighborhoods, parish church and women's society, brick downtown houses — was the daily world of her married life.

She **outlived her husband by eight years**: George Sinclair died in 1951 (buried Toronto); Bessie died in **1959** at age 84. Of the ten Eesley siblings, only her brother [George Henry](/family/george-eesley-toronto/) — who lived to 1974 — outlasted her.

She appears in the **[1899 Eesley family group portrait](/archive/eesley-family-group-portrait-1899/)** as one of the standing daughters — about 24 at the time of the photograph, and married to George Sinclair the same year.
