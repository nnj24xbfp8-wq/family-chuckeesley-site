John Eesley was baptized at Hanwell, Oxfordshire, in January 1799 — the only child of [Joseph Eesley](/family/joseph-eesley/) and [Frances "Fanny" Ayris](/family/frances-eesley/) recorded there — and married **[Susannah Bubb](/family/susan-babbs/)** at Charlecote, Warwickshire, on 10 May 1819. The 1851 census placed him at Rother Street in Old Stratford, age 51, a journeyman miller, with Susannah (50, born Charlecote, working) and several of their eleven children. He died **4 February 1870**, age 71.

His occupation as a miller is not incidental: the trade ran through the line and across the Atlantic. **Albert Robert** went to **Canada** to work in the Goldie family's flour operation (the Goldies had emigrated from Ayrshire Scotland to Ontario in 1841) — what looks like an emigration to Scotland in the older brief framing is really an emigration to Canada to work in a Scottish-Canadian mill. Albert's son **John Franklin Eesley** would be born in Hamilton, Ontario in 1859 and eventually found the [Sunshine Flour Mill in Plainwell, Michigan](/places/plainwell-eesley-mill/) — so the milling thread, three generations long, runs Hanwell → Old Stratford → Ontario → Michigan.

A second John, John's own son (d. 1874), almost certainly emigrated separately — probably to New Jersey, where Mary Eesley Bean's 1985 register placed an Eesley emigrant family with a Newark bakery. The Bean account's spouse name (Hannah Bubb) is most likely a conflation of John Sr.'s wife (Susannah) and her daughter Hannah; the New Jersey emigrant was the *son* John, not the Hanwell-born father.

> Spellings vary across the records: "Eesley" in parish registers, "Easley" in some American census hands. Dale's tree standardizes to Eesley.
