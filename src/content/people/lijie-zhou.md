Lijie was born in **Qingdao** on China's eastern coast, in **Shandong** province &mdash; the home of one branch of the Zhou family this side of the archive is being built around. She and Chuck made a life together at Stanford, where they continue to live, and where this archive itself was put together.

She joined Chuck on the [2018 visit to the Oxford public library](/docs/eesley-line-research-notes/) where the volunteer genealogist worked through the Eesley line back to Joseph and Frances Ayris. The Oxfordshire half of the surviving English research record bears her presence as well as his.

Other details withheld &mdash; living. The biographical layer here is meant to grow as Lijie chooses to add to it.

## The Zhou side &mdash; in progress

This archive's English/American side &mdash; the Eesley and Wildermuth families &mdash; took a year of intensive work to bring to the present shape, drawing on Bean's 1985 register, Robert Earl's memoirs, the Oxford library notes, Aunt Maggie's photo deck, Aunt Jeanne and cousin Roberta's oral history, and Dale Eesley's 3,400-individual FamilySearch tree. The **Zhou family of Qingdao** &mdash; Lijie's parents, grandparents, and the line back from them &mdash; is held open here as the next major branch of the archive to be built.

The shape it will take:

- **Lijie's parents** as the first two `people` entries on the Zhou side, with whatever biographical detail Lijie chooses to share.
- **Lijie's grandparents** behind them, where Lijie and her family can name them.
- **A &ldquo;Zhou family of Qingdao&rdquo; document** &mdash; the place-of-origin narrative, analogous to the [Wildermuth villages of W&uuml;rttemberg](/places/rielingshausen-town-square/) and the [Eesley parishes of Oxfordshire](/family/joseph-eesley/) on the existing sides.
- **A &ldquo;Qingdao&rdquo; entry in the `places` collection** &mdash; the city and its history (the late-19th-century German concession period, the 1922 return to Chinese control, the Tsingtao brewery, the seaside) as the urban setting Lijie was born into.
- **Generation names** (字辈 / 班辈) if the Zhou family uses them &mdash; the traditional Chinese lineage device where a single character is shared by all male family members of one generation, drawn from a generation-poem (字辈诗) written by an ancestor. Where present, these encode many generations of structure into a single line.
- **Ancestral village** (祖籍) where applicable &mdash; many Qingdao families trace to elsewhere in Shandong and beyond.
