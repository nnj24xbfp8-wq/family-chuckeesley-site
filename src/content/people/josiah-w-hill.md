Josiah W. Hill (Sr.) was Chuck's **maternal great-great-great-grandfather** on the Hill line. He was the father of [Harvey Hendershot Hill](/family/harvey-hendershot-hill/) (Chuck's great-great-grandfather, the man on the burro at the fair) and of [Josiah Way Hill](/family/josiah-way-hill/) (the WWI casualty at the Meuse-Argonne).

He was born **4 November 1854 at Washington Township, Monroe County, Ohio** and married [Janetta (Smith) Hill](/family/janetta-smith/) (b. February 1864). The marriage produced at least three children in the GEDCOM record: Harvey Hendershot (b. 10 November 1882), Josiah Way (b. 29 September 1893), and one further sibling.

He died **20 March 1918, age 63** &mdash; seven months before his namesake son Josiah Way Hill was killed in action at the Meuse-Argonne (11 October 1918). The Hill family lost father and son within a single year of each other across the World War I home-front and battlefield.

Page is a stub. The "Way" in his son's middle name suggests a parallel Way-family connection on the Hill side that runs separately from the [Way line that enters the Davis side through Victoria Anna Way](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/); the specific marriage that put *"Way"* into the Hill family is open research, but the likely point of entry is Janetta's side of the family or an earlier maternal-line marriage of Josiah Sr.'s own parents.
