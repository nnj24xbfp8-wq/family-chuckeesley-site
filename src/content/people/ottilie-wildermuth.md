Ottilie Wildermuth — born Ottilie Rooschüz on 22 February 1817 in [Rottenburg am Neckar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rottenburg_am_Neckar), died 12 July 1877 in [Tübingen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%BCbingen) — was the writer whose name carried the Wildermuth surname into civic memory across 19th-century Württemberg. She was the daughter of *Gottlob Christian Rooschüz* (1785–1847), Kriminalrat and Oberamtsrichter, who served as district judge in [Marbach am Neckar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marbach_am_Neckar) — the family seat across her girlhood, where she lived from 1825 to 1839 in the house at Marktstraße 25 that had been built in 1824–25 as the district court for her father.

In 1843, at twenty-six, she married the philologist [Wilhelm David Wildermuth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildermuth-Gymnasium) (1807–1885), professor of modern languages at the Lyzeum in Tübingen — now the [Wildermuth-Gymnasium](https://www.wildermuth-gymnasium.de), the secondary school named for the family. She founded the women's salon of Tübingen and belonged to it for thirty-four years until her death. They had five children, three of whom — Agnes Willms, Adelheid Wildermuth, and Hermann Wildermuth — survived to adulthood. Her stories and children's-magazine *Jugendgarten* (founded 1870) made her one of the most widely read Württemberg authors of the period; in 1871 she received the Great Gold Medal for Art and Science from the Kingdom of Württemberg.

## Connection to Robert Earl's branch

Resolved June 2026 from Wikipedia and the German-Wikipedia "Wildermuth, Johann David" entry plus the Wildermuth-Gymnasium history sheet:

- Ottilie's husband Wilhelm David Wildermuth was born 17 February 1807 in *Pleidelsheim* — one of the three Marbach-district villages (Pleidelsheim, Rielingshausen, Grossaspach) the 1990 Heritage names as Wildermuth heartland.
- His father was *Johann Georg Wildermuth*, a farmer in Pleidelsheim.
- Robert Earl's epigraph-citation in the 1990 Heritage — *"As Dr. Hans Wildermuth, son of Johann David Wildermuth, stated: 'The Wuerttemburg Wildermuths have their origin in the villages of Pleidelsheim and Riedenshausen [Rielingshausen] near the small town of Marbach in the Northeast of Stuttgart'"* — was a citation of someone in *this* (Ottilie's) branch of the family. Dr. Hans Wildermuth was a son or grandson of Wilhelm David and Ottilie.
- The extended Rielingshausen Wildermuth clan has been documented in parish ledgers since *1521*; Pleidelsheim and Grossaspach are part of the same tight Marbach-district radius. Ottilie's husband's father (Pleidelsheim farmer) and Robert Earl's great-grandfather Johann Michael Wildermuth (b. 1830 Grossaspach) are extended cousins through that Rielingshausen root — separated by perhaps three or four generations and one or two village hops, but firmly in the same Wildermuth surname-cluster.

So Ottilie connects to the Marietta line not by descent but by marriage into a parallel branch of the same Württemberg surname-cluster, and her 19th-century literary career is the reason civic Württemberg still carries the Wildermuth name in the Tübingen *Gymnasium*, the Marbach *Wildermuthstraße*, and the *Neckarinsel* monument.

The grandson of Wilhelm David and Ottilie, *[Eberhard Wildermuth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eberhard_Wildermuth)* (1890–1952), served as the West German Federal Minister for Housing and Construction under Adenauer in 1949–1952 — the public-life continuation of the same Tübingen branch.

Chuck's 2018 photograph of the [memorial plaque on her childhood home](/places/marbach-wildermuthstrasse/) is what brought her into this archive in the first place.
