Charlie Wildermuth was one of the **nine children of [William Clifford Wildermuth](/family/william-wildermuth/) and [Flora Schlicher Wildermuth](/family/flora-schlicher-wildermuth/)** &mdash; his name appears in the May, Charlie, William, Margaret, Blanche, Emma, Earl, Pearl, George birth-order roster his brother [Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) typed out for Robert Earl in the [17 January 1972 family-history letter](/docs/letters/earl-adam-to-robert-earl-1972-01-17/).

## The Wichita migration

The biographical line that survives, in Earl Adam's own typing, is the Marietta-to-Wichita arc:

> *Bob your Uncle Charlie met his wife in Wichita Kans. She was a Marietta girl but went to Wichita with the people Charlie worked for she was their hired girl then they lived in Wichita thats where Charlie died and was burried.*

In Earl Adam's account: Charlie went west from Marietta to Wichita, Kansas, to work for a family. A Marietta-born young woman went along with that same family as their **hired girl** &mdash; the live-in-domestic-staff arrangement that was common across late-19th and early-20th-century American households. Charlie and the hired girl met in Wichita, married, and settled there. He died and is buried in Wichita.

The detail of *"the people Charlie worked for"* taking the hired girl along to a new city is the small Marietta-economy detail that anchors when the move happened: somewhere in the 1890s or 1900s, when the practice of bringing a family's domestic help with them on a relocation was still routine. Charlie's exact birth year, marriage date, wife's name, and death date are open research.

He is one of the small Wildermuth-side west-migration cases in the archive &mdash; alongside his younger brother Edward Schlicher's Oklahoma-oil-fields move (on the Schlicher side), a small documented record of Marietta-born young people leaving the Ohio River valley for the open trans-Mississippi.

> *Source: [Earl Adam Wildermuth's 17 January 1972 letter to Robert Earl Wildermuth](/docs/letters/earl-adam-to-robert-earl-1972-01-17/), p. 2.*
