"Alf" was the third son of [Albert Robert Eesley Sr](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/) to carry the **Albert Robert** name. The family resolved the resulting three-Albert-Roberts problem with everyday nicknames:

- The father was simply **Albert Robert Sr** (or **A. R. Sr**).
- The son who became the Fremont, Ohio miller was **"Bert"** &mdash; [R. Albert Eesley Jr.](/family/albert-robert-eesley-jr/), grandfather of [Daniel Edburn Eesley](/family/daniel-edburn-eesley/) the San Francisco engineer.
- This third son was **"Alf"** or **"Alfred"** &mdash; the same Albert Robert name, the everyday family alternative.

The 1899 portrait places him **seated to the right of his father** in the family group &mdash; the **second** of the two same-named sons, while Bert stood in the back row.

He does **not** appear in [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/) by either name "Alfred" or "Alf." The obituary's roster lists the six miller-brothers as *"John R., William, Robert, A. R., C. L., and Garfield."* The most parsimonious reading is that **the obituary's "Robert" is Alf** &mdash; the obituary's compiler used "Robert" as the third name, distinct from "A. R." (Bert), but Alf was the more familiar family form.

The other possible reading is that **Alf died between 1899 and 1916** and was omitted from the obituary's surviving-children list. The "Eesleys circa 1900" PowerPoint deck explicitly identifies him in the 1899 photograph; this confirms his presence at the family's 1899 gathering.

He is **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the paternal side either way.
