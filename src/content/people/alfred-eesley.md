The earlier framing on this page had Alf as the **third Albert Robert** in the family — based on the *"Eesleys circa 1900"* PowerPoint caption that read *"Alf (Albert Robert)"*. The GEDCOM resolves it differently: his given name in Dale Eesley's FamilySearch tree (ID **LZTC-RC2**) is **Alfred Robert** Eesley. The family caption had compressed *Alfred → Alf → Albert* through ordinary handwriting and memory, not from a record. There were **two** Albert Roberts in this generation, not three — Sr the father and **Bert** (born **Robert Albert**) the son. Alf was a third son with a similar-sounding but distinct first name.

## The life on the page

He was born in **July 1867 in Michigan** — the family's Michigan years, between the Ontario start and the eventual Toronto retirement of his parents. He was the **fourth-eldest** of the ten children (after [John Franklin](/family/john-f-eesley/), [Hannah Goldie 'Anna'](/family/anna-eesley-scoon/), and [William](/family/william-eesley-college-corner/)). He outlived almost all of his siblings, dying in **1954** at age 87 and being buried at **Pleasant Cemetery, Mount Sterling, Madison County, Ohio** — meaning he ended his life in central Ohio rather than the Toronto where most of his sisters and his physician brother settled.

He appears in the **[1899 Eesley family group portrait](/archive/eesley-family-group-portrait-1899/)** as one of the seated row. The portrait's "Eesleys circa 1900" caption identifies him as *"Alf (Albert Robert)"* — the transcription error this archive has now corrected against the structured record.

He is **not named individually** in [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/)'s list of six miller-brothers — which gives them as *"John R., William, Robert, A. R., C. L., and Garfield."* The most parsimonious reading is that the **"Robert" in that list is Alf** — the obituary's compiler used "Robert" as a third name distinct from "A. R." (Bert's newspaper convention).

He is **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the paternal side.
