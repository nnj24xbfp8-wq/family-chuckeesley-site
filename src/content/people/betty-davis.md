Betty Davis was the **youngest of the three Davis sisters** — younger sister of [Dorothy Marie Davis Wildermuth](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) and **[Mary Davis](/family/mary-davis/)**. Daughter of [Homer Edward Davis](/family/homer-davis/) and [Bessie Marie (Hill) Davis](/family/bessie-hill-davis/), born in Ohio around **1926**, the year after Dorothy.

The three Davis sisters appear together at ages 5/4/3 and 6/5/4 in two photographs reproduced in [Robert Earl Wildermuth's 1990 Wildermuth/Fleming Heritage](/docs/wildermuth-fleming-heritage-1990/) — Betty on the right in both frames, the smallest of the three.

She lived through the **Unionville, Ohio house fire** with Dorothy and Mary; the sisters were given a small shed that escaped the fire while their parents took the one-room garage through the cold winter that followed.
