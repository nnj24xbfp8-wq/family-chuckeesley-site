Anne Eesley was born **1 March 1946 in Columbus, Ohio** — the second of Will and Peggy Eesley's four children, fourteen months after her eldest sister Jeanne, eleven months before her brother Charlie, and roughly five years before her youngest sister Maggie. She never married and kept the Eesley surname all her life. She died **27 July 2019 at Country Care in Farwell, Michigan**, age 73.

## A scholar of Spanish

Anne's obituary gives the academic chain that the prior thin geographic listings did not surface:

- **Marietta High School** — graduated 1964
- **Marietta College** — Bachelor of Arts, 1968
- **University of Wisconsin** — Master of Arts in Spanish, 1976
- **State University of New York** — Doctor of Philosophy, 1983

The MA-in-Spanish at Wisconsin in 1976 sits exactly after her Madrid years (early-to-mid 1970s), confirming that the [Madrid residence Peggy visited her in](/archive/anne-and-peggy-eesley-europe-c-1973/) was pre-graduate Spanish-immersion work that fed her later academic career. The SUNY PhD followed seven years later. Anne was a serious scholar of Spanish language and literature whose academic trajectory ran from Marietta High through Madrid through Wisconsin through New York into a teaching career across three universities.

## The teaching career

Anne taught as a professor at three universities:

- **Hillsdale College** — small private liberal arts college in southern Michigan
- **The Citadel** — Charleston, South Carolina (military college). The Charleston, SC address that appears in Will Eesley's 1986 obituary corresponds to her Citadel years.
- **Grand Valley State University** — Allendale, Michigan, near Muskegon. The Muskegon, MI address that appears in Charlie Eesley's 2015 obituary corresponds to her Grand Valley State years.

Her obituary also names two non-university teaching settings: **a migrant program** and the **Muskegon Correctional Facility**. The four populations she taught — Citadel cadets, Grand Valley State undergraduates, migrant-worker families, incarcerated men in a Michigan prison — span as wide a range of American student audiences as a single career can. Her obituary's central characterological line: *"Anne had a passion for teaching and the way education could help people reach their full potential."*

## A chosen-family network in Michigan

Anne never married. The obituary names two Michigan-area family-friend households she *"cherished like family"* and who survived her: **Aaron and Dina Wetters with their children Micah and Hannah**, and **Lydia (Ian) Kirkpatrick**. For an unmarried Eesley daughter ending her life in Farwell, Michigan — with one brother gone (Charlie d. 2015), her parents both gone (Will d. 1986, Peggy d. 2007), and her two surviving sisters Jeanne and Maggie living far away — the chosen-family households were her household at the end.

The obituary closes on her cooking, her hospitality, and her embroidery: *"Anne enjoyed cooking and was known for her hospitality, hosting many meals in her home and putting out her best dishes for each guest who sat at her table. Anne was skilled at embroidery and designed her own patterns to create personal gifts."* The same hospitality her great-aunt [Betty Wolfe's obituary](/docs/betty-jean-wolfe-obituary-2015/) frames on the other side of the family — two women across two generations running their late-life households on meal-making and welcoming.

## In the family photographs

Three photographs in this archive frame Anne across her life:

### Three Eesley children, c. 1948–1950 — Jeanne, Anne, infant Charlie

[A black-and-white frame of the three eldest Eesley children](/archive/three-eesley-children-c-1948/) seated in a row on a patterned rug in formal velvet jumpers and white blouses. Jeanne at right (age four or five), Anne at left (age two or three), and infant Charlie in a high chair in the middle, laughing with his mouth open. The frame predates Maggie's 1951 birth. Anne and Charlie were both born at the same Columbus hospital fourteen months apart; the photograph catches them as a family of three children before they were a family of four.

### Peggy visiting Anne in Madrid, c. 1972–1975

[A color frame of Anne and Peggy on the steps of a grand classical Madrid public building](/archive/anne-and-peggy-eesley-europe-c-1973/) — both in matching red trench coats, Peggy in white hair with glasses and dark purse, Anne in auburn hair with brown shoulder bag. Anne was living in Madrid for the Spanish-immersion residence that became the basis for her 1976 Wisconsin MA in Spanish; Peggy travelled out to spend time with her. The Madrid chapter is the cosmopolitan period her later Charleston-Muskegon-Farwell geography did not advertise but that her academic record requires.

### Extended family gathering, c. 1985–86

[A color frame of the extended Eesley family outdoors](/archive/eesley-family-gathering-c-1985-1986/), with Will and Peggy together at the back, Charlie and Terrie present, and a young Chuck about five or six in front. Anne stands in the middle row in a pale blue dress — home from Charleston where she was then teaching at The Citadel. The frame likely captures the last summer with Will alive before his June 1986 death.
