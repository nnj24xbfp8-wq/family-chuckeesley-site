George Eesley was the **physician** of [Albert Robert Eesley Sr](/family/albert-robert-eesley/) and [Jennie Goldie](/family/jeanie-goldie/)'s ten children — the only one of the six Eesley brothers who did not follow their father into the milling trade. He practiced medicine in **Toronto, Canada**, where the family had settled by the time of [William Eesley's c. 1916 obituary](/docs/william-eesley-obituary-college-corner/), which describes him as *"Dr. George, a physician of Toronto."*

That **one Eesley brother in this generation went into medicine** while six went into milling is itself the trade-pattern this family carried across an Atlantic move and a half-continent migration — the family stayed in the practical crafts almost entirely, with George the single professional outlier.

He is identified in the **1899 Eesley family group portrait** (caption: *"George"*) alongside his nine siblings and their parents.

He is **Chuck's great-great-uncle** on the paternal side.
