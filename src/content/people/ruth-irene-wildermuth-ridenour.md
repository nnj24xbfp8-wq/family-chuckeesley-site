**Ruth Irene Wildermuth Ridenour** was the **eldest** of [Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) and [Sadye Irene Fleming Wildermuth](/family/sadye-fleming-wildermuth/)'s five children &mdash; the **older sister of [Robert Earl Wildermuth](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/)** (Chuck's grandfather). The other three Wildermuth siblings were **Carl Edward Wildermuth** (later blind), **Norma Jean Wildermuth Gault**, and **Betty Joan Wildermuth Haddox**.

## The Tremont Hospital birth

In her own words, from the parallel autobiographical sketch she wrote and appended to Robert Earl's 1989 memoir:

> *"It was on a beautiful spring day, 29 April 1922, that I first saw the light as a member of the brand new Earl Adam and Sadye Irene (Fleming) Wildermuth family. At precisely 8 [AM] I came into this world at the Tremont Hospital, Sixth and Montgomery Streets, Marietta..."*

The **Tremont Hospital at Sixth and Montgomery Streets in Marietta** &mdash; new to this archive's geography &mdash; was a small downtown Marietta hospital in the early twentieth century. Ruth's was the first birth in Earl and Sadye's marriage; Robert Earl would arrive **almost exactly two years later** in 1924.

She lived through her parents' apprenticeship-and-machinist years and the move to **[413 Harmar Street](/places/413-harmar-street-marietta/)** in 1929, alongside her younger brother Robert Earl and the three younger siblings who followed.

## The Marietta Telephone Exchange and the war marriage

Ruth worked at the **Marietta Telephone Exchange** through the war years &mdash; the early 1940s, the period when so many young American women filled the local-exchange-operator workforce during World War II.

Her **aunt Laverna (Fleming) Raison** &mdash; her mother Sadye's sister &mdash; played cupid and introduced Ruth to **[Jack Ridenour](/family/jack-ridenour/)**. They married **6 May 1943 at Camp Kilmer, New Jersey** &mdash; the Army's main East-Coast staging area for the European and CBI theaters &mdash; **a week before Jack shipped out to the China-India-Burma Theater** of World War II. Ruth went back to Marietta and lived as a *"warbride 'widow'"* until Jack returned at the end of the war. The marriage opened with the same kind of separation that her cousin-by-marriage [Betty Davis](/family/betty-davis/)'s 1945 wedding to Norm Wolfe would &mdash; two years and a generational beat later.

## The next generation

Ruth and Jack had two daughters:

- **Karen Kay Ridenour**, born **8 January 1949** &mdash; **the same week as her first cousin [Terrie Lee Wildermuth](/family/terrie-lee-eesley/)** (Robert Earl and Dottie's eldest, b. 13 January 1949 in Oakland, CA). Karen Kay and Terrie were therefore born **within five days of each other** on opposite sides of the country &mdash; the two Wildermuth-side cousins of the same generation.
- **Marcia Ann Ridenour**, born **7 April 1951**.

## What Ruth's sketch records

Ruth's parallel sketch appended to Robert Earl's 1989 memoir records the family's life events from her angle:

- **Sadye Irene (Fleming) Wildermuth** died **4 August 1976** of **multiple myeloma**.
- **Earl Adam Wildermuth** suffered a massive stroke and died **8 December 1977** &mdash; *"grieving for his dear wife was a major contributing factor."*
- **Carl** (her brother) was blind and lived with their father after Sadye's death.
- **Jack Ridenour** (her husband) later died of **the same multiple myeloma their mother had had** &mdash; an unusual cluster of the same blood cancer in two close family members of the same generation.
