Tacy Elizabeth Matthews is Chuck's **maternal three-times-great-grandmother** through the Davis-Way line. She is the figure labeled **#7 "Tacy Matthews (great-grandmother)"** in the family identification legend for the [Belle Way and Howard Wickens 1901 wedding portrait](/family/belsora-way-wickens/).

She was born **4 November 1848 in Washington County, Ohio**. On **11 April 1872 at Noble County, Ohio**, age 23, she married **[Edward E. Way](/family/edward-e-way/)** (1851-1924) &mdash; the English-immigrant Way family's second-generation Ohio son. She was three years older than Edward. The Matthews and Way families both had their lives anchored in southeastern Ohio across the late nineteenth century.

## The ten children

The marriage produced ten Way children in the GEDCOM record. The eldest, [Victoria Anna Way](/family/victoria-anna-way-davis/) (b. 27 January 1874), is Chuck's direct ancestor on the Davis line through her son [Homer Edward Davis](/family/homer-davis/). Tacy lived to see Victoria marry William Armstrong Davis, to see grandson Homer born (8 February 1900), and to attend her daughter [Belle's wedding](/family/belsora-way-wickens/) to [Howard Wickens](/family/howard-edward-wickens/) on **25 September 1901**.

In the wedding portrait that survives in the family papers, Tacy stands at upper-center with the standing adults &mdash; figure #7, labeled in the family's identification legend as *"Tacy Matthews (great-grandmother)"* from [Dorothy Davis Wildermuth's](/family/dorothy-davis-wildermuth/) perspective. The photograph is **one of the last documented images of her**.

## Death four months after the wedding

Tacy died **29 January 1902 at Noble County, Ohio**, age 53 &mdash; four months and four days after her daughter Belle's 25 September 1901 wedding. She was buried at **Crooked Tree Cemetery, Noble County, Ohio**, beside her parents-in-law and the Way family plot where her own daughters Victoria (d. 1903), Belle (d. 1912), Emma (d. 1917) and Nora Angie (d. 1939) would later be buried.

Her husband Edward E. Way survived her by **twenty-two years**, dying in November 1924.
