Tommy Eesley is the eldest son of **[Leonard David Eesley](/family/leonard-david-eesley/)** (1904&ndash;1976), the eldest of Charles Leonard and Lillie Dale's ten children. He appears in two photographs in this archive across about five years of his childhood:

- **Around 1933** &mdash; as the small boy with his uncle Will at Black Lake, Michigan (Maggie Eesley's deck, slide 4). He was around five years old by the date estimate.
- **Around 1937–1939** &mdash; as the young boy in shorts and a light sweater standing at his grandfather [Charles Leonard's](/family/charles-leonard-eesley/) right hand, in the [group portrait with five of his uncles](/archive/charles-leonard-and-sons-late-1930s/). He would have been around nine to twelve.

The photographs are five or six years apart and both feature Tommy as the lone grandchild present &mdash; the eldest of the next generation, the only one yet born in the late 1930s window when both frames were made.

This page is a stub. Tommy's full birth and death dates, marriage, and descendants are pending family memory or the GEDCOM cross-reference.
