**Carl Edward Wildermuth** was the **third** of [Earl Adam Wildermuth](/family/earl-a-wildermuth/) and [Sadye Irene (Fleming) Wildermuth](/family/sadye-fleming-wildermuth/)'s five children &mdash; born **5 July 1928 in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio**, six and a half years after his eldest sister [Ruth Irene](/family/ruth-irene-wildermuth-ridenour/) and four years after his older brother [Robert Earl](/family/robert-earl-wildermuth/) (Chuck's grandfather).

## A blind son

Per his sister Ruth's parallel autobiographical sketch in [Robert Earl's 1989 memoir](/docs/robert-earl-wildermuth-memoir/): **Carl was blind**. Ruth records this in the brief family-summary section without elaboration; the cause &mdash; congenital or acquired through accident or illness &mdash; is not specified in the family record. Carl's blindness shaped the family arc through his adult life.

After his mother Sadye died of multiple myeloma on **4 August 1976**, **Carl lived with his father Earl Adam through Earl's remaining years**. Earl Adam suffered a massive stroke on **8 December 1977**, with **grieving over Sadye's death named in the family record as a major contributing factor** to the stroke, and died.

## Carl's later life and death

Carl outlived his father by **twelve years**. He died in **Newark, Licking County, Ohio on 9 December 1989**, age 61. He is buried at **East Lawn Memorial Park in Reno, Washington County, Ohio** &mdash; the same Marietta-area cemetery where his father Earl Adam is buried, alongside multiple generations of Wildermuth and Davis family.

His life after his father's 1977 death is open research &mdash; including who cared for him, where he lived, and how he died. The Newark death location places him in the same county as the Wildermuth-Marietta-side relatives' Licking County connections.
